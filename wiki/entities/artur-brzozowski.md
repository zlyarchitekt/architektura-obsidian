## Overview

Artur Brzozowski is a Polish spatial researcher and writer. His essay "Funeralne Planowanie" (Funerary Planning), published in RZUT-33 (2022), applies spatial planning analysis to the accessibility of communal (non-denominational) cemeteries in Poland. Using Podlaskie voivodeship as a detailed case study, he exposes a structural injustice in Polish funerary infrastructure: elderly women — statistically the primary cemetery visitors and the most transit-dependent demographic — are disproportionately excluded from accessing their right to mourn by planning legislation designed in 1959 and never meaningfully reformed.

## Key Works / Projects

- **"Funeralne Planowanie"** (2022) — essay in RZUT-33 on cemetery accessibility in Poland; quantitative analysis of communal cemetery distribution by voivodeship; Podlaskie case study; statistical analysis of gender and ageing patterns in cemetery use.

## Ideas & Influences

Brzozowski's essay is an example of *spatial justice* analysis applied to infrastructure that is conventionally treated as purely technical or religious. His central insight is that cemeteries are public infrastructure with accessibility requirements — failure to meet those requirements is a planning failure with gendered, class-based, and age-based consequences. He works primarily from official statistics (GUS, NIK 2016 audit report) rather than ethnographic observation.

**PL:** Esej Brzozowskiego to przykład analizy *sprawiedliwości przestrzennej* zastosowanej do infrastruktury konwencjonalnie traktowanej jako czysto techniczna lub religijna. Centralna teza: cmentarze to infrastruktura publiczna z wymogami dostępności — brak ich spełnienia to błąd planistyczny o konsekwencjach płciowych, klasowych i wiekowych.
