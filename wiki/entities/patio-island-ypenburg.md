## Overview

Patio Island is an experimental construction of patio houses in rows, situated among Dutch channels near MVRDV's Hageneiland development. Four residential units make up twelve building strips and a line of courtyards between them. The entire southeast façade overlooking the courtyard is glazed, while the northwest façade serving as a border wall is solid. Vast glass surfaces meld interior and garden into one, directing southeastern light into the garden like a mirror. Parking is provided along the road; passage beneath the wall is pedestrian-only. Smaller masses with half-saddle roofs on the ground floor strengthen interior orientation. Playful shade roofs extend behind one another, benefitting upper-storey views. Black slate cladding melts into itself, emphasising inward-turned openings.
