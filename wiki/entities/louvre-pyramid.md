## Overview

The Louvre Pyramid is a 21-metre-tall glass and steel pyramid designed by [[I.M. Pei]] as the main entrance to the Musée du Louvre in Paris, opened 1989. It is the centrepiece of the Cour Napoléon — the courtyard previously used as a car park — which was redesigned as an underground reception hall (the Napoleon Hall) with the pyramid serving simultaneously as entrance pavilion and skylight. Part of François Mitterrand's Grands Projets program (announced 1981), the pyramid is a precursor to the starchitect-as-presidential-commission model and an early instance of public outrage → 1:1 mockup → triumphant opening.

**PL:** Piramida Luwru to 21-metrowa szklana i stalowa piramida zaprojektowana przez [[I.M. Pei]] jako główne wejście do Musée du Louvre w Paryżu, otwarta w 1989 roku. Centrepiece Cour Napoléon — dziedzińca używanego wcześniej jako parking — który został przekształcony w podziemną halę recepcyjną (Sala Napoleona) z piramidą pełniącą jednocześnie funkcję pawilonu wejściowego i świetlika. Część programu Grands Projets François Mitterranda (ogłoszonego 1981).

## Design

The pyramid is composed of 603 rhombus-shaped glass segments and 70 triangular segments — 673 in total. Its geometry is a right square pyramid. The glass is ultra-clear (extra-white), not the standard float glass with its characteristic greenish tint; achieving this required direct presidential intervention in the glass supply chain (Saint-Gobain).

The pyramid marks the entrance to the Napoleon Hall below, which connects the three wings of the Louvre — Richelieu, Sully, and Denon — for the first time in the museum's history, rationalising visitor circulation through what had been a fragmented three-entry system. The underground intervention is as significant as the visible pyramid above.

Pei chose the pyramidal form as "the most rational" — a shape that admits maximum light for minimum material and structural interference, and that does not visually compete with the historic Louvre facades. This explanation was not accepted by the initial public, who saw the Egyptian symbolism as alien imposition.

**PL:** Piramida składa się z 673 segmentów szklanych. Geometria: prawa piramida kwadratowa. Szkło ultra-przejrzyste (extra-white), nie standardowe zielonkawe float-glass; uzyskanie wymagało prezydenckiej interwencji w łańcuch dostaw (Saint-Gobain). Piramida oznacza wejście do podziemnej Sali Napoleona, która po raz pierwszy łączy trzy skrzydła Luwru. Forma piramidalna jako „najbardziej racjonalna" — maksimum światła przy minimalnym materiale i konstrukcji, bez wizualnej konkurencji z historycznymi fasadami.

## Context and Controversy

Pei was commissioned directly by President Mitterrand in 1983, without an architectural competition — as part of the Grands Projets, a series of major cultural investments timed for the bicentenary of the French Revolution (1989). The project was not disclosed publicly until February 1984, when Pei had already worked on it in secrecy for months.

Public outcry was immediate and intense. The major Parisian dailies opposed it; critics called Mitterrand "Mitterramzes" — a socialist statist building megalomaniacal monuments at French expense, and entrusting them to a foreigner. The chief objections: the glass intruder violated the dignity of the historic palace; the scale was inappropriate; the Egyptian symbolism was arbitrary.

The Louvre and government responded with a 1:1 mockup: a steel-and-cable framework erected in the Cour Napoléon, full size, so Parisians could judge for themselves whether the pyramid would dominate the courtyard. It did not; the public began to reconsider.

The Ministry of Finance, which occupied the Richelieu wing, delayed its planned relocation — at one point threatening to leave the museum without its north wing. Presidential intervention was required. A second presidential intervention secured the ultra-clear glass supply.

Opening in 1989: unanimous critical acclaim. The Louvre Pyramid became, almost immediately, one of the most photographed architectural works in the world.

**PL:** Pei zlecony bezpośrednio przez Mitterranda w 1983 bez konkursu — w ramach Grands Projets. Ogłoszony publicznie w lutym 1984: natychmiastowe oburzenie. Krytyka: szklany intruz narusza godność historycznego pałacu; skala nieodpowiednia; symbolika egipska arbitralna. „Mitterramzès." Makieta 1:1 z kabli i stali na dziedzińcu. Ministerstwo Finansów opóźniało wyprowadzkę. Prezydencka interwencja w dostawy szkła. Otwarcie 1989: jednomyślny aplauz.
