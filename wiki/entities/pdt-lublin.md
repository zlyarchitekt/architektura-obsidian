## Overview
A modernist department store in the center of Lublin, designed by [[Tadeusz Witkowski]] (1959–1964). Highly regarded for its elegant insertion into the historic urban fabric of the Śródmieście district and its original glass-block (luksfery) facades.

**PL:** *Modernistyczny dom towarowy w centrum Lublina, zaprojektowany przez Tadeusza Witkowskiego (1959–1964). Wysoko ceniony za eleganckie wpisanie w historyczną tkankę Śródmieścia oraz oryginalne elewacje z luksferów.*

## Ideas & Influences
A textbook example of how post-thaw modernists approached large-scale commercial structures within dense historic centers.

## In the Sources
- Highlighted in [[rzut-powojenny-modernizm]] as a tragic example of destructive building regulation compliance. Due to the lack of formal entry in the Register of Monuments, its iconic southern glass-block staircase wall was torn out and replaced with standard EI 30 fire-rated windows to meet modern smoke-extraction requirements, destroying its original stained-glass lighting effect. 

## Contradictions / Open Questions
The original design used glass blocks precisely because they were considered "non-flammable" under 1950s fire codes. The contemporary requirement to replace one fire-resistant material with a certified modern fire-window destroyed the building's aesthetic integrity in the name of the very safety the original architect sought to provide.
