## Overview

Jerzy Łątka is a Polish scientist and lecturer at the Faculty of Architecture, Wrocław University of Science and Technology (Politechnika Wrocławska). For 16 years he has led the student research circle *Humanizacja Środowiska Miejskiego* (Humanisation of the Urban Environment) and organises the ProtoLAB workshops, in which students design and build small-scale architectural objects. His pedagogical approach is defined by interdisciplinary collaboration, explicit teamwork structures, and direct social engagement.

Łątka’s own student years were spent "not very diligently" on official coursework; instead he invested energy in student government, personal development, foreign travel, and non-architectural jobs. His turning point came in the third year when he founded a research circle with Professor Zbigniew Bać, later becoming Bać’s doctoral student. This experience transformed him from an average student into one of the department’s strongest PhD candidates.

## Key Works / Projects

- **ProtoLAB workshops** (ongoing) — design-build courses where students construct their own designs at 1:1 scale; role-based team structure (structure, materials, promotion, budget).
- **PWr Pavilion** — collaborative build involving 50 students from four research circles (architecture, structural engineering, electronics), demonstrating cross-disciplinary negotiation at scale.
- **Wrocław homeless shelter concepts** — studio project developed with the Wrocław Municipal Social Welfare Department (MOPS), giving students direct contact with social-service clients and bureaucratic processes.
- **Lviv housing for internally displaced persons** — ongoing studio collaboration with Lviv City Council, designing a residential estate partially allocated to refugees.
- **archi-tektura.eu** — Founder of the research platform for paper architecture.

## Ideas & Influences

Łątka’s intellectual lineage runs through four mentors: (1) **Professor Zbigniew Bać** — introduced the concept of *habitat* as broad, multidimensional, interdisciplinary design of the housing environment; (2) **Zeev Baran** (Israel) — foldable emergency housing for pilgrims/disaster relief, teaching the value of rapid-deployment construction; (3) **Shigeru Ban** (Japan) — Paper Nursery School project, demonstrating that student engagement in the build process is pedagogically transformative; (4) **Marcel Bilow** (TU Delft) — teaching methodology and the Bucky Lab model of hands-on technical education.

His pedagogical formula rests on three principles: (1) **teamwork as default** — architecture is never a solo act; (2) **safe creativity** — students must feel safe to propose naive ideas, which the tutor then patiently develops; (3) **real problems** — socially engaged briefs (homelessness, displacement) produce intrinsic motivation superior to grades.

Formuła pedagogiczna opiera się na trzech zasadach: (1) **praca zespołowa jako domyślna** — architektura nigdy nie jest działaniem solowym; (2) **bezpieczna kreatywność** — studenci muszą czuć się bezpiecznie, by proponować naiwne pomysły, które tutor potem cierpliwie rozwija; (3) **realne problemy** — społecznie zaangażowane briefy (bezdomność, przesiedlenia) produkują motywację wewnętrzną przewyższającą oceny.
