## Overview
A leading Polish architect of the post-war generation, strongly associated with the post-thaw modernism (*modernizm poodwilżowy*) built in Lublin during the 1960s.

**PL:** *Jeden z czołowych polskich architektów pokolenia powojennego, silnie związany z modernizmem poodwilżowym realizowanym w Lublinie w latach 60.*

## Key Works / Projects
- **[[PDT Lublin]]** (1959–1964) — Powszechny Dom Towarowy, known for its elegant integration into the historic urban fabric and its glass-block facade (destroyed during a modern fire-safety overhaul).
- **[[Biblioteka UMCS]]** (1962–1967) — The main library of the Maria Curie-Skłodowska University, famous for its structural facade resembling a bookshelf.

## Ideas & Influences
His work demonstrates the rationalism, tectonic depth, and bespoke material detailing (subtle plaster contrasts, glass blocks) characteristic of Polish 1960s architecture. 

## In the Sources
- Featured prominently in [[rzut-powojenny-modernizm]] as the original designer of two of Lublin's most important modernist structures, both of which suffer from the conflict with contemporary building codes.
