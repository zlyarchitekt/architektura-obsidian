## Overview

Karolina Dobrzyńska-Szefer is a Polish interior architect and teacher working in vocational secondary education in Poznań. She leads design studios and workshops at Zespół Szkół Budownictwa nr 1 (architecture and interior-design classes) and Zespół Szkół Zawodowych nr 6 im. Joachima Lelewela (technical drawing and 3D design for polygraphy and photography students). Her pedagogical practice is built on the principle that manual, embodied making must precede digital work: students build cardboard models, recycled-material lamps, and large-format maquettes before opening CAD software.

Dobrzyńska-Szefer was instrumental in introducing a technician qualification in interior design at her school — one of the first such programmes in Poland. She also runs annual courses for adults and actively uses national design competitions as teaching tools. Her philosophy is strongly influenced by Japanese designer Kenya Hara’s ideas of emptiness, minimalism, and design-as-storytelling, as well as by her daughter Greta, a DGNB-certified expert in ecological construction.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Interior-design technician programme, ZSB nr 1 Poznań** (introduced 2023/2024) — one of the first Polish secondary-school programmes preparing students for the interior-design profession; high student and parent demand.
- **Autonomous curricula in spatial design** — self-developed programmes approved by school directors and curators, spanning architecture, interior arrangement, technical drawing, and 3D visualisation.
- **Competition-based pedagogy** — systematic use of national competitions (e.g., School of Form, *Kształtowanie przestrzeni*) to motivate students and connect classroom work to real-world spatial problems.
- **Annual courses for adults** — evening/year-long design courses using the same manual-first methodology.

## Ideas & Influences

Dobrzyńska-Szefer’s pedagogy rests on four pillars: (1) **manual-before-digital** — conceptual thinking is learnt through hand sketching and physical model-making, which makes subsequent computer work more intentional; (2) **empathy as method** — she uses personal stories, honesty, and the motto "Wszystko możesz!" to build student confidence; (3) **competition discipline** — deadlines, material constraints, and real briefs teach professionalism better than open-ended class exercises; (4) **aesthetic restraint** — she teaches monochromatic material palettes and the "less is more" principle, explicitly rejecting decorative excess in student maquettes.

Kenya Hara’s philosophy provides the ethical-aesthetic horizon: design is storytelling, emptiness is a positive value, and high quality does not require a brand. Her daughter’s DGNB practice introduces sustainability as a social as well as environmental criterion.

**PL:** Pedagogika Dobrzyńskiej-Szefer opiera się na czterech filarach: (1) **najpierw ręce, potem ekran** — myślenie koncepcyjne uczy się przez szkic i makietę, co usensownia późniejszą pracę komputerową; (2) **empatia jako metoda** — wykorzystuje osobiste historie, szczerość i motto „Wszystko możesz!"; (3) **dyscyplina konkursowa** — deadliny, ograniczenia materiałowe i realne briefy uczą profesjonalizmu lepiej niż otwarte zadania; (4) **wstrzemięźliwość estetyczna** — monochromatyczne palety i „mniej znaczy więcej", odrzucenie dekoracyjnego nadmiaru.

Filozofia Kenyi Hary daje horyzont etyczno-estetyczny: design to opowieść, pustka to pozytywna wartość, wysoka jakość nie wymaga marki. Praktyka DGNB córki wprowadza zrównoważoność jako kryterium społeczne i środowiskowe.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The manual-before-digital hierarchy is asserted as self-evident but not empirically defended. In an era of AI-generated design, this sequencing may need revision.
- The reliance on school directors as "managers" who approve autonomous programmes creates a gatekeeper dynamic; not all teachers may enjoy the same entrepreneurial freedom.
- DGNB is referenced only through family connection; the wiki lacks a dedicated DGNB or ecological-certification concept page.
