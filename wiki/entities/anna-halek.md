## Overview

Anna Halek is a Polish architectural researcher and writer whose essay "Kraj Żelbetowych Grzybów" (Country of Reinforced Concrete Mushrooms), published in RZUT-33 (2022), provides the most substantial Polish-language analysis of Albania's Projekti Bunkerizimit — the Cold War bunkerization programme that distributed between 100,000 and 750,000 reinforced concrete shelters across the Albanian landscape. Her approach combines architectural history, political analysis, and spatial theory (Foucault's heterotopia, Turner's liminality).

**PL:** Anna Halek to polska badaczka architektury, autorka eseju "Kraj Żelbetowych Grzybów" (RZUT-33, 2022), stanowiącego najobszerniejszą polskojęzyczną analizę albańskiego Projekti Bunkerizimit. Jej podejście łączy historię architektury, analizę polityczną i teorię przestrzeni.

## Key Works / Projects

- **"Kraj Żelbetowych Grzybów"** (2022) — essay on Albania's bunkerization programme for RZUT quarterly; draws on Gjorgi Mydyti and Elian Stefa's *Concrete Mushrooms* (Barcelona, 2012) as the primary empirical source.

## Ideas & Influences

Halek reads architecture as a political instrument rather than a technical object: the Albanian bunkers worked as materialized state ideology — fear made concrete — rather than as functional defence. She applies Foucault's *heterotopia* (a space holding contradictory social roles: shelter/prison, protection/threat, monument/ruin) and Turner's *liminality* (the bunkers' failure to fulfil their designed function permanently suspends them "between positions") to explain the bunkers' strange cultural persistence after the fall of communism.

Stosuje Foucaultowską heterotopię i Turnerowską liminalność do wyjaśnienia kulturowej trwałości bunkrów po upadku komunizmu.
