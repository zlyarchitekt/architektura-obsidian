## Overview

Letchworth Garden City is the world's first garden city, realised from 1903 onwards according to the principles laid out by Ebenezer Howard in *Garden Cities of To-morrow* (1902). Designed by Raymond Unwin and Barry Parker, the settlement covers 2,300–3,400 acres with efficient railway connections and economically developable public utilities. The plan features radial streets leading to a main square, with a grid of streets adapted to natural site features. Strict regulations governed the ratio of development, garden designs, room dimensions, and building exteriors. By 1913, Letchworth housed 9,000 residents and over 30 industries, demonstrating that a garden city could be economically viable as well as socially harmonious. The architectural character blends English vernacular traditions with modern civic-design principles.

## Key Data

- **Area:** 2,300–3,400 acres (approx. 930–1,376 ha)
- **Population:** 9,000 by 1913
- **Industries:** 30+ by 1913
- **Architects:** Raymond Unwin, Barry Parker
- **Founder:** Ebenezer Howard / Garden City Pioneers Company
- **Planning:** Radial streets to main square; grid adapted to topography; strict building regulations

## Ideas & Influences

- **Vernacular modernism**: The cottages and terraces draw on English rural building traditions but are organised by modern regulatory planning.
- **Integrated economy**: Housing and industry were planned together, with the expectation that residents could live and work within the garden city.
- **Regulatory framework**: Among the earliest examples of comprehensive planning by code rather than by single-author design.

**PL:**
- **Wernakularny modernizm**: Chaty i szeregi czerpią z angielskich wiejskich tradycji budowlanych, ale są zorganizowane przez nowoczesne planowanie regulacyjne.
- **Zintegrowana gospodarka**: Mieszkalnictwo i przemysł były planowane razem, z założeniem, że mieszkańcy mogą mieszkać i pracować w granicach miasta-ogrodu.
- **Ram regulacyjne**: Jedne z najwcześniejszych przykładów kompleksowego planowania przez kody, a nie przez projekt autorski.
