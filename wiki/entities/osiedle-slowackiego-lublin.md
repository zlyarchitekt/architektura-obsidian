## Overview
A large residential estate in Lublin designed by [[Oskar Hansen]] and [[Zofia Hansen]], built between 1964 and 1981. It is universally considered one of the purest built manifestations of the [[Open Form]] theory in Poland.

**PL:** *Wielkie osiedle mieszkaniowe w Lublinie, zaprojektowane przez Oskara i Zofię Hansenów, budowane w latach 1964-1981. Powszechnie uznawane za najczystszą realizację przestrzenną teorii Formy Otwartej w Polsce.*

## Key Works / Projects
The estate is famous for its separation of vehicular and pedestrian traffic ("served" and "serving" zones), its blocks curving in harmony with the topography, varied balcony placements designed to break up scale, and specialized community spaces like the [[Teatr Formy Otwartej]].

## Ideas & Influences
The entire estate was conceptually framed not as a finished composition of buildings, but as a background meant to be animated and completed by the residents' daily lives ([[Open Form]]).

## In the Sources
- Discussed in [[rzut-powojenny-modernizm]] regarding its ongoing modernization. Because the estate's urban layout is on the municipal record, conservators managed to enforce proper [[Thermomodernization]] practices: using varied thicknesses of insulation with different conductivity (λ) to maintain the delicate original facade grids, and correcting balcony rail heights which were 20cm too short by modern standards.
