## Overview

The Bom Successo Group consists of 19 houses in an abstract geometric pattern along a route in the Bom Sucesso residential zone near Óbidos. The pattern has two layers: the ground plan and the roof seen from above, integrally related yet completely different in character. The square ground plan's wall masses of different thickness border areas that melt into one. The plan can be interpreted as a transcription of a Theo van Doesburg painting — a decisive figure in the Dutch De Stijl Movement (Neo-Plasticism). Constructed of tectonic vertical and horizontal blocks, it evokes megalithic stone works. The roof masses are set on top like Balearic taluas, producing a pattern akin to Piet Mondrian's Neo-Plasticist pictures. Arrival and parking are in front; broken-up masses toward the garden provide dining and rest areas protected from sun and wind.

Wzorzec ma dwie warstwy: plan parteru i dach widziany z góry, integralnie powiązane, lecz całkowicie różne w charakterze. Plan kwadratowy z masami ścian o różnej grubości można zinterpretować jako transkrypcję obrazu Theo van Doesburga — kluczowej postaci holenderskiego ruchu De Stijl (Neoplastycyzm).
