## Overview

Peter Zumthor is a Swiss architect and Pritzker Prize laureate (2009) known for an uncompromising minimalism rooted in material honesty, haptic experience, and landscape sensitivity. His work — from the Thermal Vals to the Kolumba Museum — privileges craft, atmosphere, and the sensory over the conceptual. In housing, the Spittelhof Estate near Basel exemplifies his approach: simple materials, parallel-slope staircases, and a composition that frames bucolic alpine terrain rather than dominating it.

**PL:** Peter Zumthor to szwajcarski architekt i laureat Nagrody Pritzkera (2009), znany z bezkompromisowego minimalizmu zakorzenionego w uczciwości materiałowej, haptycznym doświadczeniu i wrażliwości na krajobraz. Jego prace — od Therme Vals po Muzeum Kolumba — faworyzują rzemiosło, atmosferę i zmysłowość ponad konceptualność.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Thermal Vals** (1996) — quarry-stone spa baths; Zumthor's breakthrough work.
- **Kolumba Museum** (2007) — Cologne; architectural archaeology of a bombed cathedral site.
- **Spittelhof Estate** (1989–96) — low-rise housing at the forest edge, Basel.
