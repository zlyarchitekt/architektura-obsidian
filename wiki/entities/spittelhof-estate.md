## Overview

The Spittelhof Estate is a low-rise, high-density housing development on a gently sloping plot at the edge of a forested hillside just outside Basel. Designed by Peter Zumthor between 1989 and 1996, the composition consists of three masses enclosed within a frame that suits the bucolic alpine courtyard environment. Two rows of two-storey row houses angle upward, enclosing one another at the highest point and enjoying views toward untouched nature. A three-storey apartment house sits at the vortex of the triangle, oriented toward the valley. Parking is tucked beneath the taller building or on separate levels; inner green areas are divided into small, pedestrian-only gardens. Parallel-slope staircases divide interior space into two strips, minimising traffic surfaces. Every unit has a separate street entrance.

Parking jest ukryty pod wyższym budynkiem lub na osobnych poziomach; wewnętrzne zieleńce podzielone są na małe ogrody wyłącznie dla pieszych.
