## Overview
Marek Leykam was a prominent Polish modernist architect, particularly active in the post-war reconstruction of Poland. His work is characterized by a distinctive, often monumental approach to modernism, frequently utilizing exposed concrete frames and dramatic geometric forms. He was the author of several iconic pre- and post-socialist realist modernist buildings in Poland.

**PL:** Marek Leykam to wybitny polski architekt modernistyczny, szczególnie aktywny w okresie powojennej odbudowy. Jego twórczość charakteryzuje się wyrazistym, często monumentalnym podejściem do modernizmu, chętnie operującym odsłoniętym szkieletem żelbetowym i wyrazistymi formami geometrycznymi. Był autorem szeregu ikonicznych brył z okresu powojennego.

## Key Works / Projects
- **Okrąglak** (1948–1955, Poznań) — cylindrical department store, an icon of Polish mid-century modernism; see [[Okrąglak Poznań]].
- **Supersam** (Warsaw, demolished) — innovative suspended roof structure (co-designed with Jerzy Hryniewiecki and Wacław Zalewski).
- **Stadium of the 10th Anniversary** (Warsaw)
- **Ministry of National Defence building** (Warsaw)

## Ideas & Influences
Leykam's signature style frequently involved a structural grid expressed clearly on the façade, often employing prefabricated precast concrete elements. His work managed to maintain a high degree of modernist rigor even during periods when the political climate strongly favored Socialist Realism, though he was sometimes forced to compromise.
