## Overview

Copenhill (official name: Amager Bakke / Amager Resource Centre) is a waste-to-energy power plant in Amager, Copenhagen, designed by [[BIG — Bjarke Ingels Group]] under [[Bjarke Ingels]], opened 2019. The building incinerates approximately 440,000 tonnes of waste per year, generating electricity for 150,000 homes and district heating for 160,000. Its roof is a 400-metre ski slope — usable by the public year-round, with a chairlift — plus a climbing wall on the façade and a hiking trail. The smoke rings emitted from the chimney — one ring per tonne of CO₂ released — are BIG's most discussed "unsolicited solution": a technical innovation that nearly derailed the project and was ultimately achieved.

Pierścienie dymu z komina — jeden pierścień na tonę uwolnionego CO₂ — to najbardziej dyskutowane „niezamówione rozwiązanie" BIG.

## Design

**Rooftop ski slope**: The roof of the power plant is designed as a continuous sloped surface — planted with artificial grass and snow-groomed in winter — that functions as a public recreational ski slope with a chairlift. A hiking trail winds around the perimeter. A climbing wall (85m high) runs the height of the building's façade.

**Smoke rings**: Bjarke Ingels insisted that the chimney emit CO₂ in the form of smoke rings — one ring per tonne of CO₂ — as a "visible reminder of the plant's emissions" and an aesthetic spectacle. The technical challenge was significant (no existing system could produce consistent rings at this scale and weather variability); development cost and engineering risk nearly caused the project's funders to cancel this element. Ingels held firm. The rings were eventually achieved.

The smoke rings are the clearest expression of BIG's "solutions nobody asked for" principle — Ingels to partner David Zahle: "If we didn't do this, we'd be like every other architect." Zahle's concern: if the rings fail, "nothing will come of this." Ingels's position: the rings are non-negotiable.

**Form**: The building's volume slopes from street level to the plant's exhaust chimney, creating the roof slope. The facade is clad in stacked aluminium bricks.
