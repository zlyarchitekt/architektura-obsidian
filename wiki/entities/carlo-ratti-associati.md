## Overview

Carlo Ratti Associati (CRA) is an architecture and urban design practice founded by Carlo Ratti, who is simultaneously Director of the MIT Senseable City Laboratory in Cambridge, Massachusetts. The firm operates at the intersection of architecture, urban design, and technology — frequently described as a "think-and-do tank." CRA is known for projects that embed sensor networks, data visualisation, and digital intelligence into the built environment, while also pursuing biophilic and sustainability agendas at building scale.

Ratti himself is a prolific writer and speaker on the future of cities, smart urbanism, and the relationship between technology and urban life. The firm works globally, typically on high-profile mixed-use towers, urban installations, and research projects.

## Ideas & Influences

CRA's work is defined by two sometimes-competing impulses: the technological (data-driven urbanism, sensor-embedded architecture, real-time responsive systems) and the ecological (biophilic design, living systems within buildings). CapitaSpring represents the ecological strand strongly, though the firm's broader profile is more associated with digital urbanism.

The MIT Senseable City Lab connection means Ratti's architecture practice is informed by quantitative urban research — an unusual combination in the field.

**PL:** Twórczość CRA definiowana przez dwa czasem konkurujące impulsy: technologiczny (urbanistyka data-driven, architektura z osadzonymi sensorami, systemy responsywne w czasie rzeczywistym) i ekologiczny (projektowanie biofiliczne, żywe systemy w budynkach). CapitaSpring silnie reprezentuje nurt ekologiczny, choć szerszy profil pracowni bardziej kojarzony jest z urbanistyką cyfrową. Połączenie z MIT Senseable City Lab oznacza, że praktyka architektoniczna Rattiego jest informowana przez ilościowe badania miejskie — niezwykła kombinacja w dziedzinie.
