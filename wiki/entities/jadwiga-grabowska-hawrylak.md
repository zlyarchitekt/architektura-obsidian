## Overview
Jadwiga Grabowska-Hawrylak was an exceptionally influential Polish architect, renowned for shaping the post-war urban landscape of rebuilt Wrocław. Her most famous work, the residential complex on Plac Grunwaldzki (commonly known as "Manhattan" or "Sedesowce"), is considered one of the foremost achievements of European brutalism. She consistently strove to embed artistic expression within the rigid constraints of socialist prefabrication.

**PL:** Jadwiga Grabowska-Hawrylak to niezwykle wybitna polska architektka, kluczowa dla powojennego ukształtowania wrocławskiego krajobrazu. Jej słynny wrocławski "Manhattan" uchodzi za jedno z najważniejszych dzieł europejskiego brutalizmu i strukturalizmu i stanowi spektakularny dowód walki o rzeźbiarską formę mimo dławiących rygorów technologii wielkopłytowej zmuszanej na architektach komunistycznej Polski.

## Key Works / Projects
- **[[Osiedle Plac Grunwaldzki]]** (1967–1973, Wrocław) — six brutalist towers set upon an elevated pedestrian esplanade, featuring dynamic, precast concrete facades.
- **Mezonetowiec** (1960, Wrocław) — the first modernist residential block in Poland to feature split-level apartments (maisonettes), inspired directly by Le Corbusier's Unité d'habitation.

## Ideas & Influences
She famously stated that modernism is characterized by beautiful, white facades and structural truth, strongly condemning the contemporary trend of covering these buildings in disjointed pastel insulation. Despite working essentially with concrete block housing, she manipulated standard precast elements to create deep, sculptural reliefs that captured light and shadow.
