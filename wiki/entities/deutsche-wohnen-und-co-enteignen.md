## Overview

Deutsche Wohnen und Co enteignen (DWE — "Expropriate Deutsche Wohnen and Co") is a Berlin citizen initiative founded in 2018 to campaign for the socialization (*Vergesellschaftung*) of approximately 250,000 apartments owned by large profit-oriented corporations — specifically commercial landlords holding more than 3,000 apartments each. The initiative invokes Article 15 of the German Basic Law, which permits the socialization of land, natural resources, and key industries — a provision that has never been used in the history of the Federal Republic. Despite the provocative word "Enteignen" (expropriate) in its name, the legal mechanism pursued is Vergesellschaftung, not Enteignung: the name is deliberately chosen as a "Trojan horse," using the more emotionally charged term to mobilize mass support while advancing a structurally distinct constitutional reform.

**PL:** Deutsche Wohnen und Co enteignen (DWE) to berlińska inicjatywa obywatelska założona w 2018 roku, dążąca do uspołecznienia ok. 250 tys. mieszkań należących do dużych korporacji (komercyjni landlordzi z >3 tysiącami mieszkań). Powołuje się na art. 15 Konstytucji Niemiec — nigdy wcześniej nieużyty. Nazwa zawiera „Enteignen" jako „koń trojański" do masowej mobilizacji, choć mechanizm prawny to Vergesellschaftung.

## Timeline

- **2018** — Founded; initial scepticism from other housing activists; legal working group (*Vergesellschaftung AG*) begins analysis of Article 15 GG; activists (not lawyers) discover the provision had been invisible in legal professional memory for 70 years.
- **2021, February–May** — Signature drive for a referendum; ~200,000 signatures collected, ~130,000 deemed valid (70% validity rate due to German-citizens-only rule). The citizenship restriction is noted as a legitimacy gap: one-quarter of Berlin's registered residents lack German passports.
- **2021, March** — The Mietendeckel (rent cap) struck down by the Federal Constitutional Court as exceeding Senate competence; DWE gains momentum.
- **2021, May** — Vonovia announces acquisition of Deutsche Wohnen; simultaneously proposes selling 20,000 apartments to the city at 2.1 billion euro. Senate accepts; DWE activists see this as a betrayal at the initiative's moment of maximum political leverage.
- **2021, September** — Berlin referendum: more than 50% of voters support socialization — concurrent with Berlin parliamentary elections. SPD-Green-Left coalition forms; SPD (Franziska Giffey, mayor) openly opposed; Green deputies broadly sympathetic; Left deputies actively supportive.
- **2022, April** — Expert commission established under former Federal Justice Minister Herta Däubler-Gmelin; 3 DWE-delegated experts + 10 Senate-appointed constitutional law specialists; advisory (not decision-making) body; report expected within one year.

**PL:** 2018: założenie; Vergesellschaftung AG analizuje art. 15 GG. 2021 luty–maj: zbiórka podpisów (~200 tys., ~130 tys. ważnych). Marzec 2021: Mietendeckel obalony przez FTK. ## Legal Strategy

The initiative's legal argument rests on three pillars:

1. **Article 15 GG** provides explicit constitutional authorization; unlike Enteignung (Article 14), which requires case-by-case public interest justification, Vergesellschaftung is designed precisely for sector-wide structural transformation when private monopoly or quasi-monopoly damages society.
2. **Compensation below market value** is constitutionally required (otherwise it would be a sale, not a socialization). The initiative proposes *obligacje rekompensacyjne* (compensation bonds) of nominal compensation value, redeemed over 40 years from the rental income of the socialized apartments — a self-liquidating mechanism requiring no upfront budget expenditure.
3. **Democratic management** is a constitutionally mandated condition of Vergesellschaftung; the initiative proposes a new public institution with democratic oversight and control, explicitly not a state takeover (which would be nationalization, not socialization).

The constitutional robustness argument: Mietendeckel was struck down as exceeding Senate competence. Vergesellschaftung under Article 15 GG is a matter of *Landesrecht* (state law) and Berlin, uniquely, is both a city and a federal Land — making it the only city in Germany with the constitutional authority to pursue this mechanism unilaterally.

**PL:** Trzy filary argumentu prawnego: (1) Art. 15 GG jako podstawa konstytucyjna sektorowej transformacji; (2) odszkodowanie poniżej rynkowego — obligacje rekompensacyjne na 40 lat z dochodów z najmu (mechanizm samofinansujący); (3) demokratyczne zarządzanie jako konstytucyjny warunek. Argument o solidności konstytucyjnej: Mietendeckel obalony; Vergesellschaftung umocowane w konstytucji. Berlin jako jedyne miasto będące jednocześnie landem — wyjątkowe uprawnienia.

## Political Significance

Kusiak (in [[Uspołeczniamy Berlin]]) argues that DWE's success would constitute "an absolute precedent on a global scale": a democratic community in one of the centres of capitalist power demonstrating that housing financialization can be interrupted through democratic and constitutional means. The global financial actors implicated (BlackRock, Blackstone) are responsible for housing crises in cities worldwide; a successful Berlin Vergesellschaftung would be difficult to dismiss as a marginal local experiment (unlike, say, Porto Alegre's participatory budgeting).

**PL:** Według Kusiak sukces DWE byłby "absolutnym precedensem w skali globalnej": demokratyczna wspólnota w centrum władzy kapitalizmu przerywająca finansjalizację mieszkalnictwa środkami konstytucyjnymi. Trudno byłoby go zdyskredytować jako lokalną ciekawostkę.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The outcome is unresolved: no socialization law has been passed; the expert commission's report was pending as of this source.
- Vonovia's absorption of Deutsche Wohnen changes the target landscape; it is unclear whether the initiative's criteria (commercial landlords with >3,000 apartments) are affected by the merger or whether Vonovia simply becomes the primary target.
- The German-citizens-only validity rule creates a democratic gap: 25% of Berlin's registered residents without German passports cannot participate in the referendum that directly affects their housing.
- Compensation cost: the 10 bn (DWE) vs. 28–36 bn euro (Senate) gap reflects a political rather than technical disagreement — the compensation level will be set by a law not yet written.
