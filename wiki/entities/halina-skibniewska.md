## Overview

Halina Skibniewska (1921–2011) was a Polish architect and one of only three women to receive the SARP Honorowa Nagroda for independent creative work in the award's 57-year history (1966–2023). She received the award in 1978. Skibniewska was an important figure in Polish post-war modernist housing and urban planning, and a member of parliament (Sejm). Her recognition in 1978 — coming four years after Jadwiga Grabowska-Hawrylak's award in 1974 — represents one of the few moments of institutional acknowledgment of women's contributions to Polish architecture in the 20th century.
