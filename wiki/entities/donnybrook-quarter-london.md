## Overview

Donnybrook Quarter is an infill housing development on a corner plot in London, designed by Peter Barber to "patch" gaps in the city's fabric. The snow-white, permeable block is pierced with passages and fashioned with cut-outs, drawing city life inside. Two pedestrian routes cut the ensemble into three portions: two narrower blocks touching existing structures, and a taller corner mass with landmark emphasis. A triangular meeting place sits at the building's core. Upper-storey terraces connect to street-level apartments and first-floor maisonettes, which feel like separate houses due to ground-floor entrances and wide terraces. Flats vary from 40 to 100 m².
