## Overview
Maciej Nowicki (Matthew Nowicki) was arguably Poland's most internationally recognized architect of the mid-20th century. Following World War II, during which he fought in the resistance, he emigrated and made significant contributions to global modernism before dying tragically in a plane crash at the age of 40. He served as Poland's representative in the design of the UN Headquarters.

**PL:** Maciej Nowicki był bodaj najbardziej rozpoznawalnym na świecie polskim architektem połowy XX wieku. Po wojnie emigrował do USA. Był oficjalnym przedstawicielem Polski w prestiżowym gronie architektów projektujących gmach ONZ. Zginął tragicznie w katastrofie lotniczej wieku 40 lat, będąc u progu spektakularnej globalnej kariery.

## Key Works / Projects
- **J.S. Dorton Arena** (1952, Raleigh, North Carolina) — completed after his death, this arena features a revolutionary saddle-shaped, suspended cable roof.
- **UN Headquarters** (1947, New York) — co-designer in evaluating Le Corbusier and Oscar Niemeyer's blueprints.
- **Chandigarh** (India) — tapped by Albert Mayer to design the new capital; upon Nowicki's death, the commission eventually went to [[Le Corbusier]].

## Ideas & Influences
Nowicki possessed an incredible talent for integrating complex structural engineering with poetic, dynamic architectural form (like the catenary curves of the Dorton Arena). He represented a transition from strict, rigid, box-like international style towards a more expressive, sculptural structuralism.
