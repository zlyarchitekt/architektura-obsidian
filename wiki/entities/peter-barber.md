## Overview

Peter Barber is a London-based architect known for inventive, community-focused social housing that repairs urban fabric rather than replacing it. His work is characterised by snow-white, permeable masses pierced with passages and cut-outs, creating lively street life. Donnybrook Quarter (2000) exemplifies his approach: a triangular meeting place at the core, maisonettes that feel like houses, and a composition that draws city life inside while respecting London's Victorian street morphology.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Donnybrook Quarter** (2000) — infill housing with passages, maisonettes, and triangular courtyard.
- **Alexandra Road Estate** (various) — social housing on difficult urban sites.
- **McGrath Road** (2020) — terraced social housing with generous shared spaces.
