## Overview

The Bombay Sapphire Distillery at Laverstoke, Hampshire is a gin distillery created by [[Heatherwick Studio]] through the adaptive reuse of the former Laverstoke Mill — a historic banknote printing plant on the River Test. Opened in 2014, it was Heatherwick Studio's first conservation project and first commission for a production facility.

Working with English Heritage and English Nature, the studio masterplanned the transformation: 23 of over 40 derelict buildings were restored and 9 more recent industrial structures demolished; the River Test, which flows through the site (and had been channelised), was uncovered, widened, and its banks planted; a central courtyard was created. The centrepiece of the scheme is a pair of intertwined curvilinear glasshouses housing the tropical and Mediterranean plant species used in the distillation process — supplemented by plants from their native ecosystems, watered from the river and warmed by heat generated in the distillation process itself. The distillery was the first drinks manufacturing plant and the first renovation to achieve BREEAM 'Outstanding' accreditation.
