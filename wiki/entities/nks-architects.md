## Overview

NKS Architects is a Japanese firm known for small-scale residential and commercial buildings that maximise density through geometric ingenuity. Their Kanoya project in Fukuoka (2002) packs nine micro-flats (25–45 m²) and two ground-floor businesses into a narrow block, using three Tetris-like plan types. A broken concrete plate system linking parking to flats serves as a satirical "umbilical cord" critiquing car dependency. Galleried spaces embody Japanese minimalism, with flexible upper-storey terraces for three units.
