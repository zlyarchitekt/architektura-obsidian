## Overview
The Terrace Houses in Pasadena, Japan, were designed by Kiyonori Kikutake in 1973. Wedged into a bay of the hillside above Mishima, the complex contains 120 apartments adhering to contour lines and winding along them to create a complex multi-level spatial system. Access is possible from the valley direction — from car parks at the foot of the terraced structure on two levels. The development provides an example of architectural expression of co-existence and communal lifestyle as an open and yet introverted composition.

## Key Features
- 120 apartments on hillside above Mishima
- Complex multi-level spatial system adhering to contour lines
- Access from valley via car parks at foot of terraced structure on two levels
- Exterior decks used as outdoor gardens accessible via sunny terraces or stairs from roofed circulation system beneath RC structure on plinths
- Two-level composition: individual family areas on outer crust intertwined with public circulation; lower level with communal functions (laundry, conference room, children's library, game hall) protected against rain and sun
- Response to topographical features of steep hillsides
- Architectural expression of co-existence and communal lifestyle
- Open yet introverted development

## Context and Significance
The Pasadena Terrace Houses are a major work of Japanese Metabolism applied to residential hillside development. Kikutake, a founding figure of the Metabolist movement, treated the hillside not as an obstacle but as a generative substrate: the contour lines become the organising principle, the terraces become outdoor rooms, and the RC structure on plinths elevates circulation into a roofed public realm. The two-level composition — private family areas on the sun-exposed outer crust, communal facilities on the protected lower level — demonstrates the Metabolist commitment to explicit spatial programming of social relationships. The project's location on steep hillsides formerly considered uninhabitable reflects Japan's post-war land scarcity and the Metabolist ambition to conquer new territories through architectural ingenuity. The name "Pasadena" is an intriguing choice, possibly referencing the California hillside housing tradition, but the chapter does not explain the reference.

Kikutake, zalozycielski postac ruchu metabolistycznego, traktowal zbocze nie jako przeszkode, ale jako generatywne podloze: linie konturu staja sie zasada organizujaca, tarasy staja sie zewnetrznymi pokojami, a konstrukcja RC na cokolach wynosi komunikacje do krytej sfery publicznej. Dwupoziomowa kompozycja — prywatne obszary rodzinne na naslonecznionej zewnetrznej skorupie, obiekty wspolne na chronionym nizszym poziomie — demonstruje metabolistyczne zaangazowanie w jawne programowanie przestrzenne relacji spolecznych. Polozenie projektu na stromych zboczach dawniej uwazanych za niezamieszkalne odzwierciedla powojenne braki ziemi w Japonii i metabolistyczna ambicje podboju nowych terytoriow poprzez pomyslowosc architektoniczna.
