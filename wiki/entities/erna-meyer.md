## Overview
German architect and one of the first women to study architecture in Germany. She developed a feminist programme that intellectualised housework, treating the management of a household as equivalent to business administration. Her 1926 Stuttgart exhibition featured training kitchens where working-class women learned to use new domestic appliances.

**PL:** *Niemiecka architektka i jedna z pierwszych kobiet studiujących architekturę w Niemczech. Rozwinęła feministyczny program intelektualizacji pracy domowej, traktując prowadzenie gospodarstwa jako równoważne zarządzaniu biznesem. Jej wystawa w Stuttgarcie w 1926 r. prezentowała kuchnie treningowe, w których biedne gospodynie uczyły się obsługi nowoczesnych urządzeń.*

## Key Works / Projects
- **Training kitchens for Stuttgart exhibition** (1926) — progressive kitchen prototypes with films and instruction manuals; a genuine spatial revolution that disoriented users accustomed to traditional layouts.

## Ideas & Influences
Meyer believed women had to organise and educate themselves rather than wait for male experts. Her work bridges feminist activism and architectural modernism, demanding that domestic space be treated with the same rational attention as factory floor planning.
