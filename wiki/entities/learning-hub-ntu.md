## Overview

The Learning Hub at Nanyang Technological University (NTU) in Singapore — informally known as "The Hive" — is a 14,000 sqm educational building by [[Heatherwick Studio]], executed by CPG Consultants. Twelve concrete towers, each a stack of rounded tutorial rooms, taper inward at their base around a generous central public atrium, yielding 56 tutorial rooms across 8 storeys. The building is organised against the traditional university spatial model (corridors linking isolated box rooms) and for an alternative: a continuous social landscape where incidental encounter and cross-disciplinary interaction are spatially engineered. Its exterior — elaborate cast concrete surfaces including 700 relief-cast illustrations and textured columns — embodies Heatherwick's ambition to make an industrial material feel handmade.

Jego zewnętrze — rozbudowane odlewane powierzchnie betonowe obejmujące 700 reliefów i teksturowane kolumny — ucieleśnia ambicję Heatherwicta, by sprawić, że materiał przemysłowy poczuje się ręcznie wykonany.

## Design

**Spatial organisation**: The 12 towers cluster around a central naturally ventilated atrium that functions as a shared social commons. Rooms open onto shared circulation, interspersed with open spaces and garden terraces. The towers taper at their bases — the widening upper floors maximise room area while the narrowed lower sections create a light-permeable ground level.

**Room design**: Tutorial rooms are rounded with no corners and no obvious front or back. This explicitly dissolves the frontal hierarchy of conventional classrooms (teacher at front, students behind). Flexible furniture configurations allow professors to rearrange rooms for different pedagogies. 56 rooms in total.

**Concrete craft programme**: The building's entire envelope is raw concrete, treated as a surface for craft:
- 700 drawings by illustrator Sara Fanelli cast three-dimensionally into the concrete stair and elevator cores — overlapping images meant as "deliberately ambiguous thought triggers"
- 61 angled columns, each with a custom undulating texture
- Curved facade panels cast with a unique horizontal relief pattern via 10 adjustable silicone moulds
- Combined effect: "the whole project appears to have been handmade from wet clay"

**Environmental performance**: The central atrium is naturally ventilated, maximising air movement in Singapore's tropical climate (25–31°C year-round). Tutorial rooms use silent convection cooling — no energy-heavy air conditioning fans. BCA Green Mark Platinum (Singapore's highest environmental certification).

**Site area**: 2,000 sqm. **GFA**: ~14,000 sqm. **Height**: 8 storeys / 38.3m.

## Ideas & Influences

The building is Heatherwick's spatial argument about 21st-century higher education: in the digital age, access to information is ubiquitous; the campus's primary value is therefore social, not informational. Space should engineer encounter — between disciplines, between students and professors, between ideas. The brief explicitly asked for this, making NTU an unusually willing institutional client.

The concrete craft programme extends Heatherwick's broader commitment to buildings that "feel made" — that carry evidence of human labour and imagination in their surfaces, as a counterweight to the visual anonymity of mainstream commercial construction.

**PL:** Budynek jest przestrzennym argumentem Heatherwicta o szkolnictwie wyższym XXI wieku: w erze cyfrowej dostęp do informacji jest wszechobecny; podstawowa wartość kampusu jest zatem społeczna, nie informacyjna. Przestrzeń powinna inżynierować spotkanie — między dyscyplinami, studentami a profesorami, między ideami. Brief explicite o to prosił, czyniąc NTU wyjątkowo chętnym klientem instytucjonalnym.

Program rzemiosła betonowego rozszerza szersze zaangażowanie Heatherwicta w budynki, które „czują się zrobione" — noszące ślady ludzkiej pracy i wyobraźni na powierzchniach, jako przeciwwaga dla wizualnej anonimowości mainstreamu.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The spatial hypothesis (rooms without corners → collaborative learning) is an architectural claim not an evidenced outcome. No post-occupancy study cited.
- "The Hive" nickname widely used elsewhere but absent from source.
- Completion date not given in source (c. 2015 from external knowledge).
- CPG Consultants' role as "lead architect" — i.e., the licensed party responsible for building compliance — means the design credit split is not straightforward. Standard in Singapore practice for foreign firms.
