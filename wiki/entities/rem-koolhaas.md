## Overview

Rem Koolhaas (born 1944, Rotterdam) is a Dutch architect, urbanist, and theorist, founder of [[OMA]] (Office for Metropolitan Architecture). Among starchitects, he is exceptional for the intellectual seriousness of his writing: trained as a journalist before architecture, his work at OMA is paralleled by a body of essays and books — *Delirious New York* (1978), *S,M,L,XL* (1995), *Junk Space* (2001), *Countryside* (2020) — that engage urban theory at a level none of his peers match. He received the Pritzker Prize in 2000. He is also notable for simultaneously participating in the starchitect economy and offering its most penetrating internal critique.

## Ideas & Influences

**Delirious New York** (1978): Manhattan as the "Culture of Congestion" — the city as a machine for programmatic intensity, where each block is a world unto itself, and the city's vitality derives from the density and collision of different programmes. The retroactive manifesto for what New York had unconsciously built.

**Bigness** (*S,M,L,XL*, 1995): buildings at such scale they transcend context, generate their own internal cultures, and exist alongside the city rather than within it. "Fuck the context." See [[Bigness]].

**Junk Space** (2001): the actual built result of the commercial application of Bigness — the continuous, undifferentiated, climate-controlled interior of mall, airport, and hotel atrium. "Junk Space is the body double of space, its filler." A self-critique from within the starchitect economy.

Koolhaas was trained as a journalist and scriptwriter before studying architecture at the AA London and Cornell. His writing method — empirical, fast-moving, associative — owes more to journalism than philosophy, which partly explains why it retains readability where Eisenman or Tschumi become dense.

He founded AMO as a research think-tank alongside OMA — allowing the firm to operate across design, policy, and speculation simultaneously.

[[Bjarke Ingels]] trained at OMA and acknowledges Koolhaas as a formative influence.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- CCTV Beijing was a prestige commission from the Chinese state — a paradox for an architect who critiques the "regime of ¥€$" of globalised commercial culture.
- Koolhaas's critique of junk space is intellectually compelling, but OMA participates in the same commercial economy that produces it. Whether this is hypocrisy or dialectical engagement is debated.
- *Countryside* (2020) shifted his attention to rural and agricultural space — a radical departure that some read as intellectual renewal, others as restless provocation.

- *Countryside* (2020): przesunięcie ku przestrzeni wiejskiej i rolniczej — odczytywane jako intelektualna odnowa lub niespokojna prowokacja.
