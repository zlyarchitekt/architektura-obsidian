## Overview
Arseniusz Romanowicz was a master of Polish infrastructural modernism. Best known for his decades-long collaboration with Piotr Szymaniak, Romanowicz designed the most spectacular post-war railway stations and transport hubs in Poland, notably along Warsaw’s cross-city line (Linia Średnicowa). His crowning achievement is the iconic [[Dworzec Centralny]] in Warsaw.

**PL:** Arseniusz Romanowicz to niekwestionowany mistrz polskiego modernizmu infrastrukturalnego. Najbardziej znany z wieloletniej współpracy z Piotrem Szymaniakiem, Romanowicz zaprojektował najbardziej spektakularne powojenne i późnomodernistyczne dworce i węzły przesiadkowe w Polsce, głównie wzdłuż warszawskiej Linii Średnicowej. Jego koronnym dziełem jest Dworzec Centralny w Warszawie.

## Key Works / Projects
- **[[Dworzec Centralny]]** (1975, Warsaw) — highly praised by international critics as an outstanding piece of modern public space, often outclassing its Western European contemporaries.
- **Warszawa Powiśle and Warszawa Ochota stations** (1963) — featuring light, flying, parabolic thin-shell concrete roofs.
- **YMCA Boathouse** (1948, Warsaw) — early post-war functionality seamlessly integrated into the river landscape.

## Ideas & Influences
Romanowicz defined his approach with strict adherence to three principles: a building must be functional, aesthetic, and economic. He possessed a rare ability to transform massive, heavy engineering tasks (like train stations) into structures that felt lightweight, dynamic, and welcoming, often using expressively swooping rooflines and massive plate-glass walls.
