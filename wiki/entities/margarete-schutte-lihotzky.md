## Overview
Austrian architect and a pioneer of modernist housing design. She is best known for the Frankfurt Kitchen (1926) — a compact, fitted kitchen that became one of the most influential domestic spatial prototypes of the 20th century. Despite designing the canonical kitchen, she reportedly disliked cooking herself.

**PL:** *Austriacka architektka i pionierka modernistycznego projektowania mieszkań. Najbardziej znana z kuchni frankfurckiej (1926) — kompaktowej, zabudowanej kuchni, która stała się jednym z najbardziej wpływowych prototypów przestrzeni domowych XX wieku. Ponoć sama nie przepadała za gotowaniem.*

## Key Works / Projects
- **Frankfurt Kitchen** (1926) — 6.5 m² fitted kitchen with aluminium drawers, electric oven, oak worktops, and unified cabinetry system. Introduced as standard in 10,000 Frankfurt housing units; cost spread via rent instalments. Propaganda tool for bourgeois prosperity.

## Ideas & Influences
Schütte-Lihotzky applied rationalist, industrial design principles to the domestic sphere. Her kitchen was efficient and hygienic, but also fixed the user into a single optimised circuit of labour — a design achievement that simultaneously advanced and constrained women's domestic role.
