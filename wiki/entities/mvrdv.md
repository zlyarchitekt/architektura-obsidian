## Overview

MVRDV is a Rotterdam-based architectural firm founded in 1993 by Winy Maas, Jacob van Rijs, and Nathalie de Vries. Known for data-driven, experimental, and often provocatively dense designs, the firm treats architecture as a research project into extreme programmatic and spatial conditions. Their two Ypenburg projects — Hageneiland (1998–2001) and Patio Island (2001) — explore identity through material (single-material houses) and climate (glazed patio façades), respectively. Both projects push the limits of low-rise density through typological invention.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Hageneiland, Ypenburg** (1998–2001) — 37 colourful houses with single-material identity.
- **Patio Island, Ypenburg** (2001) — twelve fully glazed patio-house strips.
- **Markthal, Rotterdam** (2014) — horseshoe-shaped market hall with residential arc.
