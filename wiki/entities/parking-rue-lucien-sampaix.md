## Overview

A 1935 modernist reinforced-concrete parking garage in Paris's 10th arrondissement, destroyed by fire in October 2020 and now being converted into 16 social housing units by [[UR Architecture Urbanisme]] for social housing agency Paris Habitat. The building occupies a 431 m² plot, fills it entirely, and has four upper floors plus partial basement. Its ribbon-windowed street façade was characteristic of interwar parking modernism. After the fire, instead of demolition, the city exercised its *droit de préemption* (right of preemption) to acquire the building for Paris Habitat — a landmark example of adaptive reuse driven by planning law rather than aesthetic preference alone.

**PL:** Modernistyczny parking z 1935 roku przy rue Lucien Sampaix w Paryżu (10. dzielnica) — po pożarze z 2020 roku przebudowywany na 16 mieszkań komunalnych przez UR Architecture Urbanisme dla Paris Habitat. Przykład adaptacyjnego ponownego wykorzystania napędzonego prawem planistycznym (*droit de préemption*) i polityką mieszkaniową.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Conversion to housing** (in progress, 2020–2027): 16 apartments (studio to 4-room) + 217 m² commercial ground floor; BBC Effinergie Rénovation + Biosourcé certified; cross-ventilated flats with winter gardens (street side) and courtyard-facing living rooms; galleries and balconies in terracotta tile + blue lacquered steel; hemp-wool insulation + lime render on courtyard elevations; glass-block staircase; partially accessible rooftop.
- **Financing**: 6.7 M€ total; 63% Paris Habitat own funds + loans; 32% city grants; housing via PLAI / PLUS / PLS tiers.

## Ideas & Influences

The project's design philosophy explicitly preserves the building's "industrial heritage" — the street façade retains its rhythm of ribbon windows, cleaned and repaired concrete, with new glazing set back behind the existing plane. The courtyard is conceived as a contrasting "domestic world" with warm materials and social-gallery access. The dual-register approach avoids both historicist imitation and surface-level modernisation, instead finding the building's architecture in its own structural logic.
