## Overview

Galeria Bawełnianka is a 35,000 m² shopping centre in Bełchatów, central Poland, built on the site of the former Bełchatowskie Zakłady Przemysłu Bawełnianego (BZPB — Bełchatów Cotton Industry Works, est. 1947, liquidated 2013). It is the largest permanently vacant shopping centre in Poland's history: constructed 2007–2012, it never opened, went bankrupt in 2015, was sold to GBB Invest in 2019, and was still awaiting its first tenants as of early 2023. The building has become a case study for the systemic failures of Polish spatial planning, retail over-supply, and the legal vacuum around vacant private property.

**PL:** Galeria Bawełnianka to centrum handlowe o powierzchni 35 tys. m² w Bełchatowie, wybudowane na miejscu dawnych BZPB (od 1947 r., zlikwidowanych w 2013 r.). Nigdy nieczynna: gotowa w 2012 r., bez najemcy, bankructwo w 2015 r., sprzedana GBB Invest w 2019 r. Studium przypadku: systemowe wady polskiego planowania przestrzennego.

## Key Facts

- **Location**: ul. Bawełniana, Bełchatów, central Poland
- **Area**: ~35,000 m² (GLA)
- **Original developer**: Bawełnianka sp. z o.o. (est. 2008)
- **Architects** (original): Pallado i Skupin Architekci
- **Interior redesign concept** (never realised): HSA (Human Scale Architecture), Kraków — industrial style referencing cotton-thread history, parametric lighting over food court
- **Construction**: 2007–2012; demolition of BZPB buildings; iconic factory chimney retained as architectural feature
- **Opening**: never. Bankruptcy proceedings filed 2015; rejected by court (no funds to cover proceedings)
- **Current owner** (2019–): GBB Invest (purchased from bankruptcy receiver for PLN 57 million)
- **Competing building**: Galeria Olimpia (Echo Investment), ~32,000 m², opened 2012 on the southern periphery of Bełchatów — succeeded

## Planning Context

Bełchatów's 2010 Studium permitted six potential large retail sites simultaneously. The 2017 update maintained 566,200 m² theoretical service capacity (equivalent to 16 Bawełniankas) while projecting population decline from 57,000 to 51,000 residents by 2030 — with no market analysis. This is documented by [[Kacper Borek]] as representative of a national Polish planning pathology: systematic land over-allocation without demographic or market analysis.

**PL:** Studium Bełchatowa z 2010 r. zezwalało na 6 lokalizacji dużego handlu równocześnie. Aktualizacja 2017 r.: chłonność 566 200 m² przy prognozowanym spadku ludności. Brak analizy rynkowej. Borek diagnozuje to jako typową polską patologię planistyczną.
