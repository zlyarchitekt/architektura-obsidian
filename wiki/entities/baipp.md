## Overview

The Biuro Architektury i Planowania Przestrzennego (BAiPP) is the architecture and spatial planning office of the City of Warsaw (*m.st. Warszawa*). It is responsible for preparing Warsaw's binding spatial policy documents — the Studium Uwarunkowań i Kierunków Zagospodarowania Przestrzennego ([[SUiKZP]]), local development plans ([[MPZP]]), and the city's urbanistic design standards. BAiPP co-produced RZUT-33 (*Planowanie*), the RZUT quarterly issue dedicated to planning. Under the direction of [[Marlena Happach]], BAiPP developed Warsaw's new Studium (2018–2022) with a climate-first approach, treating the city as an ecosystem and placing [[Blue-Green Infrastructure]] at the core of spatial strategy.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Nowe Studium Warszawy** (2018–2022/2023) — 30-year spatial strategy; BZIW as structural backbone; polycentric compact city; 50+ post-WWII heritage buildings; 4 new cultural parks.
- **RZUT-33 Planowanie** (2022) — co-produced issue of RZUT quarterly on planning; articles by BAiPP staff alongside independent researchers.

## Ideas & Influences

BAiPP's approach under Happach is characterised by: **ecosystem thinking** (social + economic + natural spheres integrated); **climate primacy** (resilience over aesthetics); **evidence-based demographic analysis** (mobile phone data supplanting inaccurate registry figures); and the **"wszędzie blisko"** (everywhere close) spatial ideal — a hierarchical network of local centres reachable on foot in 10 minutes. The office developed the *bilans* (land-capacity accounting) methodology as the analytical foundation for all land designation decisions in the new Studium.

Key BAiPP staff active in RZUT-33: [[Wojciech Kacperski]] (author of overview article), [[Monika Konrad]] (deputy director, chief designer of Studium), [[Bartosz Rozbiewski]] (deputy director for spatial policy). Each has their own entity page.
