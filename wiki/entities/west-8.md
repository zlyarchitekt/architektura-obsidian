## Overview

West 8 is a Rotterdam-based urban design and landscape architecture firm founded by Adriaan Geuze in 1987. Known for bold, playful, and ecologically inventive public spaces, the firm bridges landscape architecture and urbanism. Their masterplan for Borneo Island in Amsterdam's Eastern Harbour District (1993–96) is a canonical example of multi-architect low-rise density: 8×35 m plots, "meteorite" infill blocks, and a red bridge, all set within a former industrial waterfront.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Borneo Island, Amsterdam** (1993–96) — multi-architect waterfront housing masterplan.
- **Schouwburgplein, Rotterdam** (1996) — theatrical red public square with hydraulic stages.
- **Governors Island, New York** (ongoing) — post-military landscape regeneration.
