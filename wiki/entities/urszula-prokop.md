## Overview

Urszula Prokop is a Polish architectural historian and researcher with a focus on mid-20th-century planning history, Israeli architecture, and the intersection of urbanism and political history. Her RZUT-33 essay "Plan Sharona" (2022) is one of the few Polish-language scholarly texts to critically examine Arieh Sharon's National Plan for Israel (1949–1952), drawing on both primary planning sources (Sharon's own publications) and critical revisionist scholarship (Zvi Efrat, Sharon Rotbard).

## Key Works / Projects

- **Plan Sharona** (RZUT-33, 2022) — historical essay on Israel's National Plan 1948–1953; covers the plan's five pillars, New Towns typology, land confiscation mechanisms, ethnic sorting outcomes, and political aftermath.
