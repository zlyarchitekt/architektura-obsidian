## Overview

Eduardo Souto de Moura is a Portuguese architect and Pritzker Prize laureate (2011) known for a purified, minimalist architecture of severe material precision. A frequent collaborator of Álvaro Siza, Souto de Moura works with concrete, stone, and steel in compositions that privilege mass, shadow, and structural expression over ornament. His Atrium Houses in Mathosinos, Porto, exemplify this approach: ten seaside units organised into five strips of arrival, sitting, work, living, and garden, with solid walls alternating with permeable bands.

Częsty współpracownik Álvaro Sizy, Souto de Moura pracuje z betonem, kamieniem i stalą w kompozycjach faworyzujących masę, cień i ekspresję strukturalną ponad ornamentykę.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Estádio Municipal de Braga** (2004) — carved granite stadium; Pritzker-cited masterpiece.
- **Casa das Histórias Paula Rego** (2009) — Cascais; pyramidal towers in red concrete.
- **Atrium Houses, Mathosinos** (1993) — ten seaside units with five-strip spatial logic.
