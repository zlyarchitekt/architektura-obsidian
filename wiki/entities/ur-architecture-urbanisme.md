## Overview

UR Architecture Urbanisme is a Paris-based architectural practice specialising in low-carbon residential design, adaptive reuse of existing buildings, and the modernisation of public facilities. In 2024 the firm received the prestigious *Palmarès des Jeunes Urbanistes* from the French Ministry of Ecological Transition and Spatial Planning — an award recognising innovative urban design practice that addresses contemporary climatic, social, and economic challenges at multiple scales. Their approach integrates architecture and urbanism as inseparable disciplines.

## Key Works / Projects

- **[[Parking rue Lucien Sampaix]]** (in progress, Paris 10e) — conversion of a fire-damaged 1935 modernist garage into 16 social housing units (PLAI/PLUS/PLS) + 217 m² ground-floor retail for Paris Habitat; total cost 6.7 M€; BBC Effinergie Rénovation + Biosourcé certification; completion planned before end 2027.

## Ideas & Influences

The firm's philosophy centres on minimum intervention — treating the existing structure as a resource rather than an obstacle, and drawing the project's architectural character from the building's own logic. At Lucien Sampaix, the street façade retains and celebrates the industrial grid of the 1935 garage, while the courtyard side is designed as a contrasting warm domestic world. This dual-register approach (public/industrial vs. private/organic) is a coherent strategy for converting non-residential structures to housing without falsifying either identity.
