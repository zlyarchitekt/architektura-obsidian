## Overview

The Bund Finance Centre (BFC) is a 420,000 m² mixed-use development on Shanghai's historic Bund waterfront, co-designed by [[Foster + Partners]] and [[Heatherwick Studio]]. It comprises eight buildings combining premium offices, a boutique hotel, a cultural centre, luxury retail, and a public plaza, arranged on a site that the architects describe as the "end point" of the Bund — one of the world's most famous urban waterfronts. Completed in 2017, it is notable for its contextual massing, its crafted material palette, and its kinetic cultural centre facade.

## Urban Strategy

The masterplan is framed as an **urban connector** — a permeable pedestrian zone linking the historic old town (west) with Pudong's financial district (east, across the river). The two 180m towers anchor the southern edge; the northern buildings fronting the waterfront are staggered in height to step down toward the river and relate in scale and rhythm to the 19th-century neoclassical and Art Deco landmarks that define the Bund's visual character.

The landscaped public plaza at the scheme's heart is intended to invite the public into the development rather than creating a sealed commercial enclave — though as with [[CapitaSpring]], how genuinely public a luxury commercial development's open spaces are is a critical question.

## Cultural Centre and the Moving Veil

The most architecturally distinctive element is the **cultural centre** at the heart of the scheme — an exhibition, events, and performance venue whose outer skin is a **kinetic moving veil**: a facade element that shifts position to reveal or conceal the stage on the building's balcony, adapting the building's public face to its current programme. The cultural centre is explicitly inspired by the open stages of traditional Chinese theatres — an interpretation of a Chinese spatial and theatrical tradition by two British firms.

This is the most obviously [[Kinetic Architecture|kinetic]] element in the wiki so far — a moving facade that transforms the building's identity between uses.

## Materials

- **Granite**: hand-crafted; edges of each volume taper as they rise — solid at base, increasingly transparent toward top.
- **Bronze details**: used throughout as a finishing material; contributes to the "jewel-like quality" the architects describe.
- The palette is explicitly craft-oriented — a deliberate contrast to generic glass curtain-wall commercial architecture.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- "Inspired by traditional Chinese open theatres" — designed by two British firms; the depth of cultural engagement versus superficial reference is unverifiable.
- The "cultural centre" also serves "brand events, product launches, and corporate functions" — its independence as a cultural institution is limited.
- No information on how the kinetic veil mechanism works or performs over time.
- The staggered waterfront massing reads well in plan; how it reads from the riverfront or from the Bund promenade is not addressed in the source.
