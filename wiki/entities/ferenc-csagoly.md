## Overview

Ferenc Cságoly is a Hungarian architect known for his terrain-responsive approach to low-rise housing. Together with Ferenc Keller, he designed the Terraced Houses at Barlang Street, Budapest (2001), a twenty-unit development that integrates with a hillside site pierced by natural caves. The project is celebrated for treating the land not as a blank canvas but as the primary generator of architectural form.

Projekt jest doceniany za traktowanie ziemi nie jak pustego płótna, lecz jak głównego generatora formy architektonicznej.
