## Overview
A visionary Polish architect, urbanist, theorist, and painter. Creator of the [[Open Form]] theory. A towering figure in Polish architectural education, particularly at the Warsaw Academy of Fine Arts. He often collaborated with his wife, [[Zofia Hansen]].

**PL:** *Wizjonerski polski architekt, urbanista, teoretyk i malarz. Twórca teorii Formy Otwartej. Wybitna postać w polskiej edukacji architektonicznej, zwłaszcza na warszawskiej ASP. Często współpracował z żoną, Zofią Hansen.*

## Key Works / Projects
- **LCS (Linear Continuous System) / LSC (Linijny System Ciągły)** — His utopian, unbuilt, macro-scale urban planning proposal to restructure the settlement of Poland into linear bands.
- **[[Osiedle Słowackiego (Lublin)]]** (1964–1981) — Co-designed with Zofia Hansen. Widely regarded as the most successful built translation of his Open Form concepts.
- **Przyczółek Grochowski** — A massive residential complex in Warsaw designed as one continuous meandering building. 

## Ideas & Influences
**[[Open Form]]**: The philosophy that architecture should never be closed or complete, but rather provide a flexible, unassuming framework (a *passé-partout*) for the subjective, spontaneous activities of its inhabitants. Opposed to the rigid monumentalism of traditional "Closed Form" design. He belonged to the Team 10 generation which critiqued the rigid zoning of early CIAM.

## In the Sources
- Cited extensively in [[rzut-powojenny-modernizm]] as the ideological author of Osiedle Słowackiego, where his sculptural approach to space (especially the [[Teatr Formy Otwartej]]) is currently threatened by standard building compliance laws.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
His profoundly democratic and humanist theories often clashed with the centrally planned, bureaucratic reality of communist Poland, resulting in estates that suffered from poor material execution and rigid state administration, preventing the true "co-creation" he envisioned.
