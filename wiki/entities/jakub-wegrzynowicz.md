## Overview

Jakub Węgrzynowicz is a Polish researcher and writer working at the intersection of ecology, spatial planning, and multispecies urbanism. His RZUT-33 essay "(Nie)Ścisła Ochrona Przyrody" (2022) represents one of the most systematic critiques of Poland's nature protection regime in architectural discourse — mapping the gap between EU biodiversity targets and Polish planning reality, and arguing for a fundamental shift toward multispecies planning, rewilding, and the legal protection of ecological corridors.

**PL:** Jakub Węgrzynowicz to polski badacz i autor działający na styku ekologii, planowania przestrzennego i urbanistyki wielogatunkowej. Jego esej w RZUT-33 "(Nie)Ścisła Ochrona Przyrody" (2022) stanowi jedną z najbardziej systematycznych krytyk polskiego systemu ochrony przyrody w dyskursie architektonicznym.

## Key Works / Projects

- **(Nie)Ścisła Ochrona Przyrody** (RZUT-33, 2022) — essay diagnosing structural failures of Polish nature protection; proposes multispecies spatial planning, new national parks, legally binding ecological corridors, and the "fourth nature" concept.

## Ideas & Influences

Węgrzynowicz's thinking draws on: **non-human actor theory** (other species as subjects with rights, not just "ecosystem services"); **critique of fortress conservation** (reserves as colonial fantasy islands); **ecological corridor planning** (freedom to roam; the ZBS PAN Białowieża corridor map as a planning tool); and **fourth nature** (*czwarta przyroda* — self-organizing wastelands as legitimate spatial assets). He references Kate Raworth's **Doughnut Economics** as the normative framework for multispecies planning — setting an ecological ceiling within which human needs should be met. His empirical anchor is the "Szast" forest case: biodiversity is higher where foresters withdraw than where they manage.
