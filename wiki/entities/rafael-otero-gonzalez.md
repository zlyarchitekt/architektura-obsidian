## Overview

Rafael Otero Gonzalez is a Spanish architect known for sculptural, inward-turned housing that protects inhabitants from Mediterranean sun while framing sea views. His Cadiz row houses (2004) present a completely closed exterior of snow-white walls broken only by small shaded openings. A diagonal line of houses divides the garden into arrival and gardening zones; roof terraces offer sea views. Light enters through gaps between roof surfaces, creating winding, blinding cubes that spiral inward. The 108 m² units use skylights and roof terraces to extend domestic space vertically.
