## Overview

The Zeitz Museum of Contemporary Art Africa (Zeitz MOCAA) is a 9,500 m² cultural institution at the V&A Waterfront, Cape Town, South Africa, opened on 22 September 2017. Created by [[Heatherwick Studio]] through the conversion of a historic grain silo complex, it became the most significant collection of contemporary art from Africa and its diaspora. It was established as a not-for-profit institution.

The design's central challenge was to carve usable gallery volumes and a dramatic central atrium from a matrix of forty-two cylindrical concrete silo tubes and a grading tower — structures with no right angles and highly constrained internal dimensions. Using a variety of concrete-cutting techniques, galleries were excavated from within the matrix of tubes. The geometry of the central atrium was derived from the cross-section of a grain of wheat, scaled up to fit the full diameter of the silo structure. The concrete tube shafts are capped with strengthened glass walkable by visitors; pillowed glazing panels formed from segments of flat glass admit light to the upper gallery floors. Bisected tubes contain cylindrical lifts and a spiral staircase.
