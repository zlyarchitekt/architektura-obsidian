## Overview

Kacper Borek is a Polish writer and researcher working on the intersection of urban planning failure, retail urbanism, and the life cycles of architectural objects. His RZUT-33 contribution "Skłębione Problemy Pustostanów" (2022) is a longform reportage built around the unresolved story of Galeria Bawełnianka in Bełchatów — Poland's largest permanently vacant shopping centre — which he uses to dissect systemic pathologies of Polish spatial planning and the broader structural crisis of the shopping centre typology.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Skłębione Problemy Pustostanów** (RZUT-33, 2022) — longform essay/reportage on Galeria Bawełnianka, Bełchatów; structural critique of Polish planning over-allocation; Dutch Leegstandswet as model; future of retail as hybrid building.

## Ideas & Influences

Borek's approach is investigative urbanism: using a specific failed building as a diagnostic lens for structural system failures. His key analytical moves: (1) reading a local planning disaster as an index of a national planning pathology (land over-allocation in Polish studia); (2) comparing Polish vacancy law against the Dutch Leegstandswet; (3) situating the Bawełnianka story within the global structural crisis of enclosed shopping malls. He is interested in **circularity in architecture** — buildings as cycles of use, vacancy, and transformation — and in **the agency of vacant space** as a social and political actor.
