## Overview

1,000 Trees is a mixed-use development in Shanghai designed by [[Heatherwick Studio]], completed in two phases with Phase 1 opening in 2021. The project takes the form of two mountain-like peaks clad with trees planted on structural column-capitals at each storey, rising from a park that runs along Suzhou Creek and the M50 Arts District. Local height restrictions defined the profile of the two peaks, which slope down toward the parkland. The scheme incorporates retail, offices, event venues, galleries, and a hotel. An open-air art wall embedded in the development references the historic art wall along Moganshan Road; the building's form also partly draws from the nearby Moganshan area. The project is a prominent example of [[Biophilic Design]] applied at urban mixed-use scale.
