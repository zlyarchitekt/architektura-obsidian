## Overview

Vessel is a public climbable structure at Hudson Yards Public Plaza, New York City, designed by [[Heatherwick Studio]] and opened on 15 March 2019. The structure consists of 154 interlocking flights of stairs (2,500 steps, 80 viewing landings, equivalent to approximately 15 storeys), formally inspired by the ancient stepwells of India and by amphitheatres. It was designed as a public gathering and climbing experience at the centre of the Hudson Yards mega-development by Related Companies.

Vessel was closed to public access in January 2021 following the fourth death by suicide in less than two years — a direct consequence of the structure's form: an open staircase network with minimal barriers between levels and open views to the ground. Full-height steel mesh nets were subsequently installed on each level. The structure partially reopened in October 2024, with the top level remaining closed.

Po instalacji siatek stalowych structure częściowo ponownie otwarto w październiku 2024; górny poziom pozostaje zamknięty.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The fundamental design question left open: was the open staircase without protective barriers a foreseeable harm? No design review or public design post-mortem appears in the sources.
- The structure's artistic/civic value vs. its harm record has not been publicly reconciled.
- The relationship between the structure's form (climbable, open, vertiginous) and its specific risk profile was apparently not addressed in the original design process.
