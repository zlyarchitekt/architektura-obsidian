## Overview

Tadeusz Tołwiński was a Polish architect, urban planner, and academic. He is best known for designing the garden city of Ząbki near Warsaw and for his three-volume textbook *Urbanistyka* (1939–1948), one of the foundational texts of Polish urban planning theory. He held professorships at the Warsaw University of Technology and was active during the interwar period and into the post-war reconstruction era.

**PL:** Tadeusz Tołwiński był polskim architektem, urbanistą i akademikiem. Znany z projektu Ząbek jako miasta-ogrodu pod Warszawą oraz z trzytomowej *Urbanistyki* (1939–1948), jednego z fundamentalnych tekstów polskiej teorii planowania. Był profesorem Politechniki Warszawskiej, czynny w okresie międzywojennym i powojennej odbudowy.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Ząbki (garden city, early 20th c.)** — planned suburban garden city east of Warsaw; coherent street layout and plot subdivision; cited by Uściński as a model of *ład przestrzenny* (spatial order) in contrast to chaotic post-2003 sprawl.
- ***Urbanistyka*** **(1939–1948, 3 vols)** — foundational Polish urban planning textbook; synthesised European and national traditions of urban design.

## Ideas & Influences

His garden city planning followed the tradition of Ebenezer Howard, adapted to Polish interwar conditions. His work represents the peak of early 20th-century Polish urban planning ambition, before the disruption of WWII and the subsequent imposition of socialist planning doctrine.
