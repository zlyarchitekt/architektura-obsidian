## Overview

Rudolf Świerczyński was a prominent Warsaw architect of the interwar period, best known for designing the monumental modernist headquarters of Bank Gospodarstwa Krajowego (1928–1931) and the Ministry of Communications (Ministerstwo Komunikacji, 1929–1931), both on Aleje Jerozolimskie / Chałubińskiego in central Warsaw. His style combines austere structural rationalism with deliberate monumentality — buildings that express institutional authority through mass and restraint rather than ornament, yet clearly belong to the modernist mainstream. He won the BGK competition against 25 other entries.

## Key Works / Projects

- **[[Gmach BGK Warszawa]]** (1928–1931, Warsaw) — Bank Gospodarstwa Krajowego headquarters; Aleje Jerozolimskie 7; andezyt pieniński cladding; glass-roof banking halls; won competition against 25 entries; structural engineer Ludwik Tylbor; bas-reliefs by Jan Szczepkowski; monument registration 1965.
- **Gmach Ministerstwa Komunikacji** (1929–1931, Warsaw) — Ministry of Communications building, ul. Chałubińskiego 4/6; contemporary with BGK; same architect, same institutional register.

## Ideas & Influences

Świerczyński worked within the Polish modernism tradition strongly oriented toward French influence (Le Corbusier) rather than German Bauhaus. His public buildings blend modernist structural vocabulary (reinforced concrete, ribbon windows, stripped surfaces) with the programmatic need to represent state authority — a compromise typical of the emerging Polish state of the 1920s–30s that wanted modernity without avant-garde provocation.
