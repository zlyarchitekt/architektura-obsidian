## Overview

Romea Muryń is a Polish architect and researcher whose work addresses the intersection of spatial planning, land ownership structures, and rural development in Poland. Her 2022 article "Modele zawłaszczania ziemi" (*Kwartalnik RZUT*) provides a data-driven analysis of post-1989 agricultural land privatization and its spatial consequences for Polish rural settlements. She argues for land as a common good requiring public stewardship, not a market asset subject to speculative accumulation.

Jej artykuł z 2022 r. *Modele zawłaszczania ziemi* (*Kwartalnik RZUT*) dostarcza opartej na danych analizy prywatyzacji ziemi rolniczej po 1989 r. i jej przestrzennych konsekwencji dla polskich osiedli wiejskich.

## Key Works / Projects

- **"Modele zawłaszczania ziemi"** (2022) — *Kwartalnik RZUT*; analysis of three phases of Polish agricultural land transformation (PRL nationalization, post-1989 selloff, foreign capital consolidation), comparative spatial analysis of three village typologies, and proposal for a new agrarian reform.

## Ideas & Influences

Muryń draws on Saskia Sassen's global framework for land commodification (*Expulsions*, 2014) to ground her Poland-specific analysis in a wider political-economic context. Her core argument is that post-1989 privatization did not break from the logic of PRL centralization — it merely transferred land control from the state to private (increasingly foreign) capital, severing local communities from influence over their surrounding space in both cases.

Her proposed reform — state land buyback, flexible lease model separating use-rights from ownership, regional decentralisation of land management — is positioned against neoliberal dogma while explicitly distancing itself from PRL-style renationalization. The lease model is the key instrument: by removing land from speculative ownership circuits, it lowers entry costs for small farms and redirects land-value returns to the public sector for reinvestment in social infrastructure.

**PL:** Muryń opiera się na globalnej ramie Saskii Sassen dotyczącej utowarowienia ziemi (*Expulsions*, 2014), osadzając swoją polską analizę w szerszym kontekście polityczno-ekonomicznym. Jej kluczowym argumentem jest to, że prywatyzacja po 1989 r. nie zerwała z logiką centralnego zarządzania PRL — jedynie przeniosła kontrolę nad ziemią z państwa na prywatny (coraz częściej zagraniczny) kapitał, odrywając w obu przypadkach lokalne społeczności od wpływu na kształtowanie przestrzeni.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The article focuses exclusively on agricultural land, leaving urban land commodification (which intersects with [[Warunki Zabudowy]] and [[Urban Sprawl]]) unaddressed — a significant gap given how closely related the two phenomena are in Polish post-transformation space.
- Her proposed reform lacks implementation mechanics for the transition from current KOWR centralization to a regional/local management structure.
