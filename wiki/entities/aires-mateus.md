## Overview

Aires Mateus is a Portuguese architectural practice founded by brothers Francisco and Manuel Aires Mateus in 1988. Known for severe white volumes, tectonic precision, and conceptual clarity, their work often reinterprets historical precedents through minimal means. The Bom Successo Group in Óbidos (2003) explicitly references the Dutch De Stijl movement: the ground plan evokes Theo van Doesburg's compositions, while the roof pattern recalls Piet Mondrian's Neo-Plasticist pictures. The result is a megalithic, almost stone-age reading of modernist geometry.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Bom Successo Group, Óbidos** (2003) — 19 De Stijl–inflected houses with Mondrian-like roof pattern.
- **House in Alvalade** — precedent for the square ground plan used at Bom Successo.
