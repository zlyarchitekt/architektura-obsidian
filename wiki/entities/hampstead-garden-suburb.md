## Overview

Hampstead Garden Suburb is a garden suburb in North London designed by Raymond Unwin from 1905 onwards. Founded by Henrietta Barnett and governed by the Hampstead Garden Suburb Trust (established 1904), the project aimed to create high-standard housing affordable to all social classes. Covering 243 acres, the suburb features strict planning regulations defining maximum dwellings per acre, street dimensions, and minimum room sizes. The site is divided into geometrically varied sub-units, with intensive green areas integrated to avoid uniform streetscapes. A symbolic town gate marks the boundary between nature and urbanity. Multiple architects contributed to the design, producing an ensemble of Arts and Crafts cottages, terraces, and larger houses unified by the overarching green network.

## Key Data

- **Area:** 243 acres (approx. 98 ha)
- **Architect:** Raymond Unwin (master planner)
- **Founder:** Henrietta Barnett / Hampstead Garden Suburb Trust
- **Features:** Town gate; maximum density controls; varied sub-units; multiple architects; intensive green network

## Ideas & Influences

- **Garden suburb vs. garden city**: Unlike Letchworth, Hampstead was a suburb attached to London, not a self-contained city — yet it maintained garden-city standards of density and green space.
- **Anti-monotony**: The deliberate geometric variation of sub-units and the involvement of multiple architects were strategies to prevent the visual monotony typical of speculative housing estates.
- **Symbolic enclosure**: The town gate is an explicit architectural gesture marking the threshold between urban chaos and suburban order.

**PL:**
- **Przedmieście ogrodowe vs. miasto-ogród**: W przeciwieństwie do Letchworth, Hampstead było przedmieściem przylegającym do Londynu, a nie samowystarczalnym miastem — zachowało jednak standardy miasta-ogrodu pod względem gęstości i przestrzeni zielonej.
- **Antymonotonia**: Celowa wariacja geometryczna podjednostek i zaangażowanie wielu architektów były strategiami zapobiegającymi wizualnej monotonii typowej dla spekulacyjnych osiedli mieszkaniowych.
- **Symboliczne zamknięcie**: Brama miejska to wyraźny gest architektoniczny oznaczający próg między miejskim chaosem a podmiejskim porządkiem.
