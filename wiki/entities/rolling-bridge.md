## Overview

The Rolling Bridge at Paddington Basin, London is a canal footbridge designed by [[Heatherwick Studio]] in 2002 as part of the Paddington Basin redevelopment. Rather than opening by rising as a lift bridge or swinging as a bascule, it opens by curling into a complete octagonal circle via hydraulic actuators — allowing boats to pass while eliminating the visual and mechanical bulk of conventional movable bridge hardware. The bridge won the BCSA (British Constructional Steelwork Association) Structural Steel Design Award in 2005.

The Rolling Bridge is frequently cited as an early Heatherwick Studio example of kinetic form derived from structural logic rather than applied decoration — the rolling mechanism is the architecture, not a feature added to it.

Rolling Bridge jest często cytowany jako wczesny przykład kinetycznej formy Heatherwick Studio, wyprowadzonej z logiki strukturalnej, a nie nałożonej dekoracji.
