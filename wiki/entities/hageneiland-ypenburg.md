## Overview

Hageneiland is a playful, experimental low-rise housing development on an island in Ypenburg, defined by four bridges and a trapezoid of land framed by car-accessible routes. The plan fills the site with a pattern of colourful apartment buildings in various sizes and garden enclosures. Three parallel pedestrian routes, connected by public areas created by leaving plots undeveloped, cut the site into four uniform strips. Homogeneous blocks of different sizes (containing 1–8 residential units) combined with garden structures give rise to points of transparency. The 37 buildings use five materials: tile, shingles, green or blue synthetic boards, or ribbed aluminium plates. Each house features a single material, lending a determined identity that connects home and occupant.
