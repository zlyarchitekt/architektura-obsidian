## Overview

Cano y Escario Arquitectura is a Spanish firm known for dense, sculptural housing that responds to Mediterranean climate through massing and material. Their Madrid row houses (2003) consist of ten three-storey stone-clad cubes with roof terraces, moulded into a single mobile mass along a curved street. Cut-outs, awnings, and outcroppings follow the street's geometry, while the closed southern façade protects against summer heat. Each 206 m² unit includes seasonally adaptable spaces: courtyard passageway, bedroom terrace, and shadable roof deck.
