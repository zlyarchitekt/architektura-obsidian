## Overview
Bohdan Lachert was a leading figure in the pre-war Polish architectural avant-garde. Frequently collaborating with his partner [[Józef Szanajca]], Lachert championed extreme, purist functionalism, heavily inspired by Le Corbusier and neoplasticism (De Stijl). Their architectural legacy includes some of the most radical and acclaimed "machine-for-living" designs, particularly residential villas resembling transatlantic ships.

**PL:** Bohdan Lachert był czołową postacią przedwojennej polskiej awangardy architektonicznej. Mimo że w Polsce brakowało szerokiego frontu deweloperskiego dla skrajnie lewicowych wizji, jego ścisła współpraca z Józefem Szanajcą zaowocowała jednymi z najbardziej radykalnych i purystycznych, funkcjonalistycznych dzieł ówczesnej Europy Środkowej (wille-transatlantyki). Inspiracja Corbusierem i neoplastycyzmem.

## Key Works / Projects
- **[[Dom Lachertów]]** (1928–1929, Warsaw) — legendary avant-garde application of Le Corbusier's five points.
- **Willa Szyllerów** (1929, Warsaw, demolished) — an extreme functionalist design that fully emulated ship architecture.
- **Dom Avenariusów** (1938, Warsaw)
- **Muranów Południowy** (post-war housing estate built on the rubble of the Warsaw Ghetto, reflecting his socialist leanings post-war).

## Ideas & Influences
Lachert imported the concepts of strict Le Corbusian purism to a conservative Poland. He insisted on the [[Free Plan]], flat roof terraces, unadorned white/grey facades, and the use of raw materials like exposed concrete (beton brut) even before brutalism became a global trend. Lachert, like many of his peers, believed heavily in the social democratization of architecture, but ironically built mainly upscale villas before the war.
