## Overview

Fuinneamh Workshop Architects is an Irish architecture practice. "Fuinneamh" (pronounced roughly _fin-yiv_) is an Irish language word meaning energy, vigour, or liveliness. The name signals both a linguistic and cultural grounding in Irish tradition — a meaningful choice for a practice whose known work engages seriously with Irish vernacular building. The "Workshop" designation suggests a hands-on, craft-oriented approach to practice.

Based on available sources, the practice works at a domestic and rural scale, drawing on the formal and material vocabulary of the Irish vernacular — farmhouse massing, wet dash plaster, drystone, slate — while applying contemporary spatial logic.

## Ideas & Influences

From the single known project, the practice's approach can be characterised by:
- **Disciplined spatial geometry**: the four-square diagram as generator of plan and massing.
- **Vernacular fidelity**: materials and opening rhythms drawn directly from local building tradition.
- **Classical facade logic**: blind windows to maintain rhythm where real openings are not possible — a device that implies awareness of the formal rules of historical Irish rural architecture.
- **Restraint**: no formal invention for its own sake; the interest lies in the rigour of the spatial system and the quality of material execution.

This positions the practice within a tradition of [[Critical Regionalism]] — contemporary architecture that does not imitate vernacular forms naively but engages them as a living discipline.
