## Overview

Dr. Joanna Kusiak is a Polish sociologist and urban researcher at the University of Cambridge, based in Berlin. Her scholarly work focuses on the intersection of law and the city — specifically on how legal instruments, including technical loopholes (*technikalia prawne*), are used to restructure urban space and redistribute resources between communities and capital. She is the author of the doctoral dissertation *Chaos Warszawa*, which analysed how Warsaw's reprywatyzacja (reprivatization) process deployed legal fictions to transfer urban buildings from communities to private business interests. She is simultaneously an activist-researcher (*badaczka w działaniu*) affiliated with the [[Deutsche Wohnen und Co enteignen]] initiative in Berlin, where she serves as one of the campaign's public faces and as a member of its constitutional law working group (*Vergesellschaftung AG*).

Jej praca koncentruje się na przecięciu prawa i miasta — jak instrumenty prawne służą do restrukturyzacji przestrzeni miejskiej i redystrybucji zasobów. ## Key Works / Projects

- ***Chaos Warszawa*** (doctoral dissertation) — analysis of Warsaw's reprywatyzacja process; shows how technical legal arguments (*technikalia prawne*) and "legal fictions" were used to expropriate urban communities and transfer buildings to reprywatyzacja businessmen; establishes the framework for thinking about law as a redistributive instrument in urban space.
- **"Uspołeczniamy Berlin"** (interview, Kwartalnik RZUT, 2022-09-15) — her analysis of the DWE initiative: the Vergesellschaftung / Enteignung distinction, the Vienna model, the cost dispute, and the campaign's trajectory through autumn 2022.

## Ideas & Influences

**Law as instrument of urban redistribution:** Kusiak's central methodological insight is that law is not a neutral framework but an instrument deployable in either direction — to extract resources from communities (Warsaw reprywatyzacja) or to restore public control over housing (Berlin Vergesellschaftung). The creative reading of Article 15 GG — which had never been invoked in the Federal Republic's history — exemplifies what she calls "radical legality" (*radykalna praworządność*): using the constitutional order itself, rather than protest, as the vehicle for structural change.

**Inversion of Warsaw's reprywatyzacja:** The DWE project is, structurally, the mirror image of what Kusiak analysed in Warsaw: there, private interests used legal engineering to extract public assets into private hands; here, the aim is to use legal engineering to return assets to public/community ownership. The inversion is explicit in her account: "I wrote about this topic before they appeared — they fell from the sky as the realisation of the ambition my project had set out to investigate."

**Action research:** Her move from researcher to activist in DWE is framed as a methodological step: "my research became action research." She entered the movement as a researcher, attended internal working group meetings (*Vergesellschaftung AG*), and concluded that research alone was insufficient — engagement was required. This positions her as a participant in the phenomena she studies, with attendant advantages (access, inside knowledge) and risks (loss of critical distance).

**Land as political infrastructure:** Kusiak's formulation of the Vienna model's lesson generalizes into a theory: "Every city is simply land — a collection of plots. If we want to govern any city, we must have control over that land." Selling public land is selling the capacity for political agency over urban development — not just losing an asset but losing the infrastructure of governance itself.

**PL:** *Prawo jako instrument redystrybucji miejskiej:* Prawo to nie neutralna rama, lecz instrument działający w obie strony. Twórcza lektura art. 15 GG jako "radykalna praworządność": zmiana strukturalna przez system prawny, nie przez protest. *Odwrócenie reprywatyzacji:* DWE to strukturalne lustrzane odbicie reprywatyzacji warszawskiej — tu engineering prawny przywraca zasoby do własności publicznej. *Badanie w działaniu:* badaczka → aktywistka jako krok metodologiczny. *Ziemia jako infrastruktura polityczna:* sprzedaż publicznej ziemi = sprzedaż zdolności do uprawiania polityki miejskiej.
