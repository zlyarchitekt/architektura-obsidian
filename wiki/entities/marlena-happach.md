## Overview

Marlena Happach is the Architect of the City of Warsaw (*Architektka Miasta Stołecznego Warszawy*) and director of the [[BAiPP|Biuro Architektury i Planowania Przestrzennego]] (BAiPP) — Warsaw's official planning and architecture office. She has led the preparation of Warsaw's new Studium Uwarunkowań i Kierunków Zagospodarowania Przestrzennego ([[SUiKZP]]) from 2018 onwards, overseeing its entire analytical, design, and consultation process. Her approach is explicitly climate-first: the Studium under her leadership replaced the aesthetic preoccupations of its 2006 predecessor with a structural commitment to ecological resilience, compact urban form, [[Blue-Green Infrastructure]], and social equity.

**PL:** Marlena Happach jest Architektką Miasta Stołecznego Warszawy i dyrektorką Biura Architektury i Planowania Przestrzennego (BAiPP). Kieruje przygotowaniem nowego Studium Warszawy od 2018 r. Jej podejście jest wyraźnie klimatyczne: nowe Studium pod jej kierownictwem zastąpiło estetyczne priorytety poprzednika z 2006 r. zobowiązaniem do odporności ekologicznej, zwartej formy miejskiej i równości społecznej.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Nowe Studium Uwarunkowań i Kierunków Zagospodarowania Przestrzennego Warszawy** (2018–2022/2023) — Warsaw's new 30-year spatial strategic document. Climate-first; city as ecosystem; BZIW as structural backbone; compact polycentric city; 50+ post-WWII buildings identified for heritage protection; 4 new cultural parks proposed.

## Ideas & Influences

Happach frames the Studium as a **"spatial constitution"** — an instrument whose value lies not in its legal formality but in the values it encodes and the long-horizon vision it commits the city to:

**Climate primacy**: "There is no planet B, there is no Warsaw B." Climate resilience (sponge city, BZIW, air ventilation corridors) takes precedence over visual axes and monumental set-pieces. The shift from "spectacular squares" to "climate axes" is her defining reorientation of Warsaw's planning culture.

**Ecosystem thinking**: Warsaw is treated as an integrated ecosystem in which social, economic, and natural spheres are inseparable; planning decisions in one sphere affect all others. The concept connects to the broader urban ecology discourse of the Third Landscape ([[Gilles Clément]]) and biophilic design.

**Demographic precision**: Real population data from mobile phone tracking (1.97M) vs. registry data (1.79M); planning must account for demographic volatility — the Ukraine refugee surge compressed decades of projections into months. The *bilans* (land-capacity accounting) is the methodological tool.

**Market ambivalence**: Happach praises the ecological and social quality of major developer-led mixed-use regeneration projects (Koneser, Browary Warszawskie, Elektrownia Powiśle, Fabryka Norblina) while acknowledging that "the invisible hand of the market is very visible in Warsaw" and that Supersam and other modernist landmarks were lost to market pressure. The tension is named, not resolved.

**Heritage advocacy**: Identifies 50+ post-WWII buildings for protection in the new Studium; calls for 4 new cultural parks on both Vistula banks; argues the Studium can stem the growing trend of demolishing 1990s and early 2000s buildings for larger, optimised replacements.

**PL:** Happach formułuje Studium jako "konstytucję przestrzenną". Kluczowe tezy: klimat przed estetyką ("osie klimatyczne, nie widokowe"); Warszawa jako ekosystem; dane demograficzne z telefonii; bilans terenów; ambiwalencja wobec rynku; ochrona dziedzictwa powojennego i posttransformacyjnego.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The same interview praises developer-led regeneration and laments market-driven demolitions — a tension Happach names but does not resolve.
- The Studium was prepared simultaneously with a new planning act requiring its conversion into a *plan ogólny* — Happach expresses confidence the vision will survive, but the legal and format implications are uncertain.
- Her use of "ecosystem" is largely metaphorical in the interview; whether BAiPP's planning tools deliver on the ecological integration the metaphor implies is not addressed.
