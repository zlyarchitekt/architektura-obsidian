## Overview
A Polish feminist-queer activist group known for high-visibility spatial interventions in Warsaw. In July 2020, members of the group draped rainbow flags over major Warsaw monuments — including the Nicolaus Copernicus monument, the Józef Piłsudski monument, the Warsaw Mermaid, and the Christ figure on Krakowskie Przedmieście — accompanied by the slogan "To szturm! To atak! To tęcza! Pamięci poległych w walce z nienawiścią." Prosecutors dropped the case, effectively acknowledging that the act did not constitute desecration.

To atak! To tęcza! Pamięci poległych w walce z nienawiścią." Prokuratura umorzyła sprawę, w praktyce uznając, że czyn nie stanowi znieważenia.*

## Key Works / Projects
- **Rainbow flags on Warsaw monuments** (July 2020) — spatial intervention claiming national heritage symbols for LGBTQ+ visibility.

## Ideas & Influences
The group operates in the tradition of queer direct action that treats public monuments not as sacred objects but as contested surfaces. Their strategy echoes Sara Andron's "surface commons" argument: vertical urban surfaces belong to the public and can be legitimately re-coded by citizens.
