## Overview

Dame Zaha Hadid (1950 Baghdad – 2016 Miami) was an Iraqi-British architect and founder of Zaha Hadid Architects (ZHA). She was the first woman to receive the Pritzker Architecture Prize (2004) and the first woman to be individually named as recipient of the RIBA Gold Medal (2016; the medal has been awarded since 1848). Known for radically fluid, curvilinear forms described as "landscape urbanism" or late deconstructivism, her work evolved from near-unbuildable paper architecture of the 1980s into a large global practice enabled by parametric design software. She died suddenly of a cardiac arrest in March 2016 — Kapusta: "an era ended."

**PL:** Dame Zaha Hadid (1950 Bagdad – 2016 Miami) była iracko-brytyjską architektką i założycielką Zaha Hadid Architects (ZHA). Pierwsza kobieta z nagrodą Pritzkera (2004) i pierwsza indywidualnie wyróżniona kobieta z Złotym Medalem RIBA (2016; medal przyznawany od 1848 roku). Znana z radykalnie płynnych, krzywoliniowych form, jej twórczość ewoluowała od papierowej architektury lat 80. do dużej globalnej pracowni wspieranej oprogramowaniem parametrycznym. Zmarła nagle na atak serca w marcu 2016 — Kapusta: „dobiegła kresu pewna epoka."

## Ideas & Influences

Hadid trained at the Architectural Association in London, then worked briefly under Rem Koolhaas at OMA before founding her own practice in 1980. Her early reputation rested almost entirely on architectural drawings — technically extraordinary but largely unbuildable with 1980s technology. The parametric revolution of the 1990s–2000s changed this: software tools capable of generating and structurally rationalising doubly-curved surfaces made her vocabulary finally buildable.

The Pritzker jury's 2004 citation acknowledged she was famous primarily for drawings when few buildings existed; this was a calculated bet on a trajectory, not a reward for a body of built work. The bet proved correct: in the decade after 2004, ZHA produced an extraordinary range of major public buildings across three continents.

Scholars from Middle Eastern architectural backgrounds have noted — particularly since her death — that the organic biomorphic forms of her late work draw significantly on Arabic calligraphic traditions: stroke, flow, and surface as carriers of meaning. This frames her work differently from the "pure formal experimentation" of European critical reception.

Hadid was among the 19,000 signatories of the 2013 Denise Scott Brown Pritzker petition, supporting DSB's recognition alongside Venturi.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- **Heydar Aliyev**: critics argue Hadid promoted an authoritarian namesake; Hadid and defenders argued the building was an opportunity for architectural expression in her cultural home region.
- **Al Wakrah Qatar**: the BBC interview attributed all Qatari construction deaths to her project — an unfair charge — but her general dismissiveness toward the conditions of migrant workers on her sites was documented.
- **Arabic calligraphy reading**: compelling but retrospective; Hadid herself did not consistently articulate this frame.
- **Pritzker 2004 timing**: whether awarding the prize before a major body of built work was appropriate was debated; in retrospect the trajectory it predicted was correct.
- **ZHA after Hadid's death**: the firm continues under Patrik Schumacher; whether the work maintains comparable quality and distinctiveness is contested.

- Heydar Aliyev: promocja autorytarnego imienia vs. ekspresja architektoniczna w kulturowym regionie korzeni.
- Al Wakrah: atrybucja wszystkich katarskich śmierci jej projektowi — niesprawiedliwa, ale jej ogólna bagatelizacja warunków pracy migrantów była udokumentowana.
