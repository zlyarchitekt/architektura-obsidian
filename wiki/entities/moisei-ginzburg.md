## Overview
Russian Constructivist architect and theorist, co-designer of the Narkomfin Communal House in Moscow (1930) with Ignaty Milinis. The building was intended as a "social condensator" that reduced private space and expanded collective facilities, including a communal canteen that replaced private kitchens entirely.

**PL:** *Rosyjski architekt konstruktywistyczny i teoretyk, współprojektant domu komunalnego Narkomfin w Moskwie (1930) wraz z Ignatym Milinisem. Budynek miał być „społecznym kondensatorem", który ogranicza przestrzenie prywatne i rozbudowuje kolektywne zaplecze, w tym stołówkę zastępującą prywatne kuchnie.*

## Key Works / Projects
- **Narkomfin Communal House** (1930, Moscow, with Ignaty Milinis) — prestige modernist housing for Ministry of Finance officials; double-height apartments, glazed gallery corridors, walkways between blocks; communal canteen replacing private kitchens.

## Ideas & Influences
Ginzburg applied Constructivist principles to daily life: architecture could engineer social relations by reorganising domestic space. The abolition of the private kitchen was a radical feminist gesture (freeing women from domestic labour) embedded in a broader collectivist programme.
