## Overview
An open-air amphitheater made of concrete platforms and blocks, located on an artificial hill inside [[Osiedle Słowackiego (Lublin)]]. Designed by [[Oskar Hansen]] and [[Zofia Hansen]]. 

**PL:** *Plenerowy amfiteatr z betonowych platform i kubików, położony na sztucznym wzniesieniu na Osiedlu Słowackiego w Lublinie. Zaprojektowany przez Oskara i Zofię Hansenów.*

## Key Works / Projects

## Ideas & Influences
Perhaps the most literal spatial translation of the [[Open Form]] theory: it is utterly unprogrammed. It was built as a flexible stage, a monument to Juliusz Słowacki, and an obstacle course that frames its users—mothers, children, residents—as the true "works of art."

## In the Sources
- Featured in [[rzut-powojenny-modernizm]] as a space under extreme threat from modern safety regulations. Its stairs are arranged in a delicate curve with non-standard step heights. It lacks handrails and safety barriers by design. Conforming it to modern accessibility and safety codes (adding ramps, blocking off platforms with standard 1.1m rails) would violently destroy the sculptural, open nature of the object. 

## Contradictions / Open Questions
The tension between modern accessibility/safety standards (which are fundamentally good for users) and the preservation of an abstract, risky, mid-century sculptural space. Can a space be "safe" while remaining an "Open Form"?
