## Overview

The Garden Bridge was a proposed pedestrian crossing over the River Thames in central London, designed by [[Heatherwick Studio]] and championed by actress Joanna Lumley, officially proposed in 2013 and formally cancelled on 14 August 2017. The design envisioned a pedestrian bridge planted as a woodland garden park, linking the south and north banks. It was originally proposed as entirely privately financed; by 2016, Mayor Sadiq Khan had frozen public funding after £60M of public money had been committed — including Transport for London contributions to strengthen Temple tube station to bear the north abutment load. An independent review commissioned by the Mayor concluded that the £46M of public money already spent was irrecoverably lost, and that proceeding risked further cost with uncertain benefit.

The Garden Bridge is the most significant governance controversy in Heatherwick's career. In 2017 it emerged that Heatherwick was the sole founding member of the Garden Bridge Trust and had attended eight trustee meetings, despite having repeatedly and publicly denied being part of the trust. He had also attended a secret fundraising meeting in California with Apple and then-Mayor Boris Johnson in 2013, before the design contract had been officially awarded. The Hodge Report (2017), commissioned by the Mayor, concluded that the appointments of Heatherwick Studio as designer and [[Arup]] as engineers "were not open, fair or competitive... and revealed systematic failures and ineffective control systems."

- The full £46M of public money is irrecoverably lost with no public benefit delivered; no individual was held accountable beyond the project cancellation.
- Heatherwick's repeated public denials of trust membership, later contradicted by documentary evidence, were not subject to further investigation in the sources.
