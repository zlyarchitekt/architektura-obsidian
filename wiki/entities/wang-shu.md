## Overview
Wang Shu is a Chinese architect and dean of the School of Architecture of the China Academy of Art. His practice, Amateur Architecture Studio (co-founded with his wife [[Lu Wenyu]]), is known for its critical regionalism, emphasizing traditional craftsmanship, the reuse of salvaged materials, and sensitivity to site and history in the face of China's rapid urbanization. He won the Pritzker Architecture Prize in 2012.

**PL:** Wang Shu to chiński architekt, dziekan w Chińskiej Akademii Sztuki. Jego praktyka, Amateur Architecture Studio (współzałożona z żoną Lu Wenyu), znana jest z krytycznego regionalizmu, kładącego nacisk na tradycyjne rzemiosło, ponowne wykorzystanie odzyskanych materiałów oraz wrażliwość na miejsce w obliczu gwałtownej urbanizacji Chin. Laureat Nagrody Pritzkera w 2012 r.

## Key Works / Projects
- **Ningbo Museum** (2008, Ningbo) — famous for combining modern monolithic forms with facades built from recycled rubble, bricks, and tiles from demolished local villages.
- **Xiangshan Campus, China Academy of Art** (Hangzhou)

## Ideas & Influences
Wang's work stands in direct opposition to the generic, tabula rasa modernization of Chinese cities and the importation of Western [[Starchitecture]]. He emphasizes the continuity of memory by literally embedding the ruins of the past (demolished traditional housing) into new, monumental civic structures.
