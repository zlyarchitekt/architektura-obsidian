## Overview

Piotr Uściński is a Polish politician who served as Secretary of State at the Ministry of Development and Technology (*Ministerstwo Rozwoju i Technologii*) in the PiS-coalition government. A member of parliament in the VIII and IX terms of the Sejm, and formerly Starost of Wołomin County (until 2014). He was the key government figure driving the 2022 reform of the Polish Spatial Planning Act — the *ustawa o planowaniu i zagospodarowaniu przestrzennym*.

**PL:** Piotr Uściński to polski polityk, Sekretarz Stanu w Ministerstwie Rozwoju i Technologii w rządzie koalicji PiS. Poseł na Sejm VIII i IX kadencji, wcześniej starosta wołomiński (do 2014 r.). Był kluczową postacią rządową odpowiedzialną za reformę ustawy o planowaniu i zagospodarowaniu przestrzennym z 2022 roku.

## Key Works / Projects

- **Reforma planowania przestrzennego (2022)** — driving the amendment replacing *studium* with *plan ogólny* as the primary spatial planning instrument at municipal level; mandating adoption by all municipalities by 2026; restricting WZ decisions to designated infill zones.

## Ideas & Influences

Uściński takes an evolutionary rather than revolutionary approach to planning reform. He explicitly opposes what he calls "ideological" positions against single-family housing or suburban growth, framing planning primarily in terms of civic freedom and local community needs rather than environmental or economic efficiency arguments. He acknowledges the 84.3 billion PLN/year cost of spatial chaos (PAN) but argues the solution is better plans, not restrictions on housing choice.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

His defence of suburban sprawl as "natural" sits in tension with his own government's published spatial chaos cost data. His optimism about reform implementation may not survive the change of government after October 2023. His position on WZ — preserving it as an instrument while limiting its scope — may prove insufficient in practice.
