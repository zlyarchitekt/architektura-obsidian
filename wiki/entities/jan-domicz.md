## Overview

Jan Domicz is a Polish visual artist and tutor, son of architects Antoni and Małgorzata Domicz. His works have been exhibited in Berlin, Basel, Venice, and Vienna. He joined OSSA (Ogólnopolskie Spotkania Studentów Architektury) as a tutor in 2015, forming a father-son tutoring duo with his father Antoni. Jan argues that short, intensive workshops align the "tempo" of tutors and students in a way that long-term university teaching does not. He believes that when students see a tutor more engaged than they are, it creates an irresistible passion. He also critiques Polish architecture schools for employing too few practising architects, noting that at the Academy of Fine Arts in Poznań (where he studied intermedia), the faculty consisted almost entirely of practitioners — a stark contrast to architecture faculties.

## Ideas & Influences

- **Tempo alignment**: Short workshops align tutor and student energy in a way that long-term teaching cannot.
- **Passion as pedagogy**: A tutor's visible passion is contagious and creates a feedback loop of engagement.
- **Practitioner faculty**: Architecture schools should employ practising architects, not just academics.

**PL:**
- **Wyrównanie tempa**: Krótkie warsztaty wyrównują energię tutora i studenta w sposób, którego długoterminowe nauczanie nie potrafi.
- **Pasja jako pedagogika**: Widoczna pasja tutora jest zaraźliwa i tworzy pętlę zwrotną zaangażowania.
- **Wydział praktyków**: Szkoły architektury powinny zatrudniać praktykujących architektów, a nie tylko akademików.
