## Overview

Enver Hoxha (1908–1985) was the communist dictator of Albania from 1944 until his death, ruling as First Secretary of the Albanian Party of Labour. His dictatorship produced one of the most extreme examples of spatial totalitarianism in Cold War Europe: Projekti Bunkerizimit, a programme that covered Albania's entire landscape — beaches, city centres, rural farmland, mountain passes — with between 100,000 and 750,000 reinforced concrete bunkers, beginning in the 1970s. Albania under Hoxha pursued total international isolation, breaking successively with Yugoslavia, the Soviet Union, and China, making self-sufficiency in national defence an obsession. Hoxha's portrait of himself as protector of a besieged nation was the ideological engine of the bunkerization programme. He died in 1985; the communist system fell in 1991.
