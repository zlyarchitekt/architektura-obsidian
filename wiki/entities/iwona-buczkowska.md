## Overview
A Polish-born architect who practices in France. She is primarily known for designing one of the largest and most innovative wooden housing estates in Europe in the 1980s.

**PL:** *Architektka polskiego pochodzenia praktykująca we Francji. Znana przede wszystkim jako projektantka jednego z największych i najbardziej innowacyjnych drewnianych osiedli mieszkaniowych w Europie z lat 80.*

## Key Works / Projects
- **Le Blanc-Mesnil (Pièce Pointue)** (near Paris, 1980s) — an ecological, social, all-timber housing estate with over 300 non-repetitive apartments based on triangular geometries.

## Ideas & Influences
Characterized by a bottom-up, human-centric design methodology — starting from the interior experience of a single apartment and scaling up to the urban form, the reverse of standard architectural training. Her work heavily emphasizes social interaction channels (small bridges, nooks, shared community building) over rigid functionalist separation. The first eight houses were built as prototypes to test spatial behaviour before full-scale rollout — a practice rare in contemporary housing development.

## In the Sources
- Discussed extensively as an undiscovered icon of female-led architecture that anticipated post-pandemic housing needs by creating rich local social spaces in [[rzut-bal-architektoniczki]]. The updated source adds: the prototype methodology (8 test houses), the fully triangular non-repetitive apartment layouts, the community centre on site, and the petition by Jean Nouvel and Renzo Piano that saved the estate from demolition.
