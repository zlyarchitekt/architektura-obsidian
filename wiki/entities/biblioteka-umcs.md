## Overview
The Main Library of the Maria Curie-Skłodowska University in Lublin, completed in 1967. Designed by [[Tadeusz Witkowski]]. The composition of its main block resembles a giant, abstract bookshelf.

**PL:** *Biblioteka Główna UMCS w Lublinie, ukończona w 1967 r. Zaprojektowana przez Tadeusza Witkowskiego. Kompozycja jej głównej bryły przypomina gigantyczny, abstrakcyjny regał na książki.*

## Ideas & Influences
Celebrated for its varied plaster texturing (smooth white vs. rough grey), deep window reveals that give the facade a strong tectonic rhythm, and a meticulous interior utilizing ceramics, stone, and distinctive staircase geometries.

## In the Sources
- Documented in [[rzut-powojenny-modernizm]] as a near-victim of careless [[Thermomodernization]]. The original 2017 thermal upgrade plan would have ruined the facade's proportions by flush-mounting windows against thick insulation (causing a "swollen" look). Interventions by local activists forced a revision: keeping the deep window profiles, matching original plaster textures, and preserving the interior woodwork. However, fire-safety codes still mandated the awkward insertion of oversized smoke-extraction windows that disrupted the "bookshelf" rhythm.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
Despite its iconic status in Lublin and high state of preservation, it remains inexplicably absent from the formal Register of Monuments, severely hampering efforts to protect it from invasive technical retrofits.
