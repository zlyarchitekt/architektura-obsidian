## Overview
Juliusz Żórawski was one of interwar Poland’s most brilliant modernists, highly regarded for his luxurious and impeccably detailed apartment buildings in Warsaw. He perfectly synthesized Le Corbusier’s radical architectural principles with top-tier materials and elite residential demands, culminating in some of the most sophisticated examples of interwar functionalism in Europe.

**PL:** Juliusz Żórawski uznawany jest za jednego z najwybitniejszych polskich modernistów dwudziestolecia międzywojennego, słynącego z bezbłędnie wykreślonych, luksusowych i nowatorskich apartamentowców mieszkalnych. Bezbłędnie integrował radykalne postulaty Le Corbusiera (5 zasad) z ekskluzywnymi materiałami, tworząc elitarne "maszyny do mieszkania".

## Key Works / Projects
- **[[Szklany Dom]]** (1938–1941, Warsaw) — broadly considered the absolute synthesis of Polish Corbusierism, featuring sprawling roof terraces, underground parking, and vast horizontal ribbon windows.
- **Kamienica Jana Wedla** (1938, Warsaw)
- **Apartment Building "Dom pod żaglem"** (1937, Warsaw) — fully realized Le Corbusier's 5 points.

## Ideas & Influences
Żórawski translated the raw, abstract avant-garde of the mid-1920s into a palatable, highly elegant luxury modernism for Warsaw's upper class. He proved that strict functionalism, skeletal concrete frameworks, and ribbon windows did not preclude high architectural luxury or comfort.
