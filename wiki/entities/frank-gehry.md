## Overview

Frank Owen Gehry (born 1929, Toronto) is a Canadian-American architect whose Guggenheim Bilbao (1997) became the defining moment of the starchitect era. Known for sculptural, formally complex buildings clad in titanium and stainless steel, Gehry works from physical models and sketch — not from theoretical writing or digital-first methods — yet his studio was among the first in architecture to adopt CATIA (aerospace software) to translate physical models into buildable digital specifications. Philip Johnson, at the Bilbao opening, called him "the greatest living architect." He received the Pritzker Prize in 1989 — eight years before Bilbao.

Philip Johnson w Bilbao nazwał go „największym żyjącym architektem." Pritzker 1989 — osiem lat przed Bilbao.

## Ideas & Influences

Gehry is the starchitect most resistant to theoretical self-description. His stated inspirations are material and bodily: the fish form (recurring motif from the 1980s, predating Bilbao; he acknowledges it), the movement and gleam of fish, the dynamics of water. He explained the titanium cladding on Bilbao as a happy accident: a market fluctuation at the time of construction made titanium cheaper than stainless steel — a decision as pragmatic as aesthetic.

His studio's adoption of CATIA is significant: the software, designed for aeronautical manufacturing, allowed Gehry's complex hand-modelled forms to be disaggregated into manufacturable panels and structural members. It enabled the actual construction of buildings that previously could only be approximated. However, Gehry himself does not operate CATIA; his design process remains analogue.

The "Bilbao effect" generated a paradox: clients began commissioning "a Frank Gehry building" — expecting a pre-existing signature form — rather than a genuine response to a brief. Gehry found this constraining and said so publicly in 2005.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The fish-form inspiration is well-documented and Gehry-acknowledged; other interpretations (body dynamics, Monroe's skirt, artichoke flower) cited by critics are speculation, as Kapusta notes.
- CATIA adoption was pioneering, but Gehry himself is analogue — the credit split between Gehry's formal vision and his technologists' parametric execution is an open question in the field.
- Guggenheim Abu Dhabi on Saadiyat Island: a major commission in an authoritarian patronage context (UAE), ongoing after 20 years. Part of the broader ethical controversy about Western starchitects and Gulf commissions.

- Inspiracja formą ryby dobrze udokumentowana i przyznana przez Gehry'ego; inne interpretacje to spekulacja krytyków.
