## Overview

Coal Drops Yard is a public space and retail destination in King's Cross, London, completed in October 2018 by [[Heatherwick Studio]] as part of the wider King's Cross regeneration programme by Argent LLP and King's Cross Central Limited Partnership. The project converted two brick freight buildings constructed in 1850 — originally used to receive and store coal freight arriving from northern England — into a contemporary retail and public destination.

The defining design move is the treatment of the rooflines: the two original Victorian pitched roofs are stretched and extended toward each other until they meet and merge at a central "kissing" point, creating an additional storey above and a dramatic covered linear space below. The stretched canopy shelters an open yard used for events and markets, while the new third level commands views over King's Cross station, the Francis Crick Institute, and Cubitt Square. Planning permission was granted in December 2015.

## Key Facts

- **Location**: King's Cross, London, UK
- **Completed**: October 2018
- **Commission**: 2014, King's Cross Central Limited Partnership (KCCLP)
- **Construction began**: early 2016
- **Planning approved**: December 2015
- **Designer**: [[Heatherwick Studio]]
- **Developer**: King's Cross Central Limited Partnership (KCCLP) / Argent LLP
- **Original use**: 1850s coal freight railway buildings (received coal from Northern England, distributed around London by barge and cart)
- **Heritage status**: Victorian ornate cast iron and brick; previously sealed-off infrastructure, never fully public
- **Design key move**: two Victorian gabled roofs extended with new flowing steel canopies, rising toward each other until they touch — forms entirely new floating third storey
- **Structural logic**: new steel structure entirely freestanding, threaded through historic fabric; Victorian walls bear no new load
- **Programme**: ~60 retail units (160–20,000+ sq ft); restaurants, bars; permeable public yard + routes
- **Studio milestone**: first major building completion in London for Heatherwick Studio
