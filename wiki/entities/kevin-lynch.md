## Overview

Kevin Lynch was an American urban planner and theorist best known for his 1960 book *The Image of the City*, which introduced the concept of cognitive mapping and the five elements of urban imageability (paths, edges, districts, nodes, landmarks). His work shifted urban design from purely physical or aesthetic criteria toward the perceptual and psychological experience of city inhabitants. Lynch’s influence on architectural pedagogy extends beyond his writings: he guest-taught at the Städtebauliches Institut in Stuttgart, where his fieldwork-oriented approach to understanding urban form left a lasting imprint on the department’s methodology. This lineage is transmitted to Polish architectural education through Gabriela Rembarz, who studied at the same institute and adopted its field-trip-based pedagogy.

## Key Works / Projects

- ***The Image of the City*** (1960) — introduced cognitive mapping, imageability, and the five-element vocabulary of urban perception.
- **Guest lectures, Städtebauliches Institut, Stuttgart** — transmitted a fieldwork-based, perceptual approach to urban design education that influenced subsequent generations of European urbanists.

## Ideas & Influences

Lynch’s core contribution is the idea that a city’s quality is measured not by its objective form but by the clarity and coherence of the mental image it produces in its inhabitants. This perceptual turn has three pedagogical consequences: (1) urban design must be taught through direct experience, not only drawings; (2) the inhabitant (not the architect) is the primary judge of urban quality; and (3) wayfinding, legibility, and spatial memory are legitimate design criteria.

**PL:** Rdzenny wkład Lyncha polega na tezie, że jakość miasta mierzy się nie jego obiektywną formą, ale klarownością i spójnością mentalnego obrazu, jaki wywołuje u mieszkańców. Ten percepcyjny zwrot ma trzy konsekwencje pedagogiczne: (1) urbanistykę trzeba uczyć przez bezpośrednie doświadczenie, nie tylko rysunki; (2) mieszkaniec (nie architekt) jest podstawowym sędzią jakości miejskiej; (3) nawigacja, czytelność i pamięć przestrzenna są uzasadnionymi kryteriami projektowymi.
