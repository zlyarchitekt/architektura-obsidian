## Definition
A form of procrastination driven by emotional dysregulation, anxiety, or the feeling of "not being good enough yet." In professional contexts, it disproportionately affects women due to culturally ingrained impostor syndrome, leading them to delay publishing or promoting their work until it is "perfect".

**PL:** *Forma prokrastynacji napędzana rozregulowaniem emocjonalnym, niepokojem lub poczuciem, że „nie jest się jeszcze wystarczająco dobrym”. W kontekstach zawodowych nieproporcjonalnie dotyka kobiet z powodu kulturowo uwarunkowanego syndromu oszusta, skłaniając je do opóźniania publikacji czy promocji swojej pracy do czasu, aż będzie „idealna”.*

## Historical Context
Often compounded by biological factors (hormonal fluctuations: PMS, pregnancy, post-partum depression, perimenopause, menopause) but heavily reinforced by gendered socialization that demands modesty and perfection from women, while allowing men to comfortably self-promote even imperfect, half-finished ideas. In the architecture profession it is amplified by the unequal burden of care work, which leaves women with no time for networking, publishing, or updating portfolios — activities that male colleagues treat as a 50% share of professional labour.

## Critiques & Alternatives
Recognition of the condition helps women structurally counteract it, moving to a model where self-promotion (networking, publishing) takes equal priority with the actual design work. Male mentors' advice — "spend 50% of your time on design, 50% on conferences, posts, CV updates and networking" — is rarely passed on to women in the profession.
