## Definition

The **glass ceiling in architecture** refers to the invisible structural barrier that prevents women (and other marginalised groups) from advancing to senior professional roles — practice principals, professors, competition jurors, award laureates — despite their numerical majority in architectural education. The term operationalises a broader sociological concept (the "glass ceiling") for the specific professional and academic context of architecture. In Poland, the glass ceiling is measurable through the **Glass Ceiling Index (GCI)**, the **leaky pipeline** phenomenon, and statistical data on awards, jury composition, and licensing rates.

**PL:** Szklany sufit w architekturze — niewidzialna bariera strukturalna uniemożliwiająca kobietom (i innym wykluczonym grupom) awans do ról seniorskich (właścicielki pracowni, profesorki, jurorki, laureatki nagród) mimo ich liczebnej dominacji w edukacji architektonicznej. Operacjonalizowany przez GCI, dziurawy rurociąg i dane statystyczne o nagrodach, składach jury i uprawnieniach.

## Historical Context

The term "glass ceiling" entered architecture's vocabulary gradually as the feminisation of architectural education became statistically undeniable (Poland: 70%+ women students since the 1990s) while senior parity failed to follow. RIBA (the British institute) issued formal recommendations on gender equality in jury composition in 2003; twenty years later, Polish equivalents (IARP, SARP) have not enacted comparable provisions. The academic glass ceiling in Polish architecture is documented from the founding of Politechnika Wrocławska's architecture faculty in 1948: no female dean for 45 years; no female professor for 48 years.

**PL:** RIBA 2003: zalecenia ws. równości płci w jury — po 20 latach IARP i SARP bez porównywalnych przepisów. WA PWr: 45 lat bez dekanki; 48 lat bez profesorki.

## Key Examples

**Glass Ceiling Index (GCI)** (Eurostat): measures the ratio of women among all academic staff to women among full professors. Score of 1.0 = equality.
- Polish public technical universities (2021/22): **GCI = 1.88**
- Polish architecture faculties: **GCI = 1.29** — best in the technical sector, still above equality.

**Leaky pipeline** (*dziurawy rurociąg*): progressive loss of women at each academic career stage.
- **Flow index** (0–10; 10 = full retention parity):
  - Architecture 2021: **8.45** → 2022: **8.84**

**Polish professional data (2023)**:
- Women: ~45% of IARP licensed architects (below student majority of 70%)
- SARP Honorowa Nagroda (1966–2023): 6 women / 68 men; only 3 independently awarded
- SARP Pałac Saski competition jury: 2 women / 10 men
- Poland: 21st/27 EU states on Gender Equality Index (2022)

**PL:** GCI: uczelnie techniczne 1,88; architektura 1,29. Indeks przepływów 8,84 (2022). Uprawnienia IARP: ~45% kobiet; Honorowa Nagroda SARP: 6/68; jury Pałac Saski: 2/10; PL: 21./27 UE.

## Critiques & Alternatives

The GCI and flow index measure academic pathways only — the barrier in private practice (studio ownership, competition juries, client trust) may be larger and is harder to quantify. The pipeline critique (see [[Feminization of Architecture]]) notes that the glass ceiling operates independently of pipeline size: 50+ years of female majority at WA PWr have not produced senior parity. Intersectional analysis further complicates the glass ceiling frame by showing that class, race, and sexuality compound gender disadvantage in ways the GCI does not capture.

**PL:** GCI mierzy tylko ścieżki akademickie — bariera w praktyce prywatnej (jury, klienci) może być większa i trudniej mierzalna. Pipeline critique: 50+ lat dominacji kobiet na WA PWr bez parytetu władzy. Interssekcjonalność: klasa, rasa, seksualność wzmacniają nierówność płciową poza zasięgiem GCI.
