## Definition

Natureculture (*naturokultura*) designates the sphere in which urban culture and urban nature are not separate domains but mutually constitutive: cultural practices shape how nature is perceived and valued in the city, while urban nature shapes the programmes, vocabularies, and identities of cultural institutions. The concept is used by [[Magdalena Krzoska-Hołody]] (RZUT-33, 2022) to name the interlocking system of cultural institutions and NGOs that are collectively remaking the city's relationship with its [[Blue-Green Infrastructure]] through ecological programming.

**PL:** Naturokultura to sfera, w której miejska kultura i miejska przyroda wzajemnie się konstytuują: praktyki kulturalne kształtują sposób postrzegania i wartościowania przyrody w mieście, a przyroda miejska kształtuje programy, słowniki i tożsamości instytucji kultury. Pojęcia używa Magdalena Krzoska-Hołody (RZUT-33, 2022) dla opisania sieci instytucji kultury i NGO zbiorowo przebudowujących relację miasta z BZIW przez programowanie ekologiczne.

## Historical Context

The *natureculture* concept develops in the wake of a broader disciplinary turn — in science studies (Donna Haraway's companion species work), in landscape architecture (Clément's [[Third Landscape]]), and in critical ecology — toward rejecting nature/culture as an analytic binary. In the Polish urban context, Krzoska-Hołody identifies 2022 as a moment when this turn is arriving in the programming of mainstream cultural institutions: nature walks, community gardens, foraging, bird-watching, and mindfulness in greenery are no longer specialist-institution events but are distributed across neighbourhood cultural centres and local NGOs.

This timing is not accidental: the new Warsaw Studium's explicit commitment to [[Blue-Green Infrastructure]] creates a planning context in which cultural institutions can position themselves as ecological actors. The Studium legitimates the concern; the cultural networks activate it in practice.

**PL:** Pojęcie naturokultura wyrasta z szerszego zwrotu — w studiach nauki (Haraway), architekturze krajobrazu (Clément), ekologii krytycznej — ku odrzuceniu binarności natura/kultura. W polskim kontekście miejskim Krzoska-Hołody identyfikuje rok 2022 jako moment, gdy ten zwrot dociera do głównego nurtu programowania instytucji kulturalnych: spacery przyrodoznawcze, ogrody społecznościowe, zbieranie roślin jadalnych, obserwacje ptaków i mindfulness w zieleni wychodzą poza jednostki specjalistyczne i trafiają do domów kultury i lokalnych NGO.

## Key Examples

- **Warsaw cultural networks (2022)**: Krzoska-Hołody's fieldwork documents 20 events across Targówek, Praga Północ, and other districts — nature walks, community gardens, meditation in greenery, plant identification, foraging, bird-watching. The participating venues include neighbourhood cultural centres, NGOs, and informal groups.
- **Służewski Dom Kultury**: located in the valley of Potok Służewiecki; its ecological programme (Dom Ekologii project) is rooted in the building's landscape setting — an example of architecture-ecology alignment generating a naturokultural identity.
- **Pałacyk Konopackiego (Dom Kultury Praga)**: historic bourgeois house whose room names reference former inhabitants — a model of cultural-memory layering that parallels the ecological memory Krzoska-Hołody documents in natural spaces.
- **Łąki Golędzinowskie protests (late 2022)**: residents mobilised through cultural-nature networks to resist the formalization of wild Vistula-bank meadows into a designed "natural urban park" — a case where natureculture generated planning-relevant collective action.

## Critiques & Alternatives

- The concept risks romanticising the bottom-up: cultural networks are uneven across the city, strongest in already-activated and better-resourced neighbourhoods (Praga, Targówek, Mokotów), potentially weakest where ecological planning deficits are greatest.
- Digital ecology communities (iNaturalist, eBird, Merlin) and social-media naturalist groups perform similar functions to in-person cultural events without requiring institutional infrastructure — the concept may be too institution-centred.
- Krzoska-Hołody's critique of "succession failure" applies to the concept itself: if key individuals leave or institutions lose funding, the naturokultural capital they have built may dissipate without formal recognition in planning processes.
