## Definition
Spatial order. In Polish law, it constitutes the very first statutory "own task" (zadanie własne) of a municipality, established in the 1990 Act on Municipal Self-Government. It represents a broad, systemic harmony of space (*Raumordnung*) rather than just superficial aesthetics.

**PL:** *Ład przestrzenny. W polskim prawie stanowi on absolutnie pierwsze ustawowe „zadanie własne” gminy, ustanowione w ustawie o samorządzie gminnym z 1990 roku. Oznacza głęboką, systemową harmonię przestrzeni (Raumordnung), a nie tylko powierzchowną estetykę.*

## Historical Context
Introduced into the 1990 Act by prof. Jerzy Regulski without an explicit definition, as the legislators deemed the concept self-evident. A statutory definition was only added in the 2003 Spatial Planning Act, leading to a decade where municipalities interpreted it freely.

## Key Proponents
[[Jerzy Regulski]], [[Grzegorz A. Buczek]]

## Key Examples
"Integrated order" (ład zintegrowany) as codified in the 2011 Koncepcja Przestrzennego Zagospodarowania Kraju 2030, showing that spatial order does not inherently conflict with socio-economic investment.

## Critiques & Alternatives
Oftentimes misunderstood by local mayors solely as a matter of aesthetics, which is then used as an excuse to sacrifice spatial integrity in favor of short-term budget gains (e.g., approving a massive distribution center). Furthermore, evaluating municipalities by "spatial order" is glaringly absent from most local government rankings.
