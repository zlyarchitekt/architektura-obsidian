## Definition
A sardonic term for contemporary "nano-playgrounds"—microscopic, heavily fenced areas containing a single piece of standard playground equipment, built purely to satisfy municipal building mandates rather than to provide actual recreational value.

**PL:** *Złośliwe określenie na współczesne "nanoplace" — mikroskopijne, gęsto ogrodzone tereny z pojedynczym urządzeniem, budowane wyłącznie w celu spełnienia wymogów prawnych, a nie w celach rekreacyjnych.*

## Historical Context
In Poland, building regulations mandate the inclusion of a playground for any multi-building residential development. To maximize sellable square footage, developers fulfill this obligation with the absolute minimum legal footprint, surrounding it with cheap panel fencing resulting in an oppressive aesthetic.
