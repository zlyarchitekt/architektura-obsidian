## Definition

Starchitecture (stararchitektura) is the phenomenon of celebrity-architect culture in which a small number of globally recognized architects — "starchitects" — function as cultural brands whose name alone determines the commissioning of major buildings. The building becomes primarily a vehicle for the architect's signature aesthetic rather than a response to place, programme, or community.

**PL:** Stararchitektura to fenomen kultury celebryckiej architekta, w której mała liczba globalnie rozpoznawanych architektów — „stararchitektów" — funkcjonuje jako marki kulturowe, których samo nazwisko determinuje zlecenie prestiżowych budynków. Budynek staje się przede wszystkim wehikułem estetycznej sygnatury architekta, a nie odpowiedzią na miejsce, program czy społeczność.

## Historical Context

The term crystallised around 1997, the year Frank Gehry's Guggenheim Bilbao opened and Herbert Muschamp published his landmark New York Times report on the building. The phenomenon had precursors — I.M. Pei's Louvre Pyramid (1989) was commissioned by Mitterrand without competition, as a signature intervention — but Bilbao gave the starchitect era its mythology: the city transformed by a single iconic building (see [[Bilbao Effect]]).

The cultural conditions enabling starchitecture were multiple: (1) digital fabrication tools (CATIA, originally aerospace software) allowed hitherto unbuildable geometries; (2) the museum expansion wave of the 1990s–2000s, driven by Thomas Krens's Guggenheim franchise model, created demand for architecturally spectacular venues; (3) the neoliberal city-branding logic required images of spectacular modernity; (4) the post-Cold War end of ideological architecture created a vacuum for individual personality.

Ayn Rand's *The Fountainhead* (1943) — whose hero Howard Roark is a thinly veiled Frank Lloyd Wright — provided the founding myth: the visionary architect who refuses compromise, languishes in poverty, but is ultimately vindicated by history. Capitalism needed this figure.

The era's effective end is typically dated to the 2008 financial crisis, which shifted critical and commissioning taste toward contextual, modest, and socially oriented architecture. The Pritzker Prize's subsequent trajectory — Kazuyo Sejima, Carme Pigem, Yvonne Farrell & Shelley McNamara, Anne Lacaton — formalized this shift. The Denise Scott Brown petition (2013), signed by 19,000 including Koolhaas, Hadid, and Venturi, accelerated the reckoning with the starchitect's gender problem.

Poprzednicy istniały — Piramida Luwru Peia (1989) była zlecona przez Mitterranda bez konkursu — ale Bilbao nadało erze stararchitektury mit: miasto przemienione przez jeden ikoniczny budynek (zob. Warunki: (1) narzędzia cyfrowej fabrykacji (CATIA); (2) fala ekspansji muzealnej lat 90.–2000.; (3) neoliberalna logika brandingu miejskiego; (4) próżnia po końcu ideologicznej architektury. Era zakończyła się de facto w 2008 roku.

- **[[Guggenheim Bilbao]]** (1997, Gehry) — the founding moment; titanium; CATIA; Bilbao Effect
- **[[Louvre Pyramid]]** (1989, Pei) — pre-history; Grands Projets; presidential commission
- **[[Casa de Música]]** (2005, OMA/Koolhaas) — "fuck the context"; recycled Dutch house concept
- **[[Copenhill]]** (2019, BIG/Ingels) — next-generation starchitecture: sustainability as spectacle
- **[[Vessel]]** (2019, Heatherwick) — starchitecture in crisis; 4 suicides; closed 2021

## Critiques & Alternatives

**Internal critique**: Rem Koolhaas, perhaps the most prominent starchitect, simultaneously theorised the phenomenon's hollow core: "junk space" (2001) as the built result of the "regime of ¥€$" — a permanent architectural Jacuzzi of the richest clients and most expensive fantasies.

**External critique**: Dejan Sudjic, *The Edifice Complex* (2005), placed Gulf and Chinese starchitecture in a genealogy of authoritarian monuments from Mussolini and Hitler to Saddam. The patronage of Beijing (2008 Olympics), Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and Doha by Western starchitects generated sustained ethical controversy.

**Gender critique**: Zaha Hadid was the first woman Pritzker laureate (2004) and the first individually named RIBA Gold Medal recipient (2016, medal existing since 1848). Denise Scott Brown was excluded when the Pritzker was awarded to Robert Venturi alone in 1991, despite decades of joint authorship.

**Competition critique**: The starchitect competition model privileges "fuck the context" (Koolhaas) over genuine site engagement; short design periods prevent authentic local understanding; the architect's pre-existing formal vocabulary fills the gap.

Alternatives now foregrounded by Pritzker and critical discourse: contextual, socially embedded, modest-budget practice — Lacaton & Vassal, Flores & Prats, Grafton Architects.

**Krytyka wewnętrzna**: Koolhaas jednocześnie teoryzował pustkę fenomenu: „junk space" (2001) jako wybudowany wynik „reżimu ¥€$."
**Krytyka zewnętrzna**: Sudjic *Edifice Complex* (2005) — stararchitektura jako kontynuacja monumentów autorytarnych.
**Krytyka płciowa**: Hadid — pierwsza kobieta Pritzker (2004) i RIBA Gold Medal (2016). Denise Scott Brown wykluczona z nagrody Pritzkera 1991.
**Krytyka konkursów**: „Fuck the context" jako strategia konkursowa; zbyt krótki czas na autentyczne rozpoznanie miejsca.
