## Definition

The **feminization of architecture** describes the structural process by which women have come to constitute the majority of architecture students in many countries — while simultaneously remaining underrepresented at the senior levels of the profession (practice principals, professors, jury members, award laureates). The term names a paradox: numerical dominance in education coexisting with persistent structural disadvantage in professional power, income, recognition, and decision-making. In Poland, architecture is the most feminised technical discipline (70%+ women students for 10+ years), yet women constitute only ~45% of licenced architects (IARP) and received only 6 of 74 SARP Honorowa Nagrody in 57 years.

**PL:** Feminizacja architektury opisuje strukturalny proces, w którym kobiety stają się większością studentek architektury — jednocześnie pozostając niedoreprezentowane na wyższych szczeblach zawodu (właścicielki pracowni, profesorki, składy jurorskie, laureatki nagród). Paradoks: dominacja liczebna w edukacji + trwała nierówność strukturalna w zawodzie. W Polsce architektura to najbardziej sfeminizowana dziedzina techniczna (70%+ studentek), a mimo to kobiety to tylko ~45% uprawnionych architektów IARP.

## Historical Context

In Poland, the process began in 1922 when [[Jadwiga Dobrzyńska]] and [[Wanda Wierzbicka-Nettowa]] became the first women to graduate from Polish architecture schools. By 1971, women outnumbered men for the first time at Politechnika Wrocławska's architecture faculty — a trend that has continued ever since across all Polish architecture faculties. The post-war socialist period formally declared gender equality and state design offices (from 1949) removed the need to compete for commissions, which helped women maintain continuous practice — but no women served in faculty leadership or design office directorships throughout the 1948–1989 period. The contemporary moment (2022–) sees formal equality provisions in place but persistent structural inequality measurable through quantitative indices.

**PL:** 1922: pierwsze dyplomy (Dobrzyńska, Wierzbicka-Nettowa). 1971: kobiety po raz pierwszy stanowią większość na WA PWr. PRL: formalna równość + biura państwowe → ciągłość praktyki bez władzy. 1948–1989: żadnej dekanki ani naczelnej inżynierki w żadnym biurze projektowym. Dziś: formalna równość, mierzalna nierówność strukturalna.

## Key Examples

- **Poland (general)**: 70% women among architecture students; 90% among interior design students (2022). ~45% of IARP licenced architects are women.
- **WA PWr (Politechnika Wrocławska)**: Women majority since 1971; first female dean 1993; first female professor 1996 — 48 years after faculty founding.
- **Wrocław Miastoprojekt (1989)**: 59% female workforce, zero female directors or chief engineers.
- **SARP Honorowa Nagroda (1966–2023)**: 6 women / 68 men in 57 years; only 3 independently ([[Jadwiga Grabowska-Hawrylak]] 1974, [[Halina Skibniewska]] 1978, [[Ewa Kuryłowicz]] 2021).
- **Poland EU ranking**: 21st out of 27 EU states on Gender Equality Index (2022).

## Key Ideas

**Glass Ceiling Index (GCI)** — Eurostat measure: ratio of women among all academics to women among professors. 1.0 = equality; higher = greater barrier. For Polish public technical universities 2021/22: **GCI = 1.88**. For architecture specifically: **GCI = 1.29** — best in technical sector, still above equality.

**Leaky pipeline** (*dziurawy rurociąg*) — the progressive loss of women at each successive stage of academic career (doctorate → habilitation → professorship). Measured by the **flow index** (0–10; 10 = full parity). Architecture 2021: 8.45 → 2022: 8.84 — improving, highest in technical sector.

**Feminization without power**: the structural paradox whereby an overwhelming majority of women in education does not translate into majority representation in senior professional roles. Explanations: informal networks (the cult of the single male name), jury composition (2:10 in SARP Pałac Saski competition), continuation of attribution erasure in architectural couples.

**Archidziadocen**: a neologism coined by [[BAL architektek]] and [[Milena Trzcińska]] in RZUT-34 for the current era of architectural patriarchy — implying it will eventually be recalled as an embarrassing historical episode, in the way that other eras of explicit discrimination are now viewed.

**PL:** GCI (indeks szklanego sufitu): uczelnie techniczne 1,88; architektura 1,29. Dziurawy rurociąg (leaky pipeline): kobiety "odpływają" na każdym szczeblu kariery; indeks przepływów: architektura 8,84 (2022). Feminizacja bez władzy: paradoks dominacji liczebnej bez dominacji decyzyjnej. Archidziadocen: ironiczy neologizm RZUT-34/BAL na obecną epokę architektonicznego patriarchatu.

## Critiques & Alternatives

The "feminization of architecture" framing risks treating the gender imbalance as a pipeline problem (fill the pipeline with women → eventually women reach the top) when the evidence suggests structural barriers operate independently of pipeline size. At WA PWr, women have been the majority since 1971 — yet senior parity has not followed 50+ years later. The pipeline metaphor also excludes non-binary persons (acknowledged by Aleksandra Gryc in her 2023 analysis). The intersectional feminist critique (see [[Equitable Design]]) argues that gender is only one axis of inequality in architecture — class, race, disability, and sexuality intersect and compound.

**PL:** Ryzyko framingu "pipeline": sama zmiana liczebna nie usuwa barier strukturalnych. WA PWr: kobiety większością od 1971, a parytetu władzy wciąż brak. Metafora rurociągu wyklucza osoby niebinarne. Krytyka interssekcjonalna: płeć to tylko jedna oś wykluczenia.
