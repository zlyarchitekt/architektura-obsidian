## Definition
A design strategy involving the creation of architectural installations, sculptures, or landscaped areas that inherently invite children's play, but are consciously not labeled as "playgrounds" or "play equipment" to bypass strict, standardizing legal and safety regulations.

**PL:** *Strategia polegająca na tworzeniu instalacji, rzeźb lub ukształtowania terenu, które zachęcają do zabawy, ale celowo nie są nazywane "placami zabaw", aby ominąć rygorystyczne normy i atesty bezpieczeństwa.*

## Historical Context
Emerged as a reaction to the over-bureaucratization of public play spaces, where liability fears forced designers to rely exclusively on certified, catalog-bought equipment, homogenizing urban play areas. The term "nie-plac zabaw" was popularized in Poland by Pracownia K.

## Key Proponents
[[Pracownia K]], [[Mikołaj Smoleński]]

## Key Examples
- *Fontanna Krasnali* in Wrocław.
- *Pomnik Dziecięcej Radości* by Atelier Starzak & Strębicki.

## Critiques & Alternatives
Transfers liability risk into a gray area; typically requires a specifically formulated rules-of-use (regulamin) to mitigate legal exposure for the property manager.
