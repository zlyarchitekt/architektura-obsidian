## Definition

Fourth nature (*czwarta przyroda*) designates the self-organizing ecological layer of urban and peri-urban territory: wasteland, post-industrial sites, railway margins, overgrown lots, brownfields, and other spaces where human activity has been withdrawn and spontaneous ecological processes have taken over. It is the ecological counterpart to abandonment — "architecture-free" space that is not designed, programmed, or managed, and which achieves higher biodiversity than managed parks precisely because of that absence. The concept positions ecological *non-intervention* as a legitimate and generative planning strategy.

**PL:** Czwarta przyroda to samoorganizująca się warstwa ekologiczna terytorium miejskiego i podmiejskiego: nieużytki, tereny postindustrialne, nasypy kolejowe, zarastające działki i inne przestrzenie, z których wycofała się ludzka aktywność. Jest ekologicznym odpowiednikiem porzucenia: przestrzeń "bez architektury", niezaprojektowana, nieprogramowana, niemanagowana — i osiągająca wyższą bioróżnorodność niż parki zarządzane właśnie dzięki temu brakowi.

## Historical Context

The concept of "fourth nature" belongs to a broader ecological taxonomy that classifies landscapes by degree of human intervention:
- **First nature**: pristine, unmodified ecosystems (practically absent in Western Europe and Poland)
- **Second nature**: agricultural and managed rural landscapes
- **Third nature** (Gilles Clément): designed gardens, parks, and ornamental plantings — human-curated nature
- **Fourth nature**: self-organizing, spontaneous, unmanaged — the result of withdrawal rather than design

The genealogy runs through Clément's [[Third Landscape]] (2004), which identifies urban "interstices" — railway margins, motorway verges, derelict lots — as the most biodiverse urban habitats precisely because no one manages them. In German landscape architecture, the concept appears as *Wildnis* (wildness) and in the "Industrienatur" movement (spontaneous vegetation colonising industrial ruins, most famously in the Ruhr). In Polish urban discourse, it is articulated by [[Jakub Węgrzynowicz]] in the context of planning reform, and implicitly by [[Magdalena Krzoska-Hołody]] in her analysis of the Łąki Golędzinowskie meadows.

A key empirical case: the "Szast" protection forest in Puszcza Piska, which two decades after a major natural disaster showed *higher* biodiversity in self-regenerating sections than in sections actively managed and restored by foresters. This provides empirical support for the counter-intuitive planning claim: doing less produces more nature.

**PL:** Taksonomia krajobrazów według stopnia interwencji człowieka: pierwsza przyroda (nienaruszona), druga (rolnicza, zarządzana), trzecia (Clément: ogrody, parki, nasadzenia), czwarta (samoorganizująca, spontaniczna, niezarządzana). Genealogia: Clément (*Trzeci Krajobraz* 2004), ruch "Industrienatur" w Niemczech (Zagłębie Ruhry). Empiryczne uzasadnienie: las "Szast" w Puszczy Piskiej — wyższa bioróżnorodność bez interwencji leśników.

## Key Examples

- **Las Ochronny "Szast," Puszcza Piska, Poland**: post-disaster forest in which self-regenerating sections (no forestry intervention) show higher biodiversity than sections actively managed by foresters. Research by Instytut Biologii Ssaków PAN.
- **Łąki Golędzinowskie, Warsaw**: wild Vistula-bank meadows; residents mobilised culturally and politically to resist their formalisation into a designed "natural park" — the unmanaged state was preferred over managed improvement. See [[Natureculture]].
- **Ruhr industrial ruins (Germany)**: spontaneous vegetation on decommissioned steelworks and coal pits — the *Industrienatur* case, foundational for the concept in landscape architecture.
- **Praga railway margins, Warsaw**: informal ecological corridors along disused railway tracks, valued by residents and NGOs despite (or because of) their unmaintained state.

**PL:**
- Las "Szast", Puszcza Piska: wyższa bioróżnorodność w sekcjach bez interwencji leśników.
- Łąki Golędzinowskie, Warszawa: niezarządzane łąki nadwiślańskie; protest przeciwko formalizacji w park.
- Zagłębie Ruhry, Niemcy: spontaniczna roślinność na terenach pohutniczych — paradygmat "Industrienatur".

## Critiques & Alternatives

- **Legal invisibility**: fourth nature has no legal status in Polish spatial planning documents; without formal designation, wastelands and brownfields can be developed at any time. Protection requires either creating a new planning category or stretching existing ones (e.g., designating as "ecological corridor" or "green area").
- **Aesthetic resistance**: unmaintained spaces are often perceived as neglected, unsafe, or undesirable by residents and decision-makers — overcoming this perception requires cultural work (the work [[Magdalena Krzoska-Hołody]] documents through cultural institutions).
- **Succession risk**: left entirely unmanaged, fourth-nature spaces may undergo ecological succession toward monoculture (invasive species colonisation is common); light management may be needed to maintain diversity without eliminating spontaneity.
- **Conflict with density**: in cities with housing shortages and development pressure (Warsaw, Kraków), unbuilt brownfields face strong conversion pressure. Fourth nature as planning strategy is easier to defend in shrinking cities or in cities with surplus land.

**PL:** Niewidoczność prawna (brak kategorii planistycznej w Polsce). Estetyczny opór: niezarządzane przestrzenie = "zaniedbane" w powszechnym odczuciu (praca kulturalna Krzoskiej-Hołody). Ryzyko sukcesji: bez jakiejkolwiek interwencji — monokultura inwazyjnych gatunków. Konflikt z presją densyfikacyjną w miastach z niedoborem mieszkań.

## In the Sources

- [[rzut-33-10-niescisla-ochrona-przyrody]]: Węgrzynowicz explicitly proposes "supporting wastelands" (*wspieranie nieużytków*) as a planning strategy; cites the Szast forest case; argues that the legal protection of fourth-nature sites requires formal planning designations at both national and local levels; frames the concept as part of a broader multispecies planning agenda.
- [[rzut-33-09-wychodzenie-za-rog]]: Krzoska-Hołody identifies "undefined spaces" (*przestrzenie niedookreślone*) — including brownfields and railway margins — as culturally and ecologically valued by residents; documents how cultural institutions map these attachments; uses the Łąki Golędzinowskie case as a test of fourth-nature politics.
