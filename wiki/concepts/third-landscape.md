## Definition
The "Third Landscape" (Tiers Paysage), a concept by Gilles Clément, refers to the sum of "undecided" spaces—abandoned urban plots, road verges, wastelands, and margins—that operate outside of human functional exploitation and serve as critical refuges for biodiversity.

**PL:** *"Trzeci Krajobraz" to koncepcja Gillesa Clémenta odnosząca się do "niezdecydowanych" przestrzeni (nieużytków, poboczy, terenów porzuconych), które znajdują się poza ludzką eksploatacją i służą jako kluczowe ostoje bioróżnorodności.*

## Historical Context
Formulated by Gilles Clément in his 2004 *Manifeste du Tiers paysage*. It shifted the perspective on urban "wastelands" from derelict areas needing development or tidying up, to vital ecological assets and genetic reservoirs.

## Key Proponents
[[Gilles Clément]], [[Dominika Trybe]]

## Key Examples
London's network of untamed back gardens, railway embankments, and abandoned infrastructure serving as habitats for urban foxes.

## Critiques & Alternatives
Often in tension with developmental pressures ("revitalization") which seek to formalize and sanitize these wild spaces, paradoxically reducing their ecological value.
