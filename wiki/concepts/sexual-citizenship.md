## Definition
A theoretical framework, developed primarily by geographers Jon Binnie and David Bell, arguing that citizenship is not only a legal or national status but also a spatial and sexual one. Cities are the primary sites where sexual citizenship is produced, contested, and performed. The presence of LGBTQ+ bodies, symbols, and spaces in the urban fabric forces a redefinition of who counts as a full member of the polis; conversely, their erasure or violent removal reaffirms heteronormative exclusions.

**PL:** *Teoretyczna ramka, rozwijana przede wszystkim przez geografów Jona Binnie i Davida Bella, zakładająca, że obywatelstwo nie jest wyłącznie statusem prawnym czy narodowym, lecz także przestrzennym i seksualnym. Miasta są głównymi miejscami, w których obywatelstwo seksualne jest produkowane, negocjowane i performowane. Obecność ciał, symboli i przestrzeni LGBTQ+ w tkance miejskiej wymusza redefinicję tego, kto liczy się jako pełny członek polis; z kolei ich wymazywanie lub brutalne usuwanie potwierdza heteronormatywne wykluczenia.*

## Historical Context
Emerging from 1990s queer geography and feminist urban theory, sexual citizenship scholarship responded to the visibility of gay villages in global cities (New York, London, San Francisco) and their subsequent commercialisation. Binnie and Bell stress that local context is decisive: in Poland, where national citizenship is tightly bound to Catholic and conservative heteronormativity, the city (especially Warsaw) functions as a counter-site where an alternative, inclusive sexual citizenship is struggled for.

## Key Proponents
Jon Binnie, David Bell, [[Michał Kowalski]] (Polish adaptation through queer planning).

## Key Examples
- **Warsaw's Plac Zbawiciela** (2012–2015) — Julita Wójcik's *Tęcza* became a physical claim to sexual citizenship; its repeated destruction and reclamation enacted the conflict between national-heteronormative and urban-inclusive definitions of belonging.
- **Vogue Polska poster vandalism (2022)** — the blacking-out of same-sex kisses on street posters was an attempt to deny sexual citizenship; the kiss-in protest reasserted it.

## Critiques & Alternatives
- **Commercial capture**: Sexual citizenship can be commodified (Pride as tourism product), stripping it of radical political content.
- **Urban elitism**: The framework sometimes overstates the city's progressive potential while ignoring how LGBTQ+ people in rural and small-town Poland remain invisible and unprotected.
