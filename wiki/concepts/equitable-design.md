## Definition
Spatial design deeply rooted in intersectional feminism and gender mainstreaming, which aims to create spaces that actively eliminate patriarchal hierarchies, support care work, and ensure safety and accessibility for marginalized or less dominant social groups.

**PL:** *Projektowanie przestrzenne głęboko zakorzenione w intersekcjonalnym feminizmie i gender mainstreamingu, którego celem jest tworzenie przestrzeni eliminujących patriarchalne hierarchie, wspierających pracę opiekuńczą oraz zapewniających bezpieczeństwo i dostępność grupom marginalizowanym.*

## Historical Context
Grew from feminist critiques of 20th-century modernist planning (which prioritized the male breadwinner commuting to the city center via car). Often reframed in public relations as "Equitable Design" (projektowanie równoprawne) or Universal Design due to societal stigma attached to the word "feminism", particularly in business contexts.

## Key Proponents
Leslie Kern (*Feminist City*), [[BAL architektek]], [[Architektoniczki]]

## Key Examples
[[Frauen-Werk-Stadt]] in Vienna. Removing centralized football pitches from schoolyards to equalize spatial distribution among all children.

## Critiques & Alternatives
Often diluted by corporate developers into superficial inclusivity rather than addressing the structural power dynamics of space.
