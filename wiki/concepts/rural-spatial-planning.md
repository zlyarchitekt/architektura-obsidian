## Definition

Rural spatial planning is the governance of land use, development, and spatial structure in rural and rural-urban municipalities (*gminy wiejskie* and *gminy miejsko-wiejskie*). In Poland it operates within the same legal framework as urban planning ([[Spatial Planning (Poland)]]) but is systematically underperforming: rural municipalities have the lowest coverage of binding local spatial plans (*mpzp*) in the country, leaving rural space particularly vulnerable to speculative land acquisition and spatial disorder.

**PL:** Planowanie przestrzenne obszarów wiejskich to zarządzanie użytkowaniem ziemi, zabudową i strukturą przestrzenną w gminach wiejskich i miejsko-wiejskich. W Polsce funkcjonuje w tym samym systemie prawnym co planowanie miejskie, lecz jest systematycznie niedostateczne: gminy wiejskie mają najniższe pokrycie miejscowymi planami zagospodarowania przestrzennego w kraju.

## Historical Context

Rural planning in Poland has historically been subordinated to agricultural productivity goals rather than spatial or social quality objectives. Under the PRL, planning targets were set centrally for *entire regions* with no regard for local specificity. The post-1989 transition introduced the same planning instruments as for cities, but the resources and institutional capacity to implement them were not provided at the municipal level.

The 2003 Spatial Planning Act ([[Spatial Planning (Poland)]]) disproportionately damaged rural municipalities: the invalidation of pre-1995 plans created a vacuum that urban areas could partially fill through development pressure and revenues, while rural communes — particularly agricultural ones — lacked both funding and political incentive to commission new plans. The *warunki zabudowy* instrument ([[Warunki Zabudowy]]) became a de facto planning bypass everywhere, but in rural areas it also opened the door to large-scale agricultural land conversion without public consultation.

The 2022 reform requires all municipalities to adopt a *plan ogólny* by 2026, but the scale of the task in rural areas — thousands of under-resourced communes — makes compliance by the deadline uncertain.

**PL:** Planowanie wiejskie w Polsce było historycznie podporządkowane celom produktywności rolniczej. Ustawa z 2003 r. nieproporcjonalnie uderzyła w gminy wiejskie: gminy typowo rolnicze mają najmniejsze pokrycie planami, brakuje im środków i politycznych motywów do sporządzania mpzp. Reforma 2022 nakłada obowiązek uchwalenia planu ogólnego do 2026 r.

## Key Examples

- **Osnowo and Brzozowo (Kujawsko-Pomorskie):** Small-scale village typology with individual farm dominance; strong private land attachment from pre-PRL period prevented large-scale corporate integration.
- **Michałówek (Opolskie):** Medium-scale typology with balanced small and large parcels; industrial facilities integrated within spatial planning, co-located with residential areas.
- **Bieganów (Lubuskie):** Large-scale former-PGR village; existing social infrastructure (housing blocks, allotment gardens, sports facilities, cultural centre) preserved from the communist era, but the land entirely absorbed by foreign agribusiness — a case of complete disconnection between spatial form and local community interest.

## Critiques & Alternatives

Rural areas represent a blind spot in Polish spatial planning discourse, which overwhelmingly focuses on urban sprawl in peri-urban zones. The specific pathologies of post-PGR territories — large-scale foreign land ownership, no spatial plan coverage, social infrastructure decay — require planning instruments designed for rural scale and context, not urban tools applied uniformly.

[[Romea Muryń]] argues that rural spatial planning reform must be coupled with land ownership reform: without changing the ownership structure, spatial plans alone cannot prevent continued commodification and spatial disorder. The lease model she proposes would create incentives aligned with long-term spatial quality rather than short-term land value extraction.

**PL:** Obszary wiejskie stanowią martwy punkt w polskim dyskursie o planowaniu przestrzennym, skoncentrowanym głównie na suburbanizacji stref podmiejskich. Muryń argumentuje, że reforma planowania wiejskiego musi być powiązana z reformą własności ziemi: bez zmiany struktury własnościowej same plany przestrzenne nie zapobiegną dalszemu utowarowieniu.
