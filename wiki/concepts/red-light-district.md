## Definition
An urban zone where sex work is concentrated, regulated, and made visible — historically through illuminated windows, street signage, or designated buildings. Red light districts function as both labour markets and tourist attractions, creating a permanent tension between worker safety (which requires visibility and proximity), resident quality-of-life (which resists noise and crowds), and municipal revenue (which depends on tourism but also seeks "respectable" redevelopment).

**PL:** *Strefa miejska, w której praca seksualna jest skoncentrowana, regulowana i uwidoczniona — historycznie przez oświetlone okna, znaki uliczne lub wyznaczone budynki. Dzielnice czerwonych latarni funkcjonują jednocześnie jako rynki pracy i atrakcje turystyczne, tworząc permanentne napięcie między bezpieczeństwem pracowników (wymagającym widoczności i bliskości), jakością życia mieszkańców (sprzeciwiającą się hałasowi i tłumom) a dochodami gminy (zależnymi od turystyki, ale też dążącej do „prestiżowej" rewitalizacji).*

## Historical Context
Amsterdam's De Wallen dates to the 19th century; its 350 ground-floor windows became iconic. In the 21st century, digital platforms and tourism have transformed the district: non-paying visitors now outnumber clients, creating a "walk of shame" for workers and economic pressure for relocation. Similar dynamics appear in Cologne (Pascha), Antwerp (Villa Tinto), and Basel (Toleranzzone).

## Key Examples
- **Amsterdam De Wallen** — 350 windows; alarm buttons; 2020 tour ban; four relocation scenarios proposed by mayor Femke Halsema.
- **Cologne Pascha** — 12-storey tower on city outskirts; result of court-ordered relocation from central Kleine Brinkgasse.
- **Antwerp Villa Tinto** — redesigned industrial building with 51 windows, medical care, and upper-floor B&B.
- **Basel Toleranzzone** — municipal parking-style signs for street workers; Laufhaus corridor system.

## Critiques & Alternatives
- **Tourism vs. work**: When gawkers outnumber clients, the district ceases to function as a workplace and becomes a spectacle.
- **Relocation as erasure**: Moving sex work to suburban towers (Pascha, proposed Amsterdam centre) may reduce resident complaints but increases worker isolation and vulnerability.
- **Consultation gap**: Workers are rarely included in planning decisions that reshape their workplaces.
