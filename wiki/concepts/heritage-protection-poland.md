## Definition
The legal and administrative framework for protecting culturally significant buildings and sites in Poland. Managed primarily by the Provincial Conservator of Monuments (WKZ) and Municipal Conservators (MKZ). The strongest form of protection is listing in the Register of Monuments (*Wpis do rejestru zabytków*). A weaker form is the Municipal Record of Monuments (*Gminna ewidencja zabytków - GEZ*).

**PL:** *Prawne i administracyjne ramy ochrony budynków i miejsc o znaczeniu kulturowym w Polsce. Zarządzane głównie przez Wojewódzkiego Konserwatora Zabytków (WKZ) i Miejskich Konserwatorów (MKZ). Najsilniejszą formą ochrony jest Wpis do rejestru zabytków. Słabszą formą jest Gminna ewidencja zabytków (GEZ).*

## Historical Context
The system heavily privileges very old buildings. Contemporary heritage—specifically [[Post-war Modernism]]—is drastically underrepresented in the Register. 

## The Regulatory Paradox
A crucial mechanism in Polish Building Law (Prawo budowlane) allows an investor to obtain a "deviation from technical building regulations" (*odstępstwo od warunków budowlano-technicznych*) only in "particularly justified cases." 
A formal entry in the Register of Monuments is the most universally accepted "justified case." Without this entry, modern building inspectors demand rigid adherence to current codes (like fire safety ratings or balustrade heights), which frequently requires the physical destruction of the building's original architectural features to comply.

## Key Examples
- [[PDT Lublin]] and [[Biblioteka UMCS]]: Both lack individual entry in the Register of Monuments, making it extremely difficult (or impossible in the case of the PDT fire-walls) for designers to legally circumvent destructive code-compliance retrofits.
