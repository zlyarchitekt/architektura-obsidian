## Definition
An ethical stance in architectural practice that rejects the architect's authority to impose a predetermined lifestyle on inhabitants. Instead of designing fixed programmes (this is the kitchen, this is the bedroom), the architect creates a neutral, robust spatial platform — structure, light, climate control, utility access — within which users can invent their own domestic rituals. The architect's role shifts from author to enabler.

**PL:** *Etyczne stanowisko w praktyce architektonicznej odrzucające autorytet architekta do narzucania mieszkańców określonego stylu życia. Zamiast projektować sztywne programy (to jest kuchnia, to jest sypialnia), architekt tworzy neutralną, solidną platformę przestrzenną — strukturę, światło, kontrolę klimatu, dostęp do instalacji — wewnątrz której użytkownicy mogą wynajdować własne rytuały domowe. Rola architekta przesuwa się z autora do umożliwiacza.*

## Historical Context
Non-prescriptive architecture challenges the modernist legacy of the architect as social engineer (Le Corbusier's "machine for living") and the contemporary starchitect model of the building as autobiographical sculpture. It draws on participatory design traditions but goes further: not merely consulting users, but surrendering programmatic control to them. It resonates with post-colonial critiques of architecture that impose Western norms on diverse cultures.

## Key Proponents
[[Tatiana Bilbao]], Alejandro Aravena (incremental housing), John Turner (self-help housing)

## Key Examples
- **Reconstruir MX** — Bilbao's studio offered only technical material advice; indigenous communities decided spatial solutions, including a family that rejected street-facing windows.
- **ELEMENTAL's half-a-good-house** — Aravena builds only the difficult half, leaving residents to complete the rest.
- **Traditional vernacular** — buildings adapted by users over generations without architect-imposed programmes.

## Critiques & Alternatives
- **Building codes**: Modern regulations mandate specific room types (kitchens with ventilation, bathrooms with drainage), making true non-prescription legally difficult.
- **Expertise paradox**: If the architect provides only structure and services, who guarantees structural safety or climate performance? The platform still requires expert design.
- **Commodification**: Developers can market "flexible" floorplates that are merely empty boxes, saving construction cost while claiming user empowerment.
