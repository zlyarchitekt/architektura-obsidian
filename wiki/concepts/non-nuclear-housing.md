## Definition
Housing that departs from the standard Western model designed for a two-parent, one-to-two-child nuclear family — a demographic that constitutes only about 18% of society in many countries. Non-nuclear housing includes configurations for single people, groups of friends, multi-generational households, co-housing collectives, and life-stage transitions (childless couple → parents → empty-nesters). The concept critiques the real-estate industry's persistent production of 2+1 and 2+2 flats that ignore the majority of actual living arrangements.

**PL:** *Mieszkalnictwo odchodzące od standardowego zachodniego modelu zaprojektowanego dla dwurodzicielskiej rodziny nuklearnej z jednym-dwójką dzieci — demografii stanowiącej zaledwie ok. 18% społeczeństwa w wielu krajach. Mieszkalnictwo non-nuclear obejmuje konfiguracje dla osób samotnych, grup przyjaciół, rodzin wielopokoleniowych, kolektywów co-housingowych i przejść między etapami życia (para bezdzietna → rodzice → „puste gniazdo"). Koncepcja krytykuje uporczywą produkcję przez przemysł nieruchomości mieszkań 2+1 i 2+2 ignorujących większość rzeczywistych układów mieszkaniowych.*

## Historical Context
The nuclear-family flat became the global default through post-war mass housing and mortgage systems that privileged the male-breadwinner model. Feminist and queer critiques have long pointed out that this model excludes single mothers, elderly living alone, young adults sharing flats, and non-heteronormative households. Yet planning regulations, building codes, and developer business models continue to reproduce it.

## Key Proponents
[[Tatiana Bilbao]], collective housing movements, feminist urbanists.

## Key Examples
- **Bilbao's critique** — standard apartments ignore groups of friends and multi-generational families; no market offer exists for them.
- **Pandemic care experiments** — attempts to share childcare across households failed because buildings lacked shared kitchens, laundries, and study rooms.
- **Co-living / co-housing** — contemporary attempts to build non-nuclear community, though often commodified.

## Critiques & Alternatives
- **Market failure**: Developers argue there is no demand, but the absence of supply prevents demand from forming.
- **Regulatory barriers**: Zoning often prohibits non-family households or mixed-use domestic buildings.
- **Cultural resistance**: Mortgage lenders and housing associations frequently discriminate against non-traditional households.
