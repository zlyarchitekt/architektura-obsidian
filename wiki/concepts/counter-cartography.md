## Definition

Counter-cartography (also: counter-mapping, radical cartography; Polish: *kontrmapowanie*) is a family of spatial practices that use the persuasive and narrative power of maps against the hierarchies, erasures, and exclusions that conventional cartography produces. Where standard maps claim objectivity while serving state and capital interests, counter-maps foreground subjective, embodied, and collective experience — making visible communities, ecosystems, and political relations that official cartography systematically omits. The term was coined by Nancy Lee Peluso to describe NGO re-mapping of indigenous Dayak land rights in Kalimantan (Indonesian Borneo), which was being mapped exclusively by state agencies aligned with World Bank development projects.

**PL:** Kontrmapowanie (*counter-cartography*, *radical cartography*) to rodzina praktyk przestrzennych, które używają siły perswazji i narracyjnej map *przeciw* hierarchiom, wymazaniom i wykluczeniom, które tworzy konwencjonalna kartografia. Termin ukuty przez Nancy Lee Peluso na określenie re-mapowania praw ziemskich Dajaków na Borneo (Kalimantan) przez organizacje pozarządowe, gdy był ono realizowane wyłącznie przez agencje państwowe.

## Historical Context

Cartography became institutionalised as a tool of state power — producing and guaranteeing territory, enabling colonial administration, and claiming an authority ("the territory is on paper, therefore it is real") that concealed its own constructed nature. Denis Wood argues that maps simplify the world and exclude "light, stars, shadows, birdsong, motorcycle noise" despite repeatedly asserting their accuracy; his concept of a *cartography of reality* calls for a more human, diverse, emotionally grounded spatial representation. Mark Monmonier's *How to Lie with Maps* (1991) provides the standard academic account of cartography's rhetorical deceptions. Bruno Latour's *Down to Earth* (2018) frames remapping as politically necessary in the age of climate crisis: "Everything will have to be remapped."

Counter-cartography emerged in the 1970s–80s as multiple movements began developing alternative mapping methodologies: anarchist geographers (Élisée Reclus), activist NGOs, Indigenous land rights organisations, feminist geographers. Bureau d'Etudes formalised radical cartography as a collective practice. Digital tools (web platforms, GPS, crowdsourced data) dramatically lowered barriers to participatory mapping from the 2000s onward.

**PL:** Kartografia zinstytucjonalizowała się jako narzędzie władzy państwowej — tworząc i gwarantując terytorium, ukrywając własną konstruktywność. Denis Wood: mapy upraszczają i wykluczają "światło, gwiazdy, cienie, śpiew ptaków". Mark Monmonier: *How to Lie with Maps* (1991). Bruno Latour: "Wszystko będzie musiało zostać zmapowane jeszcze raz" (*Down to Earth*, 2018). Kontrmapowanie wyłoniło się w l. 70.–80. przez geografów anarchistycznych, organizacje praw ziemskich rdzennych ludów, Bureau d'Etudes.

## Key Examples

- **Anti-Eviction Mapping Project (AEMP, San Francisco)**: tracks shell companies and Airbnb connections behind displacement; builds community counter-narratives (*Narratives of Displacement and Resistance* — murals, zine, residents' rights guide); finds displacement disproportionately affects women, elderly, working class, ethnic minorities; must keep some data secret to protect communities.
- **Port Said community mapping (Nermin Elsherif)**: replaces official city image with first-person community narrative; pins and strings connect stories across Egypt and diaspora; no hierarchy of importance among stories; participation in making and in reading.
- **Cuerpo-territorio / body mapping** (Latin American practice; Unanyan): embodied memories mapped onto a drawing of one's own body; reverses the map's standard logic (body over space); bypasses language barriers; used in Kolkata (Unanyan, 1977–) to protect ~2 million undocumented residents from displacement.
- **Unanyan's *Unintended City* (Kolkata)**: founded by Jai Sen; mapped the 2-million-person Panchannagram community, undocumented and literally absent from city maps; enabled negotiation against highway/redevelopment destruction; much of the archive was later destroyed in flooding.
- **Queering The Map (Lucas LaRochelle, Montreal)**: global LGBTQ2IA+ memory map; anonymous entries (love stories, coming-out memories, traumas, trivia) without dates or markers; global palimpsest; destroys the map's standard orienting function; builds a visible community without exposing individuals; hacked by Trump supporters in 2019.
- **Rebecca Solnit & Rebecca Snedeker, *Unfathomable City* (New Orleans)**: corruption/lead-level correlations; Gulf oil industry ecosystem destruction (water as pipeline-crossed surface rather than empty blue).

**PL:**

- Mapa Port Saidu (Elsherif): wspomnienia zamiast granic.
- Cuerpo-territorio: wspomnienia nanoszone na sylwetkę ciała; Unanyan (Kalkuta): ochrona 2 mln niezarejestrowanych mieszkańców.
- Queering The Map (LaRochelle, Montreal): globalny palimpsest wspomnień LGBTQ2IA+.
- Solnit & Snedeker, *Unfathomable City*: korupcja/ołów; niszczenie ekosystemu przez przemysł naftowy.

## Critiques & Alternatives

- **Counter-maps are also maps**: they distort, select, and serve political agendas. Weronika Kozak: "counter-mapping is in reality a lie aimed at another lie." There is no politically neutral spatial representation.
- **Visibility as risk**: making undocumented or marginalised communities legible can endanger them legally or physically (AEMP's Erin McElroy; André Mesquita; Lis Mason-Deese). The Iconoclasistas collective advocates consent-based, anonymity-respecting mapping.
- **Fragility**: the Port Said map was physically destroyed; *Queering The Map* was hacked; Unanyan's archive was lost in a flood. "The possibilities of maps end where the possibilities of people end."
- **Non-participation paradox**: Unanyan's *Unintended City* was made *about* a community but *without* that community's participation — Jai Sen questions what the maps would have looked like had residents made them themselves.
- **Radical cartography vs. data refusal**: some anarchist geographers (Reclus) called for stopping mapping altogether rather than remapping; Kozak argues the persuasive power of maps is too valuable to abandon — better to redeploy it.

**PL:** Kontrmapowanie to kłamstwo wymierzone w inne kłamstwo (Kozak). Niewidzialność jako ochrona: ujawnianie narażonych społeczności może je narazić na ryzyko. Kruche nośniki: mapa Port Saidu zniszczona, Queering The Map zhakowany. Paradoks departyypacji: Unintended City zrobione *o* społeczności, nie *przez* nią.
