## Definition

Land commodification (*utowarowienie ziemi*) is the process by which land — a finite, irreplaceable, and socially embedded resource — is transformed into a tradeable financial asset governed primarily by market mechanisms. It detaches land from its social, ecological, and productive functions and subjects it to speculative accumulation, financialization, and extraction by capital actors with no necessary connection to local communities.

**PL:** Utowarowienie ziemi (*land commodification*) to proces, przez który ziemia — zasób skończony, niezastąpiony i osadzony społecznie — jest przekształcana w zbywalny aktyw finansowy zarządzany przede wszystkim przez mechanizmy rynkowe. Odrywa ziemię od jej społecznych, ekologicznych i produkcyjnych funkcji, poddając ją spekulacyjnej akumulacji i finansjalizacji przez podmioty kapitałowe nieposiadające żadnych koniecznych więzi z lokalnymi społecznościami.

## Historical Context

Commodification of land is not new — it has been theorised since at least Henry George (*Progress and Poverty*, 1879), who argued that land value is socially produced and should be publicly captured through land value taxation. In the 20th century, socialist states attempted the opposite correction — full nationalization — which produced its own pathologies (centralization, inefficiency, elimination of local community agency).

The contemporary wave of land commodification, analyzed by [[Saskia Sassen]] in *Expulsions* (2014), operates through sophisticated financial instruments, foreign direct investment in agricultural land, and the construction of elaborate legal and accounting structures that give speculative land trade an appearance of neutrality. The result is a self-reinforcing sector dependent on continued foreign land acquisition.

In **Poland** specifically, the 1989 transition created conditions for accelerated commodification: the state land agency (AWRSP → ANR → KOWR) sold off the PGR portfolio without area limits, social criteria, or spatial planning integration. By 2016, private ownership of agricultural land had risen from 76.3% (1990) to **91.4%**, with the state retaining only 8.6%. Foreign capital — predominantly Dutch (35%) and German (38%) corporations — acquired **73%** of all foreign-held Polish agricultural land, concentrated in Zachodniopomorskie (32%) and Warmińsko-Mazurskie (19%) — the former PGR heartland.

**PL:** Współczesna fala utowarowienia ziemi, analizowana przez [[Saskia Sassen|Saskię Sassen]] w *Expulsions* (2014), operuje poprzez wyrafinowane instrumenty finansowe i bezpośrednie inwestycje zagraniczne. W Polsce prywatyzacja po 1989 r. doprowadziła do sytuacji, w której do 2016 r. własność prywatna ziemi rolniczej wzrosła z 76,3% (1990) do **91,4%**, a zagraniczny kapitał — głównie holenderski (35%) i niemiecki (38%) — przejął **73%** wszystkich zagranicznych udziałów w polskiej ziemi.

## Key Proponents / Critics

- [[Saskia Sassen]] — global theoretical frame; *Expulsions* (2014); concept of the self-perpetuating land trade sector; loss of state territorial sovereignty
- [[Romea Muryń]] — Poland-specific analysis; proposed remedy: lease model + state buyback
- **Henry George** — 19th-century precursor; land value tax as counter-instrument

## Key Examples

- **Post-PGR territories in Poland:** Land that passed from state ownership through ANR without community or spatial planning safeguards, absorbed by large-scale foreign agribusiness (cf. Bieganów village, Lubuskie).
- **Animex and Cargill Poland:** Largest agricultural combines in Poland, both foreign-owned, cited in the source as examples of capital extraction from Polish territory.
- **Berlin corporate housing (2010s–2020s):** Approximately 250,000 Berlin apartments held by stock-listed corporations (Vonovia, Deutsche Wohnen, Pears Global); rents +44% (2015–2020); rent extracted through tax havens to global shareholders (BlackRock, Blackstone). The [[Deutsche Wohnen und Co enteignen]] initiative invokes the same logic as Muryń's analysis: the original sale of public assets at below-market prices was a transfer of community wealth to capital, and the rising rents are ongoing extraction. The DWE's [[Socialization (Vergesellschaftung)]] proposal — like the lease model — aims to break the extraction circuit without repeating the PRL error of full state ownership.

## Critiques & Alternatives

The primary alternative proposed in the source ([[rzut-modele-zawlaszczania-ziemi]]) is the **lease model**: separating the right to use land from ownership, eliminating land speculation, and returning land-value increments to the public sector for reinvestment in social infrastructure. This is positioned against both the neoliberal model (pure market) and the PRL model (full state ownership + central management).

The tension: any state re-acquisition of land risks comparison to PRL nationalization. Muryń's counter-argument is that the new model is decentralized and lease-based — it does not expropriate use rights from current operators, merely removes land from the speculative ownership circuit.

**PL:** Główną alternatywą proponowaną w źródle jest **model dzierżawy**: oddzielenie prawa użytkowania od własności, eliminacja spekulacji i zwrot przyrostów wartości ziemi do sektora publicznego. Jest on pozycjonowany przeciwko zarówno modelowi neoliberalnemu, jak i modelowi PRL.
