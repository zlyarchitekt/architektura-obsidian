## Definition

Państwowe Gospodarstwa Rolne (PGR — State Agricultural Farms) were large centrally managed state enterprises that operated on nationalized agricultural land in communist Poland from approximately 1947 to 1991. They were the primary instrument of the post-war agricultural nationalization program and constituted the dominant land ownership structure in the western and northern "recovered territories" (*ziemie odzyskane*). Their dissolution after 1989 created the conditions for Poland's current agricultural land ownership crisis.

**PL:** Państwowe Gospodarstwa Rolne (PGR) były dużymi, centralnie zarządzanymi przedsiębiorstwami państwowymi prowadzonymi na znacjonalizowanych gruntach rolnych komunistycznej Polski od ok. 1947 do 1991 r. Stanowiły główny instrument powojennego programu nacjonalizacji rolnictwa i dominującą strukturę własności ziemi na zachodnich i północnych ziemiach odzyskanych. Ich likwidacja po 1989 r. stworzyła warunki dla obecnego kryzysu własności ziemi rolniczej w Polsce.

## Historical Context

**PRL Phase (1947–1989):** The *plan sześcioletni* (1950–1955, "Six-Year Plan") launched the nationalization of agricultural land. PGRs were created primarily in the western and northern territories (Zachodniopomorskie, Lubuskie, Dolnośląskie, Warmińsko-Mazurskie) where the incoming resettled population had weaker attachment to the land than pre-war populations in eastern Poland. From the mid-1960s, PGRs underwent consolidation: average size grew from 901 ha (312 PGRs, 1970) to **3,703 ha (947 PGRs, 1980)**. By 1978, the number of family farms had halved compared to 1974 levels. Original landowners received no compensation.

PGRs were not purely agricultural enterprises — they provided housing, social infrastructure, cultural facilities, and employment for entire local communities. This social function is central to understanding the consequences of their dissolution.

**Post-1989 Phase:** Centralization had produced systemic inefficiency. Social protests in 1980 demanded decentralization; the "3S reform" (1982 — *samodzielność, samorządność, samofinansowanie*) subordinated PGRs to regional authorities (*wojewodowie*). After 1989, PGRs were wound down and their land transferred to the state land agency (AWRSP, later ANR, now KOWR). The agency sold the portfolio without area limits, family farm priorities (added only in 2003), or spatial planning integration. Large parcels (100+ ha) were sold preferentially — one such parcel is three times the footprint of the Palace of Culture and Science in Warsaw.

**PL:** W fazie PRL (1947–1989) plan sześcioletni uruchomił nacjonalizację ziemi rolniczej. Od połowy lat 60. PGR-y ulegały konsolidacji: średnia powierzchnia wzrosła z 901 ha (312 PGR, 1970) do **3703 ha (947 PGR, 1980)**. PGR-y zapewniały lokalne mieszkalnictwo, infrastrukturę społeczną i zatrudnienie całym społecznościom. Po 1989 r. ich ziemie przeszły do ANR, która wyprzedała zasób bez limitów obszarowych ani kryteriów społecznych.

## Key Examples

- **Bieganów (Lubuskie):** Operated as a PGR from 1949 to 1990. Post-dissolution, its land passed from state ownership to foreign agribusiness capital, which absorbed the entire community's economic base while retaining the inherited state-funded infrastructure. The spatial analysis in [[Modele zawłaszczania ziemi]] uses Bieganów as the most extreme case of PGR-legacy land capture.
- **Zachodniopomorskie, Warmińsko-Mazurskie:** The regions with the highest former PGR concentration are now the regions with the highest foreign land ownership (Zachodniopomorskie: 32%, Warmińsko-Mazurskie: 19% of all foreign-purchased land).

## Critiques & Alternatives

The PGR model failed on its own terms — centralization, political decision-making by actors without local knowledge, and overscaling produced the 1980 social protests. But the post-1989 alternative — liquidation without community transition planning — produced a different failure: spatial disorder, social impoverishment, and foreign capital substitution for state ownership without any improvement in community control.

[[Romea Muryń]]'s proposed new agricultural reform explicitly positions the lease model as a third path between PRL renationalization and neoliberal privatization: public ownership of land, private use rights, decentralized management, community benefit.

**PL:** Model PGR zawiódł na własnych warunkach — centralizacja i przeskalowanie doprowadziły do protestów 1980. Alternatywa po 1989 r. — likwidacja bez planowania transformacji społeczności — przyniosła odmienny rodzaj porażki: nieład przestrzenny i substytucję własności państwowej przez zagraniczny kapitał.
