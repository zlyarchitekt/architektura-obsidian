## Definition
The full range of architectural and urban typologies designed or appropriated for the provision of sexual services: street-based (with or without shelters), window brothels, drive-in facilities, clubs and strip bars, BDSM studios, escort apartments, hotel rooms, Laufhaus corridors, and large-scale purpose-built erotic centres. Each typology produces distinct spatial conditions of visibility, privacy, safety, and surveillance.

**PL:** *Pełny zakres typologii architektonicznych i miejskich zaprojektowanych lub zaadaptowanych do świadczenia usług seksualnych: ulica (z lub bez wiat), okna, drive-iny, kluby i bary ze striptizem, studia BDSM, mieszkania escortowe, pokoje hotelowe, korytarze Laufhaus oraz wielkoskalowe, celowo budowane centra erotyczne. Każda typologia produkuje odmienne warunki przestrzenne widoczności, prywatności, bezpieczeństwa i nadzoru.*

## Historical Context
Sex work has always occupied the margins of urban planning — tolerated in designated zones, persecuted elsewhere, rarely designed *for* workers. The 21st century has seen two divergent trends: in some European cities (Zurich, Amsterdam) specialised infrastructure is built with public funds after referenda; in others (Poland) abolitionist policy drives the activity into invisibility, removing both safety and labour rights.

## Key Examples
- **Zurich sex drive-in** (2013) — wooden LED shelters, driver-door lock, emergency button; no worker consultation.
- **Amsterdam window brothels** — ground-floor windows, inter-door alarms, police buttons; tourist nuisance as occupational hazard.
- **Basel Laufhaus** — corridor-based room rental; municipal parking-style signs for street workers.
- **Cologne Pascha** — 12-storey tower, 126 rooms, 24-hour operation; relocation from city centre.
- **Antwerp Villa Tinto** (Arne Quinze) — 51 windows, medical care, B&B upper floors.
- **Amsterdam Erotic Centre** (Moke Architecten, proposed) — 5,000 m² tower with looping corridors; criticised for lack of worker consultation.

## Critiques & Alternatives
- **Criminalisation vs. design**: Where sex work is criminalised, workers cannot influence planning; where it is legalised, they are often subjected to stigmatising surveillance (special permits, medical checks).
- **Male gaze architecture**: Strip clubs and Playboy-inspired interiors are designed for the client, not the worker. Safety devices (panic buttons, bouncer protocols) are add-ons rather than integral spatial logic.
- **Worker consultation**: The Zurich drive-in and the Amsterdam relocation were designed without sex-worker input. Spatial safety requires participatory design.
