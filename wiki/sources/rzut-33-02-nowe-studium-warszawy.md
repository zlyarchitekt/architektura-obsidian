## Summary

A concise overview of Warsaw's new Studium Uwarunkowań i Kierunków Zagospodarowania Przestrzennego, written by Wojciech Kacperski of [[BAiPP]]. The article presents the Studium as treating Warsaw as an **ecosystem** — an integrated whole in which the social, economic, and natural spheres are inseparable — and synthesises the document's core spatial principles.

The dominant structural innovation is **Błękitno-Zielona Infrastruktura Warszawy (BZIW)** — [[Blue-Green Infrastructure]]. The city is to function as a sponge, not armour: retaining water on-site, absorbing rather than draining stormwater, with water and greenery treated as "soft infrastructure" of equal importance to hard roads, pavements, and sewers. BZIW is realised through a network of **green rings** (*zielone pierścienie*) — linear pedestrian and cycling routes through greenery connecting all Warsaw districts — as well as **linear parks** (*parki linearne*), green avenues, tree-lined cycling lanes, and water gateways. By 2050, every Warsaw resident is to have greenery within sight of their window or within a few steps.

The document also establishes the ideal of a **compact, polycentric city**: new housing concentrated on brownfields (former industrial and railway land, *pofabryczne* and *pokolejowe*) near the urban core, combined with a hierarchical network of **local centres** (*centra lokalne*) ensuring that everyday errands — school, transit stop, shop, café — are reachable on foot in 10 minutes. 99% of residents are to be within a 15-minute walk of a local centre, 93% within 10 minutes. Demographic projections, updated via mobile phone data rather than registry figures, set a realistic 2050 population of approximately 2.25 million.

**PL:** Artykuł Kacperskiego z BAiPP przedstawia nowe Studium Warszawy jako traktujące miasto jako ekosystem. Centralna innowacja strukturalna to BZIW: miasto-gąbka; zielone pierścienie; parki linearne; zieleń i woda jako "miękka infrastruktura" równa twardej. Ideałem jest miasto zwarte i policentryczne: nowa zabudowa na terenach poprzemysłowych, sieć centrów lokalnych dostępnych w 10 minut pieszo. Dane demograficzne z telefonii komórkowej: 1,97 mln rzeczywistych mieszkańców.

## Key Points

- Warsaw treated as an ecosystem: social + economic + natural spheres fully integrated.
- **BZIW** (Blue-Green Infrastructure): city as sponge; water retained on-site; greenery and water = "soft infrastructure" as important as roads and sewers.
- **Green rings** (*zielone pierścienie*): circular linear pedestrian/cycling paths through greenery connecting all districts; also green avenues, linear parks, water gateways.
- By 2050 target: every resident to have greenery within sight (tree visible from window, or nearby park/square).
- **Compact city**: new housing concentrated on brownfields near urban core; no new single-family reserves needed.
- **Polycentric structure** with local centres: 99% of residents within 15 min walk to local centre, 93% within 10 min.
- **10-minute city** ideal: school, transit, shop, café all reachable on foot in 10 minutes.
- Demographic data sourced from mobile phone tracking: 1.97M real residents vs. 1.79M by registry.
- War in Ukraine added tens of thousands of residents, compressing mid-century demographic projections.
- Aging population: 1 in 4 Warsaw residents over 65 by 2050; walkable proximity = social health.
- Economic weight: Warsaw holds 50% of Poland's office stock, 5% of registered businesses, 17.7% of Polish GDP.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The article presents BZIW as a fully integrated system but does not address implementation mechanics — how it is enforced in private development, who mandates it at the plan-making level.
- The 10-minute city ideal is ambitious; Warsaw's peripheral districts and low-density suburban fringes may be structurally unable to meet this standard without first filling spatial gaps — which requires funding, land, and political will the article does not address.
