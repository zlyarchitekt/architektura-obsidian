## Summary

This Divisare project page is Heatherwick Studio's own promotional account of Coal Drops Yard, presented at completion (October 2018). Like the Vessel Divisare source, it is an insider text — authoritative on design intent and process, silent on reception, criticism, or commercial performance.

**PL:** *Strona projektu Divisare to własny materiał promocyjny Heatherwick Studio z Coal Drops Yard (ukończenie październik 2018). Podobnie jak źródło Divisare dla Vessel — miarodajne w zakresie intencji projektowej i procesu, milczące wobec recepcji i krytyki.*

Coal Drops Yard is two elongated Victorian railway buildings (1850s, King's Cross) originally built to receive coal from Northern England for distribution around London. They fell into partial dereliction, serving light industry, warehousing, and nightclubs before near-abandonment in the 1990s. Heatherwick Studio was commissioned by King's Cross Central Limited Partnership in 2014 to transform them into a "lively retail district." The studio's solution is structurally and formally inventive: the two existing gabled roofs are extended with entirely new flowing steel canopies that **rise toward each other and touch**, creating an entirely new third level — a floating covered outdoor terrace suspended between the heritage structures. The new canopy structure is described as "entirely freestanding," threaded through the historic fabric, meaning the Victorian brick and cast-iron viaducts bear none of the new structure's load.

**PL:** *Coal Drops Yard to dwa wydłużone wiktoriańskie budynki kolejowe (lata 50. XIX w., King's Cross), zbudowane pierwotnie do rozdziału węgla. Po dekadach dezinwestycji Heatherwick Studio (zlecenie 2014) przekształciło je w dzielnicę handlową. Kluczowym rozwiązaniem są nowe przepływające stalowe daszki nad każdym budynkiem, które unoszą się ku sobie i stykają, tworząc nowe trzecie piętro — pływający taras — podwieszony między historycznymi strukturami. Nowa stalowa konstrukcja jest samonośna, nie obciążając historycznych murów.*

The project went from commission (2014) to opening (October 2018) in four years, with construction beginning in early 2016. It was the **first major building completion in London for Heatherwick Studio** — despite the studio having been based in King's Cross for 17 years. The project also signals the studio's ambitions for large-scale London work at the time: a new Google building in King's Cross was concurrently under construction (co-designed with BIG), and Olympia London was in the pipeline.

**PL:** *Projekt: zlecenie 2014, otwarcie październik 2018, budowa od początek 2016. Był to pierwsza główna realizacja budowlana Heatherwick Studio w Londynie, mimo 17-letniego pobytu w King's Cross. Sygnalizuje też ambicje na dużą skalę: Google King's Cross (z BIG) i Olimpia Londyn były równolegle w toku.*

## Key Points

- Programme: ~60 retail units (from 160 sq ft to 20,000+ sq ft); restaurants, bars; significant public space through-routes.
- Commission: 2014, King's Cross Central Limited Partnership (KCCLP).
- Construction: began early 2016; opened October 2018.
- Structural key: two new flowing steel roofs, **entirely freestanding** (new structure threaded through heritage fabric), connected at upper level to form a third floating storey.
- Heritage context: Victorian coal drops, ornate cast iron and brick, 1850s — previously never fully public; project opens them for the first time.
- Circulation: entrances at both ends + Stable Street; the yard becomes permeable public space.
- Quote from Thomas Heatherwick: "These amazing Victorian structures were never originally built to be inhabited by hundreds of people, but instead formed part of the sealed-off infrastructure of London."
- Quote from Lisa Finlay (Group Leader): "One of which is an entirely free-standing new structure threaded through the historic buildings, from which a spectacular new third level is suspended."
- Concurrent projects at time of opening: Google North Bayshore HQ (with BIG), Olympia London.
