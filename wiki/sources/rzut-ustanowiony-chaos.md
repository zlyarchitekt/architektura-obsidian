## Summary

An interview with Piotr Uściński, Secretary of State at the Polish Ministry of Development and Technology, conducted by Zofia Piotrowska for Kwartalnik RZUT. Published September 2022. The interview diagnoses the failure of Polish spatial planning since the 2003 Spatial Planning Act and describes the government's planned reform.

The 2003 Act created a structural crisis in two moves: it invalidated all local spatial plans (*mpzp*) adopted before 1995, giving municipalities only 10 months to replace them — an impossibly short window. The instrument introduced to fill the vacuum — the *warunki zabudowy* (WZ, building conditions decision) — became an administrative bypass allowing construction almost anywhere without public consultation. WZ is described as "the most hated tool among planners and the most popular among developers."

The 2022 reform's central mechanism replaces the non-binding *studium* with a legally binding *plan ogólny* (general plan), cheaper and faster to produce. All municipalities are required to adopt a *plan ogólny* by 2026. WZ decisions will be restricted to designated infill zones (*obszary uzupełnienia zabudowy*) but are not abolished — new decisions will be valid for 5 years only. Uściński defends an evolutionary rather than revolutionary approach and explicitly rejects the "ideological" position that single-family housing or suburban growth are inherently bad. Notably absent from the reform: metropolitan area governance, sustainable transport, and climate adaptation.

## Key Points

- The 2003 Spatial Planning Act invalidated pre-1995 local plans and gave only 10 months for replacement — the root cause of today's planning chaos.
- *Warunki zabudowy* (WZ) can be issued almost anywhere, without public consultation; it is an administrative act, not local law, and can be legally forced on an unwilling municipality.
- Spatial chaos cost: **84.3 billion PLN/year** (PAN report) — mainly infrastructure maintenance for dispersed settlement.
- The 2022 reform replaces *studium* (internal municipal document, not binding on residents) with *plan ogólny* (binding law, required by 2026).
- WZ decisions will only be permissible within "infill zones" defined by the *plan ogólny*; new decisions valid for 5 years only.
- Public participation simplified to one round (from up to five), with digital access.
- **Missing from reform:** metropolitan area governance, sustainable transport, climate adaptation.
- Uściński explicitly rejects the argument that single-family housing should be discouraged — frames restriction as "ideological."
- Constitutional principle stated: **the right to build is NOT part of property rights** in Polish law.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- Uściński's defence of suburban sprawl as "natural and not inherently negative" is in direct tension with the PAN cost figure (84.3 billion PLN/year) he cites himself — that figure is almost entirely a cost of low-density dispersed settlement.
- The reform explicitly excludes metropolitan area governance, yet the interview repeatedly identifies major cities as the sites of worst planning dysfunction. Missing a metropolitan tier is the most significant gap.
- WZ decisions are preserved after reform; whether the *plan ogólny* requirement will actually constrain them is untested.
- The interview was conducted under the PiS-coalition government. Reform implementation status after the October 2023 change of government is an open question for future ingestion.

## Quotes

> "Głównym celem planowania jest to, żeby ludziom żyło się dobrze, a nie ograniczenie kosztów funkcjonowania gminy."

(The primary goal of planning is for people to live well — not to reduce the municipality's operating costs.)

> "Chaos przestrzenny – jak można określić niektóre realizacje – nie wynika z woli inwestora, tylko z różnych uwarunkowań, takich jak brak planów miejscowych."

(Spatial chaos — as one might describe some developments — does not arise from the developer's intention, but from various conditions, such as the absence of local plans.)

> "Z prawa własności nie wynika prawo do zabudowy."

(Property rights do not include a right to build.)

> "Udało nam się doprowadzić do sytuacji przegrany–przegrany."

(We managed to create a lose-lose situation.)
