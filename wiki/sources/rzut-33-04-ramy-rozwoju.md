## Summary

An interview with [[Monika Konrad]], deputy director and chief designer of the Studium at [[BAiPP]], conducted by Zofia Piotrowska. The article presents the methodological and structural foundations of Warsaw's new Studium — the analytical framework, data challenges, legal constraints, and core spatial concepts underpinning the 30-year vision.

Konrad frames the Studium as *ramy* (frameworks), not a prescription: it sets the target model of the city without predetermining every planning decision for 30 years. Flexibility was proven necessary during its preparation — COVID-19 changed work patterns and residential preferences; the Ukrainian refugee crisis compressed decades of demographic projections into months. The ecosystem concept (city as one organism, a system of communicating vessels) is the Studium's philosophical foundation, ensuring long-term urban resilience — defined as the ability to adapt to change and recover from unforeseen negative events.

A key methodological innovation is the morphological typology — ten mixed-use urban fabric types (MU1–MU10) and three large modernist estate types (M1–M3) — used as the analytical backbone for spatial decisions. The Studium also introduces an ecosystem services analysis, the first of its kind in Poland (comparable to Barcelona, Berlin, New York), as the basis for BZIW designation.

## Key Points

- **Framework, not prescription**: the Studium sets the target model of the city without predetermining all decisions for 30 years; flexibility essential — COVID-19 and the Ukrainian refugee crisis both occurred during its preparation.
- **Ecosystem philosophy**: Warsaw treated as a single organism — a system of communicating vessels; only their integration ensures long-term urban resilience (adaptability + recovery from unforeseen events).
- **Morphological typology**: ten mixed-use fabric types (MU1–MU10) and three modernist estate types (M1–M3) describe the characteristic patterns of Warsaw's urban fabric; "amorphic zones" (*strefy amorficzne*) requiring corrective action are also identified.
- **Mixed-use flexibility**: most areas designated as *mieszkaniowo-usługowe* (residential-service), not separate residential and commercial; mixing possible both horizontally and vertically, including industry 4.0 and research functions.
- **Data gaps**: property tax data by function unavailable; health data dispersed; real estate market data must be purchased privately; demographic data not systematically collected — all limit planning precision.
- **Brownfield priority**: key development reserves — Żerań FSO, Stare Świdry, Odolany, Ulrychów, Port Żerański, Port Praski, Szwedzka Street area, Kamionek.
- **Ecosystem services analysis**: first such analysis in Poland; comparable methodology to Barcelona, Berlin, New York; forms the basis for BZIW system designation.
- **BZIW implementation logic**: "protect existing green areas, then create connections between them" — produced the Green Rings (two pedestrian-cycling rings connecting major recreation areas including the Vistula riverside and Warsaw Fortress remains).
- **Legal limitations**: WZ decisions need not comply with the Studium (primary systemic flaw); no *bank gruntów* (land bank) in Poland; compulsory purchase mechanisms underused; planning powers weaker than Germany or Netherlands.
- **Metropolitan scale**: Studium is limited to Warsaw's administrative boundaries; metropolitan coordination handled by the parallel *Strategia rozwoju metropolii warszawskiej do roku 2040*.
- **Panorama and height policy**: *wnętrza krajobrazowe* (landscape interiors) designated — Vistula Valley is a key example; city panorama monitoring points; high-rise zones defined.
- **Modernist housing estates**: fully formed estates to be protected; incomplete estates to be completed coherently.
- **Food production in the city**: allotment gardens (RODs) generated the most submissions during the 2018–2019 public consultation, demanding their preservation; food production also possible in parks, courtyards, rooftops, balconies.
- **"Wszędzie blisko" (everywhere close)**: Warsaw envisioned as a city where school, transit, shop, café are all reachable on foot in 10 minutes; 93% within 10 minutes to a local centre by 2050.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- Konrad acknowledges that the Studium's legal enforceability is fundamentally undermined by WZ decisions and the absence of a land bank — yet presents the document as capable of "stopping sprawl." This tension between ambition and enforceability is named but not resolved.
- The ecosystem services analysis is described as a Polish first, but no methodology is specified; how it informed specific BZIW designations is not explained.
- Metropolitan planning depends on a separate strategic document (*Strategia 2040*) prepared simultaneously — whether the two visions align is not addressed.
- The morphological typology (MU1–MU10) implies nuanced land-use differentiation, yet the move to *mieszkaniowo-usługowe* designations implies flexibility; these two logics — typological precision vs. programmatic openness — exist in unresolved tension.

## Quotes

> "Zależało nam na tym, aby nowe Studium nie determinowało wszystkich decyzji planistycznych, jakie będą podejmowane w ciągu następnych 30 lat, ale wyznaczało ramy rozwoju przestrzennego, czyli docelowy model miasta."

(We wanted the new Studium not to predetermine all planning decisions for the next 30 years, but to set the framework for spatial development — the target model of the city.)

> "Warszawa ma strukturę mozaikową, która stanowi jej piękno. Próbujemy zharmonizować zagospodarowanie przestrzenne tak, żeby nie było skrajności."

(Warsaw has a mosaic structure, which is its beauty. We try to harmonise land use so that there are no extremes.)
