## Summary

Wikipedia article on Thomas Heatherwick (born 17 February 1970), English designer and founder of [[Heatherwick Studio]]. Unlike the studio's own Divisare project pages ingested previously, this source is encyclopaedic in scope — spanning biography, chronological works list, awards, design philosophy, and documented controversies. It is the most critically informative source on Heatherwick in the wiki.

The article documents a career of approximately 30 years, beginning with the founding of Heatherwick Studio in 1994 after graduation from the Royal College of Art. The studio's output spans scales from product design (the Magis Spun chair, the Routemaster bus) to major urban infrastructure ([[Little Island]], [[Coal Drops Yard]]) and civic buildings ([[Seed Cathedral]], [[Learning Hub NTU]], [[Zeitz MOCAA]]). The article confirms dates and details absent from the promotional Divisare sources — notably that the East Beach Café's steel was deliberately rusted then sealed with an oil-based coating (not a self-forming COR-TEN patina), and that [[Little Island]]'s principal funder was the Furstenberg Family Foundation (Diane von Fürstenberg), with Heatherwick Studio selected via design competition in 2012.

The article is also the most critical source on Heatherwick in the wiki, documenting four significant controversies: (1) the structural failure and demolition of B of the Bang (2009); (2) the alleged design originality dispute over the 2012 Olympic cauldron, settled out of court with organisers acknowledging key elements were present in a prior Atopia proposal; (3) the Garden Bridge project collapse (2017), in which Heatherwick was found to have been the sole founding member of the Garden Bridge Trust while repeatedly denying involvement — £46m in public money was lost and the appointments of both Heatherwick Studio and [[Arup]] as engineers were found to have been "not open, fair or competitive"; (4) the [[Vessel]] closures following four suicides in under two years.

## Key Points

- **Person**: Thomas Alexander Heatherwick, born 17 February 1970, London; CBE (2013), RA, RDI, HonFREng
- **Education**: Manchester Polytechnic (3D design); Royal College of Art (furniture design, graduated 1994)
- **Studio**: Heatherwick Studio founded 1994; ~250 staff; King's Cross, London
- **Design philosophy**: refuses to separate design disciplines; problem-solving "like solving a crime by a process of elimination"; rejects "sliced-up ghettos of thought"
- **Steel correction**: East Beach Café steel was allowed to rust then fixed with oil-based coating — NOT a self-protecting COR-TEN patina
- **Little Island funder**: Furstenberg Family Foundation (Diane von Fürstenberg) — confirmed; selected Heatherwick in 2012 after design competition
- **Vessel**: opened March 2019; closed 2021 after four suicides; partially reopened October 2024
- **Garden Bridge**: £46M public money lost; Heatherwick sole founding member of Garden Bridge Trust while denying membership; appointments of Heatherwick Studio + Arup "not open, fair or competitive"
- **Olympic cauldron 2012**: out-of-court settlement acknowledged key elements were in Atopia's prior 2007 proposal; Heatherwick denied copying
- **B of the Bang**: structural failure; council sued for £1.7M; sculpture core sold for scrap 2012
- **Confirmed dates**: East Beach Café 2007 (RIBA National Award 2008); Learning Hub NTU 2015; Little Island opened 21 May 2021; Coal Drops Yard October 2018; Zeitz MOCAA opened 22 September 2017

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- East Beach Café steel: Wikipedia contradicts the "weathering steel / self-protecting patina" framing in existing wiki pages. The steel was "allowed to rust before the resulting colours were fixed with an oil-based coating." This is a controlled process, not COR-TEN's autonomous patination. The Weathering Steel concept page and East Beach Café entity/source pages require correction.
- Olympic cauldron: the out-of-court settlement acknowledges Atopia's elements were present in the cauldron; Heatherwick's categorical denial was not retracted. The historical record contains both.
- Garden Bridge Trust: Heatherwick "repeatedly denied being part of the trust" while being its sole founding member. Documented factual contradiction between his public statements and the record.
- Vessel: no structural criticism of the design itself in the source; the suicides are a product of form (open staircase, no barriers). No post-closure design critique presented.
- Airo Car (IM Motors, 2021): production "set to start 2023" — current status unknown in wiki.

**PL:**
- Stal East Beach Café: Wikipedia przeczy ujęciu „weathering steel / samochroniąca patyna". Stal była celowo skorodowana i zafiksowana powłoką olejową — nie COR-TEN. Strony Weathering Steel i East Beach Café wymagają korekty.
- Kocioł olimpijski: ugoda pozasądowa potwierdza elementy Atopii; kategoryczne zaprzeczenie Heatherwicaka nie zostało cofnięte.
- Członkostwo w Garden Bridge Trust: Heatherwick wielokrotnie zaprzeczał byciu częścią trustu, będąc jego jedynym założycielem. Udokumentowana sprzeczność.
- Vessel: brak krytyki konstrukcji w źródle; samobójstwa są produktem formy (otwarta klatka schodowa, brak barier).
- Airo Car (2021): produkcja planowana na 2023 — aktualny status nieznany.
