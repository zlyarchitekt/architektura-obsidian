## Summary

CapitaSpring is a 51-storey, 93,000 m² mixed-use skyscraper in Singapore's CBD — built on the site of a former public car park and hawker centre. It was designed collaboratively by [[Carlo Ratti Associati]] (CRA) and [[BIG — Bjarke Ingels Group]], and is explicitly marketed as a "biophilic skyscraper": over 80,000 plants are integrated across multiple elevations, sky gardens, and a four-level mid-building open-air "Green Oasis," giving it a Green Plot Ratio of 1:1.4 — meaning 140% of the site area is landscaped.

The project does programmatic double duty: it is a premium Grade A office tower and Citadines serviced residence while simultaneously recreating the historically significant Market Street Hawker Centre (56 food stalls) at floors 2–3 and pedestrianising Market Street at ground level. The 18m-high "City Room" at the base shelters a public open space with separate lobbies for offices, residences, retail, and the food centre. The rooftop hosts Singapore's tallest urban farm, operated by 1-Group, growing over 150 species of fruit, vegetables, and herbs for the rooftop restaurants.

The Green Oasis at the building's core (levels approx. 17–20) is a 35m open-air garden designed on the vertical plant hierarchy of tropical rainforest ecosystems: large shade-tolerant leaves at the "rainforest floor" transitioning to smaller-leaved canopy species at the top.

[[Bjarke Ingels]] and Brian Yang were BIG's partners-in-charge. [[Carlo Ratti]] led CRA's team.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- Source is promotional/press release — no critical review, post-occupancy evaluation, or user research.
- 80,000 plants in an air-conditioned tower: the long-term maintenance burden and ecosystem viability at this scale are not addressed.
- "Green Plot Ratio of 1:1.4" is a Singapore planning metric — how meaningful it is versus actual biodiversity or ecological performance is an open question.
- The hawker centre recreation is presented as cultural preservation, but the original was demolished to build this tower — the framing warrants scrutiny.
- 99%+ of office and retail committed at time of publication suggests commercial success, but the "public benefit" of a private Grade A tower is a legitimate critical question.

**PL:**
- Źródło to materiał promocyjny/prasowy — brak recenzji krytycznej, oceny post-occupancy ani badań użytkowników.
- 80 000 roślin w klimatyzowanym wieżowcu: długoterminowe obciążenie konserwacyjne i wykonalność ekosystemu w tej skali nieomówione.
- „GPR 1:1,4" to singapurska metryka planistyczna — jak znacząca jest w porównaniu z rzeczywistą bioróżnorodnością lub efektywnością ekologiczną — pytanie otwarte.
- Odtworzenie hawker centre przedstawiane jest jako zachowanie kulturowe, ale oryginał wyburzono, by wybudować tę wieżę — narracja wymaga weryfikacji.
- 99%+ biur i handlu zakontraktowanych w chwili publikacji sugeruje sukces komercyjny, ale „korzyść publiczna" prywatnej wieży klasy A to prawomocne pytanie krytyczne.
