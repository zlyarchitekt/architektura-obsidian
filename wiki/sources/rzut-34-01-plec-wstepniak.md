## Summary

The editorial introduction to RZUT-34, a co-production of the RZUT quarterly and [[BAL architektek]] (Dominika Janicka, Barbara Nawrocka, Dominika Wilczyńska). The issue's theme is PŁEĆ — Gender — understood in the broadest intersectional sense: not a "women's issue" or a "feminist number" but an investigation of how privilege and exclusion work inside both the architecture profession and the built space it produces.

The editors coin the term **archidziadocen** ("archi-patriarchal age") as a sardonic label for the current era in Polish architectural culture — a period they expect future generations to recall with disbelief. They frame the issue's ambition as expanding sensitivity to injustice and indifference, not as crafting political programmes. The RIBA example from 2003 is invoked to measure the pace of change: twenty years after the British institute published recommendations for gender equality (including jury representation), the situation in Poland remains largely unresolved — the Izba Architektów RP's own social media post addressing architect Barbara Ziemba in a patronising register is cited as evidence.

A key theoretical move in the editorial is the definition of **intersectionality** as the governing frame: gender inequality in architecture overlaps with class, age, sexual orientation, and identity. Urban planning is identified as a structural mechanism that reproduces gendered divisions of labour — favouring the nuclear family model, pushing care work into the private sphere, and locating it on distant peripheries with poor transit connections. LGBTQ+ communities are similarly marginalised into "isolated islands" in the urban fabric, with symbolic conflicts (the rainbow sculpture) illustrating how public space functions as a site of political contestation.

[[Denise Scott Brown]] is cited as the canonical international example of a woman whose professional contributions were systematically invisible — "Ach! To pan jest TYM architektem! O! Pani też jest architektem?" — despite equal or greater creative input. The domestic parallel is the persistent cult of the single (usually male) name in Polish architecture, under which entire teams labour without attribution. **Purplewashing** — symbolic feminism as marketing strategy, without structural change — is identified as the risk facing any language-only or representation-only reform.

## Key Points

- **RZUT-34 theme: PŁEĆ** — gender as privilege and exclusion, intersectional; not a women's issue in isolation.
- **Archidziadocen** — neologism for the era of architectural patriarchy, expected to be remembered with disbelief.
- **RIBA 2003 precedent**: 20 years of recommendations on jury representation have not resolved the structural problem.
- **Intersectionality**: gender inequality in architecture intersects with class, age, sexual orientation — the same system produces the glass ceiling, the peripheralisation of care work, and the marginalisation of LGBTQ+ spaces.
- **Denise Scott Brown effect**: the systemic invisibility of women's architectural contributions, hidden behind a single male name.
- **Cult of the single name**: Polish architecture's "one (usually male) name, large team" culture as a gender-inequality mechanism.
- **Purplewashing**: risk of language reform (feminatives, street renaming) without structural change — a marketing posture, not equality.
- **Built space as a political arena**: LGBTQ+ spaces reduced to isolated urban islands; rainbow sculpture conflicts as territorial politics.
- **Inclusive design** rooted in intersectional feminism: attentive to the needs of the body, reproductive labour, groups excluded by norms, and non-human nature.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The editorial calls for "extending sensitivity to injustice" without specifying concrete mechanisms — this is the purplewashing it warns against, applied to itself. The tension between aspiration and instrument is not resolved in the wstępniak; the resolution is deferred to the articles.
- The LGBTQ+ urbanism thread (isolated islands) is touched upon but not developed — article 17 from RZUT-33 ([[rzut-33-17-wizja-pierwszego-queer-miastx-w-pl]]) provides the fuller treatment.
