## Summary
An interview with Grzegorz A. Buczek, architect, urbanist, and former mayor, published in Kwartalnik RZUT. The conversation dissects the systemic dysfunction of Poland's spatial planning laws. Buczek highlights the structural imbalance of power between municipal councils and mayors (wójt, burmistrz, prezydent), the frequent political manipulation by voivodes (wojewoda) and heritage conservators (WKZ), and the destructive, illusionary nature of public consultations. Crucially, Buczek argues that the prevailing legal interpretation of the *Warunki Zabudowy* (WZ) decision—that it does not need to comply with the municipal *Studium*—is based on a flawed, "flat" reading of legislation that ignores the administrative primacy of the Act on Municipal Self-Government.

Buczek zwraca uwagę na strukturalną nierównowagę władzy między radą gminy a wójtem/burmistrzem/prezydentem, na częste polityczne manipulacje ze strony wojewodów i konserwatorów zabytków (WKZ) oraz na fasadowy charakter konsultacji publicznych. Przede wszystkim dowodzi, że dominująca interpretacja prawna decyzji WZ – według której nie musi ona być zgodna ze Studium – opiera się na błędnym, „płaskim” czytaniu ustaw, ignorującym nadrzędność Ustawy o samorządzie gminnym.*

## Key Points
- **Ład przestrzenny (Spatial Order)**: It is the primary statutory task of a municipality, originally introduced to the 1990 Act by prof. Jerzy Regulski. True spatial order (*Raumordnung*) means deep systemic harmony, not merely superficial aesthetics, and does not conflict with economic investment.
- **Systemic Imbalance**: Since 2002, municipal executive power (wójt/president) has completely overshadowed the legislative council (rada). The executive controls the planning process, often using the municipal council as a rubber stamp.
- **External Undermining**: The Voivode (Wojewoda) can invalidate planning resolutions, often for political reasons. The Provincial Conservator of Monuments (WKZ) frequently uses bureaucratic obstruction or extra-legal demands to block WZ decisions or effectively force their vision onto local plans.
- **The WZ vs. Studium Paradox**: Buczek argues that a WZ decision *should* legally comply with the municipal *Studium*. Because the Act on Municipal Self-Government compels the mayor to execute the council's resolutions (which includes the *Studium*), issuing a WZ contrary to the *Studium* is a failure of duty. However, Polish jurisprudence ignores this hierarchy.
- **Illusory Participation**: Public consultations are often dragged out over so many years (e.g., in Warsaw) that citizens forget their input, effectively destroying public trust.
- **Urbanists Incapacitated**: Planners in municipal planning offices (MPU) are directly subordinate to the executive, severely limiting their professional independence. The deregulation (Gowin reform) stripped urbanists of their professional chamber.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- There is a profound contradiction between the letter of the Act on Municipal Self-Government (which makes the *Studium* a binding resolution upon the executive) and the established jurisprudence of administrative courts (which treat the *Studium* as non-binding for WZ decisions).
- The paradox of public participation: "there can be less participation even when there is more of it" if the process is drawn out so long that it becomes irrelevant.
