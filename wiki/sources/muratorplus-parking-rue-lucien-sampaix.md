## Summary

Przy rue Lucien Sampaix w 10. dzielnicy Paryża pożar z października 2020 roku trwale uszkodził modernistyczny garaż z 1935 roku. Zamiast rozbiórki miasto skorzystało z prawa pierwokupu, a budynek przekazało w długoterminową dzierżawę agencji mieszkaniowej Paris Habitat. Na zlecenie Paris Habitat pracownia [[UR Architecture Urbanisme]] projektuje tutaj 16 mieszkań komunalnych i lokal handlowy na parterze — realizacja planowana przed końcem 2027 roku.

This project is a model case for the emerging European trend of treating existing structure as an asset rather than a liability. The four design principles adopted from the outset — minimum demolition, energy-performance certification (BBC Effinergie Rénovation + Biosourcé), cross-ventilated flats with winter gardens and continuous balconies, and community-enhancing shared spaces — flow directly from Paris's bioclimatic local plan (*PLU bioclimatique*), which explicitly privileges renovation and energy-efficiency upgrades over new construction. The project illustrates how planning law can structurally redirect architectural practice toward low-carbon outcomes.

The financing model is equally instructive: at 6.7 M€ total (≈ 334 k€/unit), 63% comes from Paris Habitat's own funds and loans, 32% from city grants. The flats will be let through three French social-housing subsidy tiers (PLAI for the most vulnerable, PLUS for low-to-moderate incomes, PLS for lower-middle class) — ensuring a genuinely mixed community within a single 16-unit block.

**PL:** Projekt przy rue Lucien Sampaix pokazuje jak prawo planistyczne może strukturalnie skierować praktykę architektoniczną ku niskoemisyjności. Finansowanie opiera się na modelu mieszanym (Paris Habitat + dotacje miejskie), a mieszkania oferowane będą w trzech systemach wsparcia (PLAI, PLUS, PLS) zapewniających dostępność dla różnych grup najemców.

## Key Points

- 1935 modernist reinforced-concrete garage (431 m² plot, 4 upper floors + partial basement) destroyed by fire in October 2020; instead of demolition, the city exercised its *droit de préemption* and transferred the building to Paris Habitat.
- UR Architecture Urbanisme won the competition; the firm specialises in low-carbon residential design and received the *Palmarès des Jeunes Urbanistes* from the Ministry of Ecological Transition in 2024.
- Street facade: existing concrete structure retained and reinforced; insulated layer set back behind existing plane; beton nettoyé, repaired and painted; rhythm of ribbon windows preserved — "industrial heritage, not a problem."
- Courtyard facade: entirely different character; hemp-wool insulation + lime render; natural timber joinery; galleries and balconies (terracotta tile surfaces, blue lacquered metal balustrades) creating intermediate zones between flat and shared space.
- Roof: partially accessible to residents, partially planted.
- Staircase: illuminated by glass blocks; lift discretely integrated into concrete core.
- Flat mix: studios to 4-room apartments on four floors above a 217 m² commercial ground-floor unit.
- Each flat cross-ventilated; street side has *jardins d'hiver* (winter gardens) as thermal buffers; day rooms and kitchens face the courtyard.
- Total investment: ~ 6.7 M€; financing: 63% Paris Habitat + loans, 32% city grants. Completion planned before end 2027.
