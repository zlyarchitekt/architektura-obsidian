## Summary

The Learning Hub at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore, designed by [[Heatherwick Studio]] with CPG Consultants as lead architect, is a 14,000 sqm, 8-storey educational building organised around a principle of programmatic inversion: rather than the conventional university building (corridors connecting isolated box lecture rooms), it is twelve concrete towers, each a stack of rounded tutorial rooms, arranged around a generous central public atrium. The 56 rooms have no corners, no obvious front or back — they are designed to dissolve the frontal hierarchy of traditional teaching and support collaborative, active learning.

The brief grew from NTU's reading of the digital revolution: if students can access knowledge anywhere, the campus's primary function is no longer the lecture but the encounter. The building is thus a *social machine* — interspersed with open spaces, informal garden terraces, nooks, and balconies to encourage accidental interaction between students and professors across disciplines.

The second major design problem was material: Singapore's building codes and sustainability requirements made concrete necessary. The challenge, as Heatherwick frames it, was "how to make this humble material feel beautiful." The response was an elaborate programme of craft-casting: 700 drawings by illustrator Sara Fanelli three-dimensionally cast into the concrete cores; 61 angled columns with individually developed undulating textures; curved facade panels cast with a unique horizontal relief pattern from ten adjustable silicone moulds. The result is intended to read as if the building had been "handmade from wet clay."

Environmentally, the building's permeable central atrium is naturally ventilated; the tutorial rooms use silent convection cooling rather than air conditioning fans. It received Green Mark Platinum from the Singapore Building and Construction Authority (BCA).

Brief wyrósł z odczytania przez NTU rewolucji cyfrowej: jeśli studenci mają dostęp do wiedzy wszędzie, podstawową funkcją kampusu nie jest już wykład, lecz spotkanie. Efekt ma czytać się jakby budynek był „wykonany ręcznie z mokrej gliny."

## Key Points

- **Location**: Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore
- **Programme**: 56 tutorial rooms, central public atrium, open spaces, garden terraces; 14,000 sqm GFA; 8 storeys, 38.3m
- **Form**: 12 concrete towers (each a stack of rounded rooms) tapering inward at base around central atrium
- **Pedagogical argument**: rooms without corners or fronts/backs — dissolves teacher-front, student-back hierarchy; designed for active learning and incidental encounter
- **Material mandate**: Singapore building codes + sustainability brief = concrete required; design challenge = make concrete beautiful
- **Craft response**: 700 Sara Fanelli illustrations cast 3D into concrete cores; 61 textured columns; 10 silicone moulds for facade panel relief
- **Passive design**: naturally ventilated atrium; silent convection in rooms; Green Mark Platinum (highest BCA rating)
- **Lead architect**: CPG Consultants (executed design); Heatherwick Studio (design architect)
- **Heatherwick quote**: "the most important commodity on a campus is social space to meet and bump into and learn from each other"

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- Source is Heatherwick Studio's own description + NTU/CPG quotes — all promotional. No independent assessment of whether the spatial model actually produces the incidental encounters it promises.
- The claim that "rooms without corners" support collaborative learning is an architectural hypothesis, not an evidenced outcome. No post-occupancy evaluation mentioned.
- Sara Fanelli's 700 illustrations cast into concrete are described as "deliberately ambiguous thought triggers" — aspirational framing; whether users experience them as such or simply stop noticing them is unknown.
- The building is widely referred to as "The Hive" but this name does not appear in the source.
- No date of completion stated in source. (From external knowledge: 2015.)

- Źródło to własny opis Heatherwick Studio + cytaty NTU/CPG — wszystko promocyjne. Brak niezależnej oceny, czy model przestrzenny faktycznie generuje przypadkowe spotkania.
