## Summary

An interview with Magda Wypusz, founder and coordinator of the Mood for Wood workshops, conducted by Marcin Stępień. Mood for Wood is a 12-day educational programme based on learning-by-doing: architecture students go through the entire design-and-build process — budgeting, programming, scheduling, client negotiation, specialist collaboration, transport logistics — to produce a piece of street furniture for a real user and a real site.

The project began in 2015 with a 4,000 PLN grant from the International Visegrad Fund and has grown to editions costing 250,000–300,000 PLN. It operates within the Builder Method Erasmus+ programme (2020–2023), an eight-organisation European network promoting design education through making. Wypusz argues that the hermetic isolation of architecture schools is damaging: students need contact with non-designers (NGOs, institutions, residents) to demystify the profession and learn negotiation. The interview also contains a stark demographic warning about SARP (Polish Architects' Association): with rapidly ageing membership and almost no inter-branch cooperation, the organisation risks collapse within a decade unless young architects join and learn its bureaucratic functions.

Notable built outcomes include: a mobile furniture system for Kolektyw Kąpielisko (Poznań) by BudCud; Bujaki (rocking benches) at Brama Poznania by Mikołaj Smoleński; an outdoor gallery and workshop space for the Amici therapeutic youth shelter by Studio Nomad (Hungary); and Point of Blue — a blue platform over the Olza river in Cieszyn by Karol Żurawski.

## Key Points

- Mood for Wood is a 12-day design-build workshop where students experience the full professional cycle: brief → design → budget → build → handover.
- Founded 2015; evolved from 7 days / 4,000 PLN to 12 days / ~300,000 PLN per edition.
- International and interdisciplinary: students from multiple universities and countries; participants include non-architects (NGOs, local institutions) as co-designers.
- The programme is part of Builder Method (Erasmus+, 2020–2023), an 8-organisation European network developing a handbook and website for experiential design education.
- Entry fees are kept low (150–200 EUR) to avoid financial exclusion; full per-student cost is 2,800–3,500 PLN.
- Tutors are carefully vetted; the organisation avoids interfering in tutor-student dynamics during design days.
- SARP demographic crisis: average age rising fast; 25 regional branches rarely cooperate; statutory functions (competition juries, dispute resolution, planning consultations) may have no successors in 10 years.
- Parallel free workshop: KOTydż (kotydz.com), fully cost-free.

- Założone 2015; ewoluowało z 7 dni / 4 tys. zł do 12 dni / ~300 tys. zł na edycję.
- Międzynarodowe i interdyscyplinarne: studenci z wielu uczelni i krajów; uczestnicy obejmują osoby spoza architektury (NGO, instytucje lokalne) jako współprojektantów.
- Program jest częścią Builder Method (Erasmus+, 2020–2023), sieci ośmiu organizacji europejskich opracowujących podręcznik i stronę internetową dla doświadczalnej edukacji projektowej.
- Opłaty wpisowe są niskie (150–200 EUR), by uniknąć wykluczenia finansowego; pełny koszt na studenta to 2800–3500 zł.
- Tutorzy są starannie weryfikowani; organizacja unika ingerencji w dynamikę tutor–studenci podczas dni projektowych.
- Kryzys demograficzny SARP: szybko rosnąca średnia wieku; 25 oddziałów regionalnych rzadko współpracuje; funkcje statutowe mogą nie mieć następców za 10 lat.
- Równoległy darmowy warsztat: KOTydż (kotydz.com).

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- Wypusz’s claim that architecture schools are "hermetically isolated" and that non-designers should be involved from the first year is radical; it challenges the studio-centric model of most European faculties but is not empirically defended beyond anecdote.
- The SARP crisis warning is based on personal observation; no membership or retirement data are cited.
- The comparison to Hello Wood (Hungary), which charges 450–650 EUR without transport, raises questions about financial sustainability of the low-fee Polish model.
- Several tutors and project partners (BudCud, Studio Nomad, Hello Wood) are mentioned but not given entity pages in this ingest due to limited textual depth.

- Teza Wypusz o „hermetycznej izolacji" szkół architektonicznych i konieczności angażowania osób spoza branży od pierwszego roku jest radykalna; kwestionuje studialno-centrowy model większości europejskich wydziałów, ale nie jest empirycznie obroniona poza anegdotą.
