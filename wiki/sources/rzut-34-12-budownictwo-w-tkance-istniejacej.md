## Summary

Interview by Katarzyna Dolecińska with Annabelle von Reutern (Business Development, Concular GmbH) and Diana Anastasija Radke (Managing Partner, KVL Bauconsult GmbH) on building in existing fabric, circular construction, and the gender dynamics of architectural practice. The conversation covers the material-waste crisis (only 1% of built materials are reused), the Concular digital marketplace for reclaimed building components, the founding of the Berlin-based Association for Building in Existing Fabric (Verband für Bauen im Bestand), and the interviewees' personal reflections on women, ego, monumentality, and care in architecture.

**PL:** Wywiad Katarzyny Dolecińskiej z Annabelle von Reutern (Business Development, Concular GmbH) i Dianą Anastasiją Radke (Partnerka Zarządzająca, KVL Bauconsult GmbH) o budownictwie w istniejącej tkance, konstrukcji cyrkularnej i dynamice płciowej w praktyce architektonicznej. Rozmowa obejmuje kryzys odpadów materialnych (tylko 1% wbudowanych materiałów jest ponownie wykorzystywanych), cyfrowe rynku Conculary dla odzyskanych komponentów budowlanych, założenie berlińskiego Stowarzyszenia na rzecz budownictwa w tkance istniejącej oraz osobiste refleksje rozmówczyń na temat kobiet, ego, monumentalności i troski w architekturze.

## Key Points

- **Material crisis**: According to the German Federal Statistical Office, 534 million tons of mineral building materials were consumed in Germany in 2013. Buildings and infrastructure (28 billion tons as of 2010) represent a significant anthropogenic raw-material warehouse. Yet only 1% of all built-in materials are reused; most end up as waste after demolition.
- **Concular**: A start-up that developed a portal connecting supply and demand for existing built materials. Components from demolished buildings are entered into an online database; registered users find them in inventory or receive alerts. Everything dismantled from an existing building can be delivered directly to a new construction site, saving storage and transport costs. The app benefits developers and manufacturing firms seeking cheaper raw materials.
- **Circular house pilot**: Concular is coordinating a pilot project on the site of a former brewery in Berlin-Neukölln. A building constructed mainly from secondary materials will offer affordable housing, event spaces, and offices — including Concular's future headquarters.
- **KVL Group**: Specialises in ecological projects. As a company they produce 66 tons of CO₂ annually — equivalent to 2,600 m² of reinforced-concrete ceiling. They shifted focus from office micro-operations to improving efficiency in client projects, leading them to building in existing fabric: "the concrete ceilings have already been poured, keeping them limits energy consumption and minimises CO₂ emission."
- **Verband für Bauen im Bestand**: Founded in Berlin in February 2023 by von Reutern, Radke, and other recycling-architecture enthusiasts. The board consists exclusively of women, though the membership is mixed. Radke notes that almost as many comments were about the all-female board as about the association's purpose — which irritated her.
- **Why women?**: Von Reutern: "We were invited! Certainly because we were already doing this and were active on this front." Radke speculates: "Maybe the answer is trivially simple: because women can have children. They can leave something forever. Men have to create monuments for themselves — through new buildings, literature, films, songs, artworks. And we don't need that." Von Reutern: "We have reproduction in us." Radke: "And we can tend to other things. Or maybe we lack ego? Maybe it's upbringing?"
- **Care work and architecture**: Von Reutern notes women are discouraged from stepping into the spotlight and boasting. When she speaks with certainty that her proposal is better, people think "bitch"; when a man does it, they say "genius". Radke: "Building in existing fabric is also care work." Von Reutern connects this to the German Pavilion at the Venice Architecture Biennale 2023 — "Open for Conservation" (*Offen für Konservierung*), showing maintenance work including cleaning, preparation, repair. The term *Instandbesetzung* (occupation for preservation) combines renovation and squatting.
- **City as body**: Radke: "For me the city is a body, and buildings are soft tissues, even though architecture is associated with something hard and rocky. Soft tissues allow the city to function." The association is not against new construction; it wants to enable disinterested, intelligent approaches to already-realised reality.
- **Demolition as arrogance**: Von Reutern: "When I see demolished buildings, I strongly feel that people don't learn to appreciate what they have. ... It's very arrogant to just wave your hand and say 'I have a better idea'." Radke: "The same people worked on these objects as us, just 10–20 years earlier. Demolition is a kind of arrogance toward the past. Every time we destroy a building from the 1950s, 60s, 70s, 80s, 90s, we tear a page out of a history textbook."
- **Berlin examples**: The reconstructed Berlin City Palace vs. the demolished Palace of the Republic. Von Reutern was in a think tank for Schinkel's Bauakademie where people pushed for palace reconstruction using the argument "a city's history cannot be invented". She agrees — which is why she doesn't understand why the palace was rebuilt while the Palace of the Republic was demolished. "The palace was still there, and the castle was already gone. That was history."
- **Zaha Hadid**: In the 1980s, Hadid co-founded a feminist organisation of women planners and architects that created "female projects" oriented toward sustainability, energy efficiency, and emphasis on elderly and disabled people. "40 years have passed and it's astonishing to me that women simply haven't been heard." Radke: "I don't find it astonishing at all."
- **Female architecture**: Von Reutern does not believe in "female architecture". "There is perhaps architecture made by women. We all have a female and male part. The difference is that as women we are more familiar with this vast thematic area. That is, we know what it's like to ride with a pram, with a wheelchair, to care for the elderly. When you know that, your perspective changes."
- **Sustainability and gender**: Von Reutern: "Sustainable development is the statement that we again feel whole as a collective. The goal is to protect our planet. And half the world's population has not yet been asked how they want to fight for it. The world looks the way it does because it is based on a system of exploitation. If the other half of the world's population is now allowed to play its role in key positions, the world will change. This is the only chance to reverse this situation."

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- Radke is irritated that the all-female board received as much commentary as the association's purpose, yet both interviewees also advance essentialist claims about women (reproduction vs. monuments, care work, lack of ego). The tension between strategic essentialism and gender-deconstruction is unresolved.
- The argument that "demolition is arrogance toward the past" is powerful but may conflict with the need to remove structurally unsound, energy-inefficient, or politically compromised buildings (e.g. the Palace of the Republic contained asbestos). The ethical calculus of what deserves preservation vs. removal needs a framework beyond age alone.
- Von Reutern's claim that "half the world's population has not yet been asked" frames sustainability as gender justice, but the interview does not offer concrete mechanisms for how women's leadership in existing-fabric construction would differ from men's beyond a general ethic of care.
