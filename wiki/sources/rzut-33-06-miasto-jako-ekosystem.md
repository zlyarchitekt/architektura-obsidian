## Summary

A short interview with [[Monika Wróbel]], architect and urbanist from the Fundacja Skwer Sportów Miejskich, by Igor Łysiuk. Unlike the BAiPP-authored articles in RZUT-33 Part 1, this article provides an external practitioner's perspective on the new Warsaw Studium.

Wróbel identifies two highlights: (1) the ecosystem framing — the holistic, cross-sectoral vision that connects the spheres of planning into a coherent whole rather than treating them in isolation; and (2) the locality agenda — local centres as *hearts of their communities*, delivering fair access to services and more even distribution of workplaces. She endorses the "wszędzie blisko" (everywhere close) ideal as the most publicly resonant element, likely to attract both residents and investors.

The article contributes external validation: Wróbel draws on Fundacja Skwer Sportów Miejskich project experience to confirm that residents' demand for greenery has grown year-on-year — corroborating the Studium's green-first approach from ground-level observation rather than planning theory.

## Key Points

- **External validation from practice**: Wróbel draws on Fundacja Skwer Sportów Miejskich project experience to confirm that residents' demand for greenery has grown year-on-year — corroborating the Studium's green-first approach from practice, not planning theory.
- **Ecosystem framing as governance mechanism**: the "city as ecosystem" concept is valued not only philosophically but as a governance tool — it creates a systemic view where "individual sectors see each other and work toward a common goal."
- **Local centres as social equity**: the network of local and district centres is viewed primarily as a fairness instrument — ensuring fair access to services and more even distribution of workplaces across Warsaw's districts.
- **"Wszędzie blisko" (everywhere close)**: Wróbel identifies this as the most publicly resonant element of the Studium vision — most likely to convince district representatives and investors.
- **Car restriction as the main axis of controversy**: predicted to be the most politically contested element of Studium reception, given residents' entrenched habits — an important forecast for the document's political trajectory.
- **Nature as equal structural element**: the recognition of natural capital as an equal component of Warsaw's spatial structure (not supplementary decoration) is identified as a qualitative shift in planning culture.
