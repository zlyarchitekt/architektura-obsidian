## Summary
A dialogue published in Kwartalnik RZUT between two Polish architectural collectives — BAL architektek (Dominika Janicka, Dominika Wilczyńska, Barbara Nawrocka) and Architektoniczki (Ewelina Jaskulska, Joanna Aleksandrowicz, Honorata Grzesikowska). The conversation ranges from the existence (or myth) of "female" design, through the rediscovery of Iwona Buczkowska's timber estate Le Blanc-Mesnil, to the tactical choice between the stigmatised word "feminism" and the pragmatically safer "projektowanie równoprawne" (equitable design). It documents structural barriers: emotional procrastination driven by socially conditioned modesty, the care-work burden that eclipses networking time, and outright sexism in the profession (e.g. an examination-board member stating that rising female licence numbers depress architects' fees). The fuller RZUT-34 version adds extensive material on motherhood as a turning point for office dynamics, Dominika Janicka's multi-city online collaboration model, McKinsey diversity data, and the struggle to make women architects visible.

## Key Points
- **Iwona Buczkowska**: Polish-born architect in France; designed Le Blanc-Mesnil (Pièce Pointue) in the 1980s — the largest timber housing estate in Europe. 300+ non-repetitive triangular apartments, built as prototypes (first 8 houses tested before full rollout). Design proceeded from interior scale upward to urban form, the reverse of standard academic training. Community spaces, small bridges and nooks encourage neighbourly encounter. Threatened with demolition; saved by a petition signed by Jean Nouvel and Renzo Piano.
- **The "F" Word vs. Equitable Design**: In business contexts the word "feminism" immediately kills negotiation, so Architektoniczki use "projektowanie równoprawne" (equitable design) as a tactical entry point, introducing feminist goals gradually. BAL insists on using the word publicly to normalise it, acknowledging intersectional feminism (LGBTQ+, ecofeminism, race, class).
- **Football-centrism (Futbolocentryzm)**: Centralised football pitches in schoolyards spatially enforce patriarchal hierarchies, marginalising girls and less physically aggressive boys. Architektoniczki's "equitable schools" programme rejects this model.
- **Emotional Procrastination**: Research-cited phenomenon (PMS, pregnancy, post-partum depression, perimenopause as biological factors; unequal power, overwork, sexism, mobbing as socio-psychological factors) causes women to delay self-promotion because they feel "not ready yet," perpetuating invisibility.
- **Economic penalty of feminisation**: An examination-board member openly lamented that rising numbers of women passing the architectural licence exam would drive fees down — a frank statement of how the profession's prestige is tied to male exclusivity.
- **Motherhood as structural turning point**: BAL had to invent a new collaboration model when Dominika Wilczyńska became a mother; the group explicitly learned that a colleague could no longer devote 100% attention. Dominika Janicka lives in a different city and collaborates entirely online.
- **Diversity data**: McKinsey (2020) and Glasgow/Leicester studies are cited: firms in the top gender-diversity quartile are 25% more likely to outperform on profitability; firms with >30% women in management outperform male-dominated boards.
- **The Power of 10+**: Invoked as a metric for equitable public space — at least ten reasons to be there, ensuring flexibility and social diversity.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- Tactical tension: using the word "feminism" maintains ideological clarity but closes doors with conservative clients and private investors; using "equitable design" opens doors but risks diluting the structural critique.
- If Buczkowska's interior-to-macro method is genuinely different from standard training, why is it not taught as a valid alternative in schools?
- The McKinsey/league-table data shows correlation, not causation — does gender-diverse leadership genuinely produce better spatial design, or merely better business outcomes?

> (Business will never risk being a social innovator. It rather responds to trends that it has thoroughly researched beforehand. This proves that feminist urbanism and the word 'feminist' are still unpopular and niche.)

> "Kto dzisiaj by zbudował prototyp? To było zupełnie inne myślenie. Zastanawianie się, jak ta przestrzeń będzie funkcjonować i działać, a nie określanie PUM-u i jechanie z żelbetem."
> (Who would build a prototype today? That was completely different thinking. Wondering how this space would function and work, not defining the building programme and pouring concrete.)

> "To bardzo smutne, że tyle kobiet zdało uprawnienia, bo od tego stawki w architekturze maleją i mężczyźni muszą uciekać z zawodu."
> (It is very sad that so many women passed the licence exam, because that drives architectural fees down and men have to flee the profession.) — examination-board member cited by Architektoniczki.

> "Mamy wiele grup marginalizowanych, np. społeczność LGBT+, mamy ekofeminizm, a sytuacja białych bogatych kobiet jest zupełnie inna niż czarnych imigrantek."
> (We have many marginalised groups, e.g. the LGBTQ+ community, we have ecofeminism, and the situation of white wealthy women is completely different from that of black immigrants.) — BAL on intersectional feminism.
