## Summary
A dialogue published in Kwartalnik RZUT between two Polish architectural collectives: BAL architektek and Architektoniczki. The conversation explores the state of women in the architectural profession, "female" design perspectives, and the structural difficulties related to gender (such as emotional procrastination and care-work burdens affecting networking). They discuss practical implementations of feminist spatial planning, like moving away from "football-centrism" in schools, and analyze the historical precedent set by Iwona Buczkowska's timber housing estate in France.

**PL:** *Opublikowany w Kwartalniku RZUT dialog między dwoma polskimi kolektywami architektonicznymi: BAL architektek i Architektoniczki. Rozmowa dotyczy sytuacji kobiet w zawodzie architekta, „kobiecych” perspektyw projektowych oraz strukturalnych trudności związanych z płcią (takich jak prokrastynacja emocjonalna i obciążenie pracą opiekuńczą wpływającą na networking). Architektki dyskutują o praktycznym wdrażaniu feministycznego planowania przestrzennego, np. odejściu od „futbolocentryzmu” w szkołach, oraz analizują historyczny precedens osiedla drewnianego Iwony Buczkowskiej we Francji.*

## Key Points
- **Iwona Buczkowska**: A Polish-born architect who designed Le Blanc-Mesnil (Pièce Pointue) near Paris in the 1980s. It was the largest timber housing estate in Europe, pioneering ecological, pro-social, non-standard (triangular geometries) design, starting from the interior scale rather than top-down masterplanning.
- **The "F" Word vs. Equitable Design**: Due to public and business hostility toward the word "feminism", gender mainstreaming advocates often use the term "projektowanie równoprawne" (equitable design) or "universal design" when pitching to investors or local governments. 
- **Football-centrism (Futbolocentryzm)**: Placing a football pitch at the physical and symbolic center of a schoolyard naturally marginalizes children who do not fit the aggressive, physically dominant stereotype (including many girls and non-sporty boys), enforcing patriarchal spatial hierarchies. 
- **Emotional Procrastination**: Women in the profession often suffer from emotional procrastination (delaying publication/promotion due to a socially conditioned feeling of "not being good enough yet") compounded by a lack of time for networking caused by unequal caregiving responsibilities.
- **The Power of 10+**: The concept that successful public spaces must offer users at least 10 different reasons to be there, ensuring diversity and flexibility.
