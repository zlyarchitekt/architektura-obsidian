## Summary

This Wikipedia article provides a concise encyclopaedic overview of Critical Regionalism as an architectural theory, covering its two founding texts (Tzonis & Lefaivre 1981 and Frampton 1983), their respective theoretical emphases, and the major practitioners associated with the concept. It is less a synthesis than a well-structured introduction: short, citation-heavy, and broadly accurate, though necessarily compressed.

Krótki, oparty na cytatach, ogólnie trafny — choć siłą rzeczy skompresowany.*

The article establishes a key distinction rarely noted in the wiki so far: the term "critical regionalism" had two near-simultaneous origins with different emphases. Tzonis and Lefaivre (1981) introduced it first; Frampton (1983) gave it different meaning — one that subsequently dominated the discourse. Tzonis/Lefaivre's version stresses making visible the disruption and loss of place through estrangement: elements stripped of context and set in unfamiliar combinations. Frampton's version stresses topography, climate, light, and *tectonic form* as the instruments through which global modernity can be inflected by local particularity.

**PL:** *Artykuł ustanawia kluczowe rozróżnienie: termin „regionalizm krytyczny\" miał dwa niemal jednoczesne źródła o różnych akcentach. Tzonis i Lefaivre (1981) wprowadzili go jako uwidocznienie utraty miejsca przez wyobcowanie. Frampton (1983) nadał mu inne znaczenie — które zdominowało późniejszy dyskurs — kładąc nacisk na topografię, klimat, światło i formę tektoniczną.*

A secondary contribution is the Salingaros critique: despite being sympathetic to Critical Regionalism's goals, Salingaros argues that in practice it "willfully perpetuates the form languages of Modernism" rather than genuinely protecting regional traditions. This is the strongest internal critique of the concept in the wiki.

**PL:** *Dodatkowym wkładem jest krytyka Salingarosa: pomimo sympatii dla celów regionalizmu krytycznego, twierdzi on, że w praktyce „celowo powiela języki formalne modernizmu\" zamiast autentycznie chronić tradycje regionalne.*

## Key Points

- Term coined simultaneously (different meaning) by Tzonis/Lefaivre (1981, *Architecture in Greece*) and Frampton (1983, *The Anti-Aesthetic* anthology).
- Frampton's source: Paul Ricoeur's "how to become modern and to return to sources" + Heidegerrean phenomenology.
- Frampton's six points: emphasis on **topography, climate, light; tectonic form over scenography; sense of touch over visual sense**.
- Two founding examples in Frampton: Utzon's **Bagsværd Church** (1976) — universal concrete shell + locally inflected interior vaulting; Aalto's **Säynätsalo Town Hall** (1952) — tactile brick surface resisting universal technology.
- Tzonis/Lefaivre emphasise **de-familiarisation** (elements stripped of context) rather than rooting; theirs is a more deconstructive reading.
- Broad list of practitioners (Frampton sense): Siza, Ando, Peter Zumthor, Carlo Scarpa, Sverre Fehn, Glenn Murcutt, Tadao Ando, Wang Shu, Juhani Pallasmaa, WOHA (Singapore), among many others.
- Salingaros critique: CR in practice perpetuates Modernism's form languages; "true regionalism must free itself from any global form language imposed from above."
- Cultural studies extension: Spivak & Butler use "critical regionalism" for a deconstructive alternative to nationalism (beyond architecture).
- Minnette de Silva (Sri Lanka, 1950s) cited as independent precursor who called the same approach "Regional Modernism."

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- The article does not fully resolve the Tzonis/Lefaivre vs. Frampton distinction — the two founders had genuinely different ideas and the term is used loosely in most subsequent literature.
- Salingaros's critique deserves more weight: if Critical Regionalism in practice produces work that still looks like abstracted Modernism, what is the actual difference from 'sensitive modernism'? This is an open question for the wiki.
- The long list of practitioners (Juhani Pallasmaa, Wang Shu, Peter Zumthor, Tadao Ando, Glenn Murcutt) spans wildly different cultural contexts and formal vocabularies — are they really all doing the same thing?

## Quotes

> "Critical regionalism should adopt modern architecture, critically, for its universal progressive qualities but at the same time value should be placed on the geographical context of the building. Emphasis should be on topography, climate, light; on tectonic form rather than on scenography." — Kenneth Frampton, 1983

> "In practice, critical regionalism willfully perpetuates the form languages of Modernism. True regionalism has to free itself from any global form language imposed from above." — Nikos Salingaros
