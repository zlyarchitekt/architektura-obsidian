## Summary

The Bund Finance Centre is a 420,000 m² mixed-use development on Shanghai's famous waterfront, jointly designed by [[Foster + Partners]] and [[Heatherwick Studio]]. Eight buildings combine premium offices, a boutique hotel, a cultural centre, and luxury retail, arranged around a landscaped public plaza. Two 180m landmark towers anchor the southern end of the site; the waterfront-facing buildings are staggered in height to relate in scale and rhythm to the 19th-century neoclassical landmarks along the Bund.

The masterplan is conceived as a "point of connection" between Shanghai's historic old town and the new financial district of Pudong across the river — highly permeable for pedestrians, with the buildings defining the "end point" of the Bund as a promenade.

The most architecturally distinctive element is the cultural centre at the heart of the scheme: an exhibition, events, and performance venue whose exterior is a **kinetic moving veil** — a facade element that shifts to reveal or conceal the stage on the building's balcony, adapting the building's public face to its changing programme. The cultural centre is inspired by the open stages of traditional Chinese theatres. Materiality across the development is defined by hand-crafted granite and bronze details — edges of each volume taper as they rise, creating an impression of solidity at the base transitioning to transparency at the top.

## Key Points

- 420,000 m²; 8 buildings; Shanghai Bund waterfront.
- Co-designed by [[Foster + Partners]] and [[Heatherwick Studio]] — two major global firms collaborating.
- Two 180m towers in the south; waterfront buildings staggered to match 19th-century Bund scale and rhythm.
- Masterplan as urban connector: old town ↔ Pudong financial district; pedestrian permeability central.
- Cultural centre with **kinetic moving veil**: facade shifts to reveal/conceal stage; inspired by traditional Chinese open-stage theatres.
- Material palette: hand-crafted granite (edges taper as they rise), bronze details — "jewel-like quality."
- Public landscaped plaza at the heart of the scheme.
- Program: premium offices, boutique hotel, cultural centre, luxury retail (boutiques, concept stores, luxury mall, restaurants).

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- Source text is largely from Foster + Partners' own project page — promotional, no critical voice.
- The "cultural centre" in a luxury commercial development raises questions about who it actually serves and how independent it is from the developer's commercial interests.
- "Inspired by traditional Chinese open theatres" is a framing by Western architects about Chinese cultural heritage — the authenticity and depth of the engagement is unverifiable from this source alone.
- No information on the actual kinetic mechanism, maintenance, or performance of the moving veil.
- The staggering of waterfront buildings to "relate in scale and rhythm" to the historic Bund is a contextual design claim — how well it succeeds is a matter of architectural criticism not addressed by this source.
