## Summary
An essay published in Kwartalnik RZUT analyzing the destructive conflict between post-war modernist architecture and contemporary building regulations in Poland. Because 1960s/70s buildings are rarely listed as protected monuments (wpis do rejestru zabytków), they are forced to fully comply with modern fire safety, thermal insulation, and accessibility standards. Using three examples from Lublin, the authors demonstrate how this rigid legal compliance often ruins the delicate tectonic proportions and spatial philosophies of these structures.

**PL:** *Esej opublikowany w Kwartalniku RZUT, analizujący destrukcyjny konflikt między powojenną architekturą modernistyczną a współczesnymi przepisami budowlanymi w Polsce. Ponieważ budynki z lat 60. i 70. rzadko widnieją w rejestrze zabytków, zmusza się je do pełnej zgodności z dzisiejszymi normami przeciwpożarowymi, termicznymi i BHP. Na trzech przykładach z Lublina autorzy pokazują, jak sztywne trzymanie się przepisów niszczy delikatne proporcje tektoniczne i wizje przestrzenne tych obiektów.*

## Key Points
- **The Legal Trap**: Contemporary building law allows defining technical "deviations" (odstępstwa od przepisów techniczno-budowlanych) almost exclusively for buildings formally listed in the heritage register. Without this protection, safety codes are absolute.
- **PDT Lublin**: The original fire-safety concept relied on "non-flammable materials" (glass blocks / luksfery) forming a unique structural grid. Today, this was torn down and replaced with standard EI 30 fire windows for smoke extraction, destroying the staircase's spatial character.
- **Biblioteka UMCS**: An unprotected architectural gem subjected to standard thermomodernization. Standard thick insulation threatens a "swollen facade" effect (opuchlizna), ruining the window-to-wall relief. It took intervention from activists to maintain the correct proportions and original plaster textures.
- **Osiedle Słowackiego**: A flagship realization of Open Form. Upgrading the facade required using insulation of varying thermal conductivity (λ) to preserve the structural relief. The "Teatr Formy Otwartej" amphitheater is uniquely threatened because its non-standard stairs and lack of railings violate modern safety codes, yet adding them would destroy its sculptural logic.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- There is a profound paradox in current regulations: the original modernist use of non-flammable glass blocks (which aimed at safety under old laws) becomes illegal under new laws enforcing specific "fire-rated windows." The pursuit of absolute safety destroys the cultural asset.
